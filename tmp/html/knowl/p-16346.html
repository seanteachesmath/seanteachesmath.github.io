The field lines obey

\begin{align*} &\frac{\dee{x}}{x}=\frac{\dee{y}}{3y}\qquad\text{ if } x,y\ne 0\\ &\implies 3\ln |x|=\ln |y|+C\\ &\implies |x|^3=e^C|y|\\ &\implies y=\pm e^{-C}x^3\\ &\implies y=C'x^3 \end{align*}

with \(C'\) a nonzero constant. \(x=0\) and \(y=0\) are also field lines, since on the \(y\)-axis \(\vF\parallel\hj\) and on the \(x\)-axis \(\vF\parallel\hi\text{.}\)

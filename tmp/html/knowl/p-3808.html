A Taylor polynomial gives a polynomial approximation for a function \(s(t)\text{.}\) Since \(s(t)\) is itself a polynomial, any \(n\)th-degree Taylor polynomial, with \(n\) greater than or equal to the degree of \(s(t)\text{,}\) will simply give \(s(t)\text{.}\) So, in our case, \(T_{100}(t)=s(t)=4.9t^2-t+10\text{.}\)

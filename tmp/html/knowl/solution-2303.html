Solution 15.2.6.28.1.

The boundary of the solid \(V\) enclosed by \(S\) and \(z= \pm 1\) consists of three pieces: \(S\text{,}\) the top disk

\begin{equation*} S_1=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2 + y^2 \le 2,\ z= 1} \end{equation*}

and the bottom disk

\begin{equation*} S_2=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2 + y^2 \le 2,\ z= -1} \end{equation*}

On \(S_1\text{,}\) \(\hn=\hk\) and

\begin{equation*} \vF\cdot\hn=\vF\cdot\hk=xy-z-z^2\Big|_{z=1}= xy-2 \end{equation*}

so that, denoting \(D=\Set{(x,y)}{x^2+y^2 \le 2}\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_{S_1} \vF \cdot \hn\, \dee{S} = \dblInt_D (xy -2) \,\dee{x}\dee{y} =-2\,\text{Area}(D)= - 4 \pi \end{equation*}

Here we have used that the integral \(\dblInt_D xy \,\dee{x}\dee{y}=0\) because \(xy\) is odd under \(x\rightarrow -x\text{.}\) On \(S_2\text{,}\) \(\hn=-\hk\) and

\begin{equation*} \vF\cdot\hn=-\vF\cdot\hk= -(xy-z-z^2)\Big|_{z=-1}=-xy \end{equation*}

so that

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_{S_2} \vF \cdot \hn\, \dee{S} = \dblInt_D (- xy)\, \dee{x}\dee{y} = 0 \end{equation*}

By the divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.2),

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot \hn \dee{S} &= \dblInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\, \dee{V} - \dblInt_{S_1} \vF \cdot \hn\, \dee{S} - \dblInt_{S_2} \vF \cdot \hn\, \dee{S} \\ &= 0 -(- 4 \pi) - 0 = 4\pi \end{align*}

since

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &= \pdiff{}{x}(x+e^{yz}) +\pdiff{}{y}\big(2yz+\sin(xz)\big) +\pdiff{}{z}(xy-z-z^2)\\ &= 1 + 2z -1 -2z\\ &=0 \end{align*}

Say whether the following statements are true or false.

  1. If \(\vF\) is a 3D vector field defined on all of \(\bbbr^3\) , and \(S_1\) and \(S_2\) are two surfaces with the same boundary, but \(\dblInt_{S_1} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} \ne \dblInt_{S_2} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\text{,}\) then \(\vnabla\cdot\vF\) is not zero anywhere.
  2. If \(\vF\) is a vector field satisfying \(\vnabla\times\vF\) = 0 whose domain is not simply-connected, then \(\vF\) is not conservative.
  3. The osculating circle of a curve \(C\) at a point has the same unit tangent vector, unit normal vector, and curvature as \(C\) at that point.
  4. A planet orbiting a sun has period proportional to the cube of the major axis of the orbit.
  5. For any 3D vector field \(\vF\text{,}\) \(\vnabla\cdot(\vnabla\times\vF)\) = 0.
  6. A field whose divergence is zero everywhere in its domain has closed surfaces \(S\) in its domain.
  7. The gravitational force field is conservative.
  8. If \(\vF\) is a field defined on all of \(\bbbr^3\) such that \(\int_C \vF \cdot \dee{\vr} = 3\) for some curve \(C\text{,}\) then \(\vnabla\times\vF\) is non-zero at some point.
  9. The normal component of acceleration for a curve of constant curvature is constant.
  10. The curve defined by
    \begin{equation*} \vr_1(t) = \cos(t^4)\,\hi + 3t^4\hj,\qquad -\infty \lt t \lt \infty, \end{equation*}
    is the same as the curve defined by
    \begin{equation*} \vr_2(t) = \cos t\,\hi + 3t\,\hk,\qquad -\infty \lt t \lt \infty \end{equation*}

In parts (2), (3) and (4) we will choose \(\vF\) to be the vector field

\begin{equation*} \vG(x,y) = -\frac{y}{x^2+y^2}\hi + \frac{x}{x^2+y^2}\hj \end{equation*}

We saw, in Example 13.3.14, that \(\vnabla\times\vG=\vZero\) except at the origin where it is not defined. We also saw, in Example 15.3.8, that \(\oint_{C}\vG\cdot\dee{\vr}=2\pi\) for any counterclockwise oriented circle centred on the origin.

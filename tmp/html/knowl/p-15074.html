The body of a snowman is formed by the snowballs \(x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 12\) (this is its body) and \(x^2 + y^2 + (z - 4)^2 = 4\) (this is its head).

  1. Find the volume of the snowman by subtracting the intersection of the two snow balls from the sum of the volumes of the snow balls. [Recall that the volume of a sphere of radius \(r\) is \(\frac{4\pi}{3} r^3\text{.}\)]

  2. We can also calculate the volume of the snowman as a sum of the following triple integrals:

    1. \begin{equation*} \int_0^{\frac{2\pi}{3}} \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^2 \rho^2\sin{\varphi} \ \dee{\rho}\,\dee{\theta}\,\dee{\varphi} \end{equation*}
    2. \begin{equation*} \int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^{\sqrt{3}} \int_{\sqrt{3}\,r}^{4-\frac{r}{\sqrt{3}}} r\ \dee{z}\,\dee{r}\,\dee{\theta} \end{equation*}

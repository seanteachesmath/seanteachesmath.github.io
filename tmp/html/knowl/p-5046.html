As \(x\rightarrow 0+\text{,}\) the numerator \(\log x\rightarrow-\infty\text{.}\) That is, the numerator is becoming an increasingly huge, negative number. As \(x\rightarrow 0+\text{,}\) the denominator \(x\rightarrow 0+\text{,}\) which only serves to make the total fraction even larger, and still negative. So, \(\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow 0^+}\dfrac{\log x}{x} =-\infty\text{.}\)

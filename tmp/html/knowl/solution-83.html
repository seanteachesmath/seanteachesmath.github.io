Solution 2.5.2.5.1.

Write \(X=-x\text{.}\) As \(x\) becomes more and more negative, \(X\) becomes more and more positive. From QuestionÂ 2.5.2.4, we know that \(2^X\) grows without bound as \(X\) gets larger and larger. Since \(2^x= 2^{-(-x)}= 2^{-X} =\frac{1}{2^X}\text{,}\) as we let \(x\) become a huge negative number, we are in effect dividing by a huge positive number; hence \(\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow-\infty} 2^x = 0\text{.}\)

A more formulaic way to describe the above is this: \(\lim\limits_{x\rightarrow-\infty} 2^x = \lim\limits_{X\rightarrow\infty} 2^{-X} = \lim\limits_{X\rightarrow\infty} \frac{1}{2^X} =0\text{.}\)

Let

\begin{equation*} V=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2\le 1-z^4,\ 0\le z\le 1} \end{equation*}

Then the boundary, \(\partial V\text{,}\) of \(V\text{,}\) with the orientation that is used in the divergence theorem, consists of two parts

  • the surface \(S\text{,}\) but with the upward pointing normal, and
  • the disk \(D=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2\le 1,\ z=0}\text{,}\) with normal \(-\hk\text{.}\)

So the divergence theorem gives

\begin{gather*} \tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} =\dblInt_{\partial V}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =- \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} +\dblInt_D\vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S} \end{gather*}

As \(\vnabla\cdot\vF=0\) and \(\vF(x,y,0) = \big( 1 \,,\, 1 \,,\,1\big)\)

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_D\vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S} =-\dblInt_D\dee{S} =-\pi \end{gather*}

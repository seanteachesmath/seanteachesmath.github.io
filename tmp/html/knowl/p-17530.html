In one second the point sweeps out an arc of this circle that subtends an angle of \(\Om\) radians. This arc is the fraction \(\frac{\Om}{2\pi}\) of a full circle and so has length \(\frac{\Om}{2\pi}2\pi \ell=\Om\ell=\Om|\vr|\,\sin\theta\text{.}\) Thus the point is moving with speed \(\Om|\vr|\,\sin\theta\text{.}\) The velocity vector of the point must have length \(\Om|\vr|\,\sin\theta\) and direction perpendicular to both \(\Ha\) and \(\vr\text{.}\) The vector \(\vOm\times\vr\) is perpendicular to both \(\vr\) and \(\vOm=\Om\Ha\) and has length \(\ |\vOm|\,|\vr|\,\sin\theta =\Om|\vr|\,\sin\theta \ \) as desired. So the velocity vector is either \(\vOm\times\vr\) or its negative. By the right hand rule it is \(\vOm\times\vr\text{.}\)

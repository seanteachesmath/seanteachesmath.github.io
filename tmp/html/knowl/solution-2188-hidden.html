(a) No. When \(u=v=0\text{,}\) \(z=4\) is not between \(0\) and \(1\text{.}\)

(b) Yes. Note that when \(x=\sqrt{4-u}\,\cos v\text{,}\) \(y=\sqrt{4-u}\,\sin v\) and \(z= u\) with \(0 \le u \le 1\text{,}\) \(0 \le v \le 2\pi\text{,}\)

  • \(\displaystyle z+x^2+y^2=4\)
  • \(\displaystyle 0\le z=u\le 1\)
  • For each fixed \(z=u\) between \(0\) and \(1\text{,}\) \((x,y)\) runs once around the circle \(x^2+y^2 =4-z =4-u\) as \(v\) runs from \(0\) to \(2\pi\text{.}\)

(c) Yes. Note that when \(x=u\,\cos v\text{,}\) \(y=u\,\sin v\) and \(z= 4-u^2\text{,}\) with \(\sqrt{3} \le u \le 2\text{,}\) \(0 \le v \le 2\pi\)

  • \(\displaystyle z+x^2+y^2=4\)
  • \(\displaystyle 0\le z=4-u^2\le 1\)
  • For each fixed \(z=4-u^2\) between \(0\) and \(1\text{,}\) \((x,y)\) runs once around the circle \(x^2+y^2 =4-z =u^2\) as \(v\) runs from \(0\) to \(2\pi\text{.}\)

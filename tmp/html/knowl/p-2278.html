The typical way to get used to implicit differentiation is to play with problems involving tangent lines to curves. So here are a few examples finding the equations of tangent lines to curves. Recall, from Theorem 3.3.4, that, in general, the tangent line to the curve \(y=f(x)\) at \(\big(x_0,y_0\big)\) is \(y=f(x_0)+f'(x_0)(x-x_0)=y_0+f'(x_0)(x-x_0)\text{.}\)

(a) To get a part of \(S_1\text{,}\) we need to use one of the parametrizations A, B, C, D, E, F. In the cases of A, B, C, for \(\vr(\theta, \phi) =x(\theta, \phi)\,\hi+ y(\theta, \phi)\,\hj+z(\theta, \phi)\,\hk\) to lie inside \(S_2\) we need (recalling that all points of \(S_1\) have \(z(\theta,\phi)\ge 0\) and hence \(0\le\phi\le\frac{\pi}{2}\))

\begin{align*} x(\theta, \phi)^2 + y(\theta, \phi)^2 \le 1 &\iff 4\sin^2\phi \le 1 \iff \sin\phi \le \frac{1}{2}\\ &\iff 0\le\phi\le \frac{\pi}{6} \end{align*}

In the cases of D, E, F, for \(\vr(\theta, z) =x(\theta, z)\,\hi+ y(\theta, z)\,\hj+z(\theta, z)\,\hk\) to lie inside \(S_2\) we need (recalling that all points of \(S_1\) have \(z(\theta,z)\ge 0\) and hence \(z\ge 0\))

\begin{align*} x(\theta, z)^2 + y(\theta, z)^2 \le 1 &\iff 4-z^2 \le 1 \iff z \ge \sqrt{3} \end{align*}

So parametrizations A and F work.

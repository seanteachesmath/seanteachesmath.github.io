We'll use spherical coordinates to compute the flux integral \(\ \dblInt_{S_1}\vF\cdot\hn \,dS\ \text{.}\) As the spherical coordinate \(\rho=4\) on all of \(S_1\text{,}\) we can paramerize \(S_1\) by

\begin{align*} &\vr(\theta,\varphi) = 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi \,\hi + 4\sin\theta\sin\varphi \,\hj + 4\cos\varphi \,\hk\\ &0 \le \theta \le 2 \pi,\ 0 \le \phi \le \pi/3 \end{align*}

So

\begin{align*} \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\theta} &= -4\sin\theta\sin\varphi \,\hi + 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi \,\hj\\ \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\varphi} &= 4\cos\theta\cos\varphi \,\hi + 4\sin\theta\cos\varphi \,\hj - 4\sin\varphi \,\hk\\ \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\theta}\times\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\varphi} &= \det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi & \hj & \hk\\ -4\sin\theta\sin\varphi & 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi & 0\\ 4\cos\theta\cos\varphi & 4\sin\theta\cos\varphi & -4\sin\varphi \end{matrix}\right]\\ &= -16\big(\cos\theta\sin^2\varphi\,,\, \sin\theta\sin^2\varphi\,,\, \sin\varphi\cos\varphi\big)\\ &= -4\,(\sin\varphi)\,\vr(\theta,\varphi) \end{align*}

and, by (14.3.1),

\begin{align*} \hn\,\dee{S} &= \pm\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\theta}\times \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\varphi}\,\dee{\theta}\dee{\varphi} = \mp 4\,(\sin\varphi)\,\vr(\theta,\varphi)\,\dee{\theta}\dee{\varphi} \end{align*}

To get the outward pointing normal, i.e. the normal point in the same direction as \(\vr(\theta,\varphi)\text{,}\) we take the plus sign. As \(\vF = \vr(\theta,\varphi)\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \vF\cdot \hn\,\dee{S} = 4\overbrace{|\vr(\theta,\varphi)|^2}^{4^2}\ \sin\varphi\,\dee{\theta}\,\dee{\varphi} = 64 \sin\varphi\,\dee{\theta}\,\dee{\varphi} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_{S_1}\vF\cdot\hn \,dS = 64 \int_{0}^{2\pi} \dee{\theta} \int_{0}^{\pi/3}\dee{\varphi}\ \sin \varphi = 64 \cdot 2\pi \Big[ - \cos \varphi \Big]_0^{\pi/3} = 64 \pi \end{gather*}

The temperature \(T(x,y)\) at a point of the \(xy\)--plane is given by

\begin{equation*} T(x,y) = 20 - 4x^2 - y^2 \end{equation*}
  1. Find the maximum and minimum values of \(T(x,y)\) on the disk \(D\) defined by \(x^2 + y^2 \le 4\text{.}\)
  2. Suppose an ant lives on the disk \(D\text{.}\) If the ant is initially at point \((1, 1)\text{,}\) in which direction should it move so as to increase its temperature as quickly as possible?
  3. Suppose that the ant moves at a velocity \(\vv = \llt -2, -1\rgt\text{.}\) What is its rate of increase of temperature as it passes through \((1, 1)\text{?}\)
  4. Suppose the ant is constrained to stay on the curve \(y = 2 - x^2\text{.}\) Where should the ant go if it wants to be as warm as possible?

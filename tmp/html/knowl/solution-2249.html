Solution 14.3.6.39.1.

(a) By definition \(\vF\) is a conservative vector field with potential \(f\text{.}\) Suppose that the curve \(C\) starts at \(P_1\text{,}\) on \(S\text{,}\) and ends at \(P_2\text{,}\) on \(S\text{.}\) Then \(f(P_1)=f(P_2)=c\) and, by Theorem 13.4.2,

\begin{equation*} \int_C \vF \cdot \dee{\vr} = f(P_2) - f(P_1) =c - c =0 \end{equation*}

(b) Since \(\vF=\vnabla f\text{,}\) \(\vF\) is normal to the level surfaces of \(f\) by Lemma 13.3.6. So, at any point of \(S\text{,}\) \(\vF\) is a scalar multiple of \(\hn\) and \(\vF\times\vG\) is perpendicular to \(\hn\text{.}\) Thus \((\vF \times \vG) \cdot\hn=0\) and

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S (\vF \times \vG) \cdot\hn\, \dee{S} = 0. \end{equation*}

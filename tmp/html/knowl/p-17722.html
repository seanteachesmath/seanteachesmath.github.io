Then the boundary, \(\partial V\text{,}\) of \(V\) consists of two parts, namely \(S\) (with normal pointing upwards) and the disk

\begin{equation*} D=\Set{(x,y,0)}{x^2+y^2\le 1} \end{equation*}

(with normal pointing downwards). The divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.2) gives

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot \hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} -\dblInt_D \vF \cdot (-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_D (x^2+y^2)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

Switching to polar coordinates, the flux is

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_S \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\int_0^1\dee{r}\,r\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ r^2 =2\pi \int_0^1 \dee{r}\, r^3 =2\pi \tfrac{1}{4} =\tfrac{\pi}{2} \end{gather*}

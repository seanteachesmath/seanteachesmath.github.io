Lemma 14.4.1.

The rate at which fluid mass is crossing through a surface \(S\) is the flux integral

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S \rho(x,y,z,t)\vv(x,y,z,t)\cdot\hn(x,y,z)\,\dee{S} \end{equation*}

Here \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid, \(\vv\) is the velocity field of the fluid, and \(\hn(x,y,z)\) is a unit normal to \(S\) at \((x,y,z)\text{.}\) If the flux integral is positive the fluid is crossing in the direction \(\hn\text{.}\) If it is negative the fluid is crossing opposite to the direction ofÂ \(\hn\text{.}\) The rate at which volume of fluid is crossing through a surface \(S\) is the flux integral

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S \vv(x,y,z,t)\cdot\hn(x,y,z)\,\dee{S} \end{equation*}

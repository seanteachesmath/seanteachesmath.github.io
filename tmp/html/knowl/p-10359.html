(a) For large \(n\text{,}\) \(n^2\gg 1\) and so \(\sqrt{n^2+1}\approx \sqrt{n^2}= n\text{.}\) This suggests that we apply the limit comparison test with \(a_n=\frac{1}{\sqrt{n^2+1}}\) and \(b_n=\frac{1}{n}\text{.}\) Since

\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{1/\sqrt{n^2+1}}{1/n} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+1/n^2}} =1 \end{equation*}

and since \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1}{n}\) diverges, the given series diverges.

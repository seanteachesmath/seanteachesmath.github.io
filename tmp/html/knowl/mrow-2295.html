\begin{align*} \sin\left(\theta(t)\right)&=\frac{x(t)}{z(t)}\\ \end{align*}

Differentiating with respect to \(t\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \theta'(t)\cos\left(\theta(t)\right) &=\frac{x'(t)z(t)-x(t)z'(t)}{z(t)^2}\\ \theta'(t)&=\frac{x'(t)z(t)-x(t)z'(t)}{z(t)^2\cos\left(\theta(t)\right)}\\ \end{align*}

From our diagram, we see \(\cos\left(\theta(t)\right)=\dfrac{0.5}{z(t)}\text{,}\) so:

\begin{align*} &=2\frac{x'(t)z(t)-x(t)z'(t)}{z(t)}\\ \end{align*}

Substituting in \(x'(t)=2\text{,}\) \(z(t)=1.3\text{,}\) \(x(t)=1.2\text{,}\) and \(z'(t)=\dfrac{2\times1.2}{1.3}\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \theta'(t) &=2\frac{2\times 1.3-1.2\times\frac{2\times 1.2}{1.3}}{1.3} \approx .592 \mbox{ radians/min} \end{align*}

(a) We know that \(e^x = \sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty \frac{x^n}{n!}\) for all \(x\text{.}\) Replacing \(x\) by \(-x\text{,}\) we also have \(e^{-x} = \sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-x)^n}{n!}\) for all \(x\) and hence

\begin{align*} \cosh(x) \amp=\frac{1}{2}\big[e^x+e^{-x}\big] =\frac{1}{2}\Big[\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{x^n}{n!} + \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-x)^n}{n!}\Big] = \sum_{\genfrac{}{}{0pt}{}{n=0}{n\text{ even}}}^\infty\frac{x^n}{n!}\\ \amp = \sum_{n=0}^\infty\frac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!} \end{align*}

for all \(x\text{.}\) In particular, the interval of convergence is all real numbers.

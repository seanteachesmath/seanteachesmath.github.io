The divergence theorem, of course. The integral can be easily evaluated by using that, for any solid \(\cV\) in \(\bbbr^3\text{,}\)

\begin{gather*} \tripInt_\cV\dee{V}=\text{Volume}(\cV) \end{gather*}

and

\begin{gather*} \bar x =\frac{\tripInt_\cV x\,\dee{V}}{\text{Volume}(\cV)}\qquad \bar y =\frac{\tripInt_\cV y\,\dee{V}}{\text{Volume}(\cV)}\qquad \bar z =\frac{\tripInt_\cV z\,\dee{V}}{\text{Volume}(\cV)} \end{gather*}

where \((\bar x,\bar y,\bar z)\) is the centroid of \(\cV\text{.}\)

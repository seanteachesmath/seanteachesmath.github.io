Integrals that look like \(\dblInt_S \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\text{,}\) with \(\hn(x,y,z)\) being a unit vector that is perpendicular to \(S\) at \((x,y,z)\text{,}\) are called flux integrals. We shall see in ยง14.4, that when \(\vv\) is the velocity field of a moving fluid and \(\rho\) is the density of the fluid, then \(\dblInt_S \rho\vv\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\) is the rate at which fluid is crossing the surface \(S\text{.}\)

Solution 4.1.2.11.1.

If the acceleration was constant, then it was

\begin{equation*} \frac{17 500\;\mathrm{mph}}{\frac{8.5}{60}\;\mathrm{hr}} \approx 123 500 \;\frac{\mathrm{miles}}{\mathrm{hr}^2} \end{equation*}

So, the velocity \(t\) hours from liftoff is

\begin{equation*} v(t)=123500t \end{equation*}

Therefore, the position of the shuttle \(t\) hours from liftoff (taking \(s(0)=0\) to be its initial position) is

\begin{equation*} s(t)=\frac{123500}{2}t^2=61750t^2 \end{equation*}

So, after \(\frac{8.5}{60}\) hours, the shuttle has travelled

\begin{equation*} s\left(\frac{8.5}{60}\right)=(61750)\left(\frac{8.5}{60}\right)^2\approx 1240 \;\mathrm{miles} \end{equation*}

or a little less than 2000 kilometres.

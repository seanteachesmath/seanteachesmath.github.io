  1. \(\displaystyle f_2(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & 2 \leq x \lt 3\\ 0 & \text{else} \end{cases}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle f_3(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & 3 \leq x \lt 4\\ 0 & \text{else} \end{cases}\)

  3. For any \(n\text{,}\) \(f_n(x)=1\) for an interval of length 1, and \(f_n(x)=0\) for all other \(x\text{.}\) So, the area under the curve is a square of side length one.

    Then \(A_n = \int_0^\infty f_n(x)\,\dee{x}=1\) for all \(n\text{.}\) That is, the sequence \(\{A_n\}\) is simply \(\{1,1,\ldots,1\}\text{,}\) a sequence of all 1s.

  4. Given the description above, \(\displaystyle\lim_{n \to \infty}A_n=1\text{.}\)
  5. For any fixed \(x\text{,}\) recall \(\{f_n(x)\} = \{0,\ldots,0,1,0,\ldots 0,0,0,0,0,\ldots\}\text{.}\) In particular, there are infinitely many zeroes at its end. So, \(\displaystyle\lim_{n \to \infty} f_n(x)=0\text{.}\) Then \(g(x)=0\) for every \(x\text{.}\)
  6. Given the description above, \(\displaystyle \int_0^\infty g(x)\,\dee{x} = \int_0^{\infty}0\,\dee{x}=0\text{.}\)

Remark: what we've shown here is that, for this particular \(f_n(x)\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \lim_{n \to \infty}\int_0^{\infty}f_n(x)\,\dee{x} \neq \int_0^{\infty}\lim_{n \to \infty} f_n(x)\,\dee{x} \end{equation*}

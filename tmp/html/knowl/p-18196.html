This vector field cannot have a vector potential on its domain of definition, i.e. on \(\bbbr^3\setminus\{(0,0,0)\} =\Set{(x,y,z)}{(x,y,z)\ne(0,0,0)} \text{.}\) To see this, suppose to the contrary that it did have a vector potential \(\vA\text{.}\) Then its flux through any closed surface 5  (i.e. surface without a boundary) \(S\) would be

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_S\vB\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} = \dblInt_S\vnabla\times\vA\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\oint_{\partial S}\vA\cdot\dee{\vr} =0 \end{gather*}

by Stokes' theorem, since \(\partial S\) is empty. But we found in Example 14.4.2, with \(m=1\text{,}\) that the flux of \(\vB\) through any sphere centred on the origin is \(4\pi\text{.}\)

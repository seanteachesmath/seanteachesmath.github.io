Solution 15.4.3.2.1.

Think of the \(xy\)-plane as being the plane \(z=0\) in \(\bbbr^3\text{.}\)

We are going to apply Stokes' theorem (Theorem 15.4.1) with \(S\) being the given region \(R\) in the \(xy\)-plane and with \(\vF(x,y,z) = F_1(x,y)\,\hi +F_2(x,y)\,\hj\text{.}\) Then

  • the unit normal vector to \(S\) specified in Stokes theorem is \(\hk\) (if you walk along \(\partial S = C\) in the direction of the arrow on \(C\) with the vector from your feet to your head having direction \(\hk\) then \(S=R\) is on your left hand side) and
  • \(\dee{S}=\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) and
  • the curl of \(\vF\) is
    \begin{equation*} \vnabla\times\vF=\det\left[\begin{matrix}\hi & \hj & \hk \\ \pdiff{}{x} & \pdiff{}{y} & \pdiff{}{z} \\ F_1(x,y) & F_2(x,y) & 0 \end{matrix}\right] =\Big(\frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y}\Big)\,\hk \end{equation*}

So Stokes' theorem gives

\begin{align*} \oint_{C} \big[F_1(x,y)\,\dee{x} +F_2(x,y)\,\dee{y}\big] &=\oint_{\partial S}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\dblInt_{S}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\ \dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_{R}\Big(\frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x} - \frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y}\Big)\ \dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

We saw this kind of problem in Question 4.4.11.4.3. The fourth-degree Taylor polynomial for \(f(x)\) about \(x=1\) is

\begin{align*} T_4(x)&=f(1)+f'(1)(x-1)+\frac{1}{2}f''(1)(x-1)^2+\frac{1}{3!}f'''(1)(x-1)^3\\ \amp\hskip2in+\frac{1}{4!}f^{(4)}(1)(x-1)^4\\ \end{align*}

while the third-degree Maclaurin polynomial for \(f(x)\) about \(x=1\) is

\begin{align*} T_3(x)&=f(1)+f'(1)(x-1)+\frac{1}{2}f''(1)(x-1)^2+\frac{1}{3!}f'''(1)(x-1)^3 \end{align*}

So, we “chopped off” the term of degree 4 to get \(T_3(x)\text{.}\) However, our polynomial is not in this form. It's not clear, right away, what the term \(f^{(4)}(x-1)^4\) is in our given \(T_4(x)\text{.}\) So, we will use a different method from Question 4.4.11.4.3.

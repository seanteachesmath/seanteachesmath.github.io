Argument 1: We may rewrite the equation \(z=\frac{9-x^2-y^2}{9+x^2+y^2}\) of the curved boundary of \(V\) as

\begin{equation*} z(9+x^2+y^2) = 9-x^2-y^2 \iff x^2+y^2 = \frac{9(1-z)}{1+z} \end{equation*}

This is the equation of the circle of radius \(r(z)=\sqrt{\frac{9(1-z)}{1+z}}\) centred on \(x=y=0\text{.}\) So \(z\) runs from \(0\) to \(1\text{,}\) and for each fixed \(0\le z\le 1\text{,}\) \(y\) runs from \(-r(z)\) to \(r(z)\) and, for each fixed \(y\) and \(z\text{,}\) \(x\) runs from \(-\sqrt{r(z)^2-y^2}\) to \(\sqrt{r(z)^2-y^2}\text{.}\) So

\begin{gather*} \tripInt_V x\,\dee{V} =\int_0^1 \dee{z}\int_{-r(z)}^{r(z)}\dee{y} \int_{-\sqrt{r(z)^2-y^2}}^{\sqrt{r(z)^2-y^2}} \dee{x}\ x =\int_0^1 \dee{z}\int_{-r(z)}^{r(z)}\dee{y}\ 0 =0 \end{gather*}

since \(\int_{-a}^a x\ \dee{x}=0\) for any \(a \gt 0\text{.}\)

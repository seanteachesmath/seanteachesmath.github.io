  • Set \(S=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+z^2=4,\ 0\le y\le 3}\text{.}\) We are to compute \(\dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\text{,}\) with \(\hn\) denoting the outward normal to \(S\text{.}\) \(S\) is not the boundary of a solid, so we cannot compute \(\dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\) by applying the divergence theorem directly. The figure on the left below shows the part of \(S\) that is in the first octant.

  • On the other hand \(S\text{,}\) is “almost” the boundary of
    \begin{equation*} V = \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+z^2\le 4,\ 0\le y\le 3} \end{equation*}
    The boundary, \(\partial V\) of \(V\) consists of three pieces — \(S\) and the two disks
    \begin{align*} D_l &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+z^2\le 4,\ y=0}\\ D_r &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+z^2\le 4,\ y=3} \end{align*}
    The figure on the right above shows the parts of \(S\text{,}\) \(V\text{,}\) \(D_l\) and \(D_r\) that are in the first octant.

The outward normal to \(D_r\) is \(\hj\) and the outward normal to \(D_l\) is \(-\hj\text{,}\) to the divergence theorem gives

\begin{align*} \tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot \vF\,\dee{V} &=\dblInt_{\partial V}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_{S}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} + \dblInt_{D_r}\vF\cdot\hj\,\dee{S} + \dblInt_{D_l}\vF\cdot(-\hj)\,\dee{S} \end{align*}

Since \(\vnabla\cdot\vF = 2\) and \(\vF\cdot\hj = x+z\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &= \tripInt_V 2\,\dee{V} -\dblInt_{x^2+z^2\le 4}\hskip-5pt(x+z)\,\dee{x}\dee{z} - \dblInt_{x^2+z^2\le 4}\hskip-5pt(-x-z)\,\dee{x}\dee{z}\\ &= \tripInt_V 2\,\dee{V}\\ &= 2\ \text{volume}(V) =2(\pi 2^2) 3 =24\pi \end{align*}

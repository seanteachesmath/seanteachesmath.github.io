From the observations that

\begin{equation*} \vnabla\times\vF=\det\left[\begin{matrix}\hi & \hj & \hk \\ \pdiff{}{x} & \pdiff{}{y} & \pdiff{}{z} \\ e^{x^2}+yz & \cos(y^2)-x^2 & \sin(z^2)+xy \end{matrix} \right] =x\,\hi-(z+2x)\,\hk \end{equation*}

and that we can rewrite \(x^2-2x+4y^2=15\) as \((x-1)^2+4y^2=16\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} \oint_C\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\dblInt_S \vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_{(x-1)^2+4y^2\le 16}\, [x\hi-(z+2x)\hk]\Big|_{z=-7+x+2y}\cdot(-1,-2,1)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &=\dblInt_{(x-1)^2+4y^2\le 16} [-x-(-7+x+2y+2x)]\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &=\dblInt_{(x-1)^2+4y^2\le 16} [7-4x-2y]\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{align*}

To evaluate the integrals of \(x\) and \(y\) we use that, for any region \(R\) in the \(xy\)--plane,

\begin{equation*} \bar x =\frac{\dblInt_R x\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}}{\text{Area}(R)}\qquad \bar y =\frac{\dblInt_R y\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}}{\text{Area}(R)} \end{equation*}

Our ellipse is \(\frac{(x-1)^2}{4^2}+\frac{y^2}{2^2}=1\) and so has area \(\pi ab = \pi\times4\times 2=8\pi\) and centroid \((\bar x,\bar y) = (1,0)\text{.}\) So, using \(R=\Set{(x,y)}{\frac{(x-1)^2}{4^2}+\frac{y^2}{2^2}\le 1}\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \oint_C\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\dblInt_R [7-4x-2y]\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &=\text{Area}(R) \big\{7-4\bar x-2\bar y\}\\ &=8\pi[7-4\times 1-2\times 0]=24\pi \end{align*}

Solution 5.1.2.5.1.

For any constant \(n\ne 1\text{,}\) an antiderivative of \(x^n\) is \(\frac{1}{n+1}x^{n+1}\text{.}\) The constant \(n\) does not have to be an integer.

\begin{align*} F'(x)&=4\sqrt[3]{x}-\frac{9}{2x^{2.7}}\\ &=4 x^{\tfrac{1}{3}}-\frac{9}{2}x^{-2.7}\\ F(x)&=4\left(\frac{1}{\frac{1}{3}+1}\right)x^{\left(\tfrac{1}{3}+1\right)}-\left(\frac{9}{2}\right)\left(\frac{1}{-2.7+1}\right)x^{(-2.7+1)}+C\\ &=4\left(\frac{3}{4}\right)x^{\tfrac{4}{3}}-\left(\frac{9}{2}\right)\left(\frac{10}{-17}\right)x^{-1.7}+C\\ &=3x^{\tfrac{4}{3}}+\frac{45}{17x^{1.7}}+C \end{align*}

Solution 15.3.1.9.1.

(a) The curves \(y = x^2 + 4x + 4\) and \(y = 4 - x^2\) meet when

\begin{equation*} x^2 + 4x + 4 = 4 - x^2 \iff 2x^2 +4x = 2x(x+2) = 0 \end{equation*}

So the curves intersect at \((0,4)\) and \((-2,0)\text{.}\) Here is a sketch.

(b) Let

\begin{equation*} R=\Set{(x,y)\in\bbbr^2}{ x^2+4x+4\le y\le 4-x^2,\ -2\le x\le 0} \end{equation*}

By Green's theorem (Theorem 15.3.2)

\begin{align*} \oint_C xy\, \dee{x} + (e^y + x^2 ) \dee{y} &=\dblInt_R \left\{ \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x}(e^y+x^2) -\tfrac{\partial }{\partial y}(xy) \right\}\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &= \int_{-2}^0\dee{x} \int_{x^2+4x+4}^{4-x^2}\dee{y} \ x\\ &= \int_{-2}^0\dee{x}\ (-2x^2-4x) x\\ &=\left[-\frac{x^4}{2} -\frac{4x^3}{3}\right]^0_{-2}\\ &=-\frac{8}{3} \end{align*}

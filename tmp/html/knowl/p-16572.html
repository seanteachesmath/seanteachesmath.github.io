Note that:

  • Along the line segment from \((0, 0)\) to \((1,0)\text{,}\) \(x\) increases from \(0\) to \(1\text{,}\) while \(y\) is held fixed at \(y=0\text{.}\) So we may parametrize this segment by \(\vr(x) = x\,\hi\text{,}\) \(0\le x\le 1\text{.}\)
  • Along the line segment from \((1, 0)\) to \((1,\pi)\text{,}\) \(y\) increases from \(0\) to \(\pi\text{,}\) while \(x\) is held fixed at \(x=1\text{.}\) So we may parametrize this segment by \(\vr(x) = \hi + y\,\hj\text{,}\) \(0\le y\le\pi\text{.}\)
  • Along the line segment from \((1, \pi)\) to \((0,\pi)\text{,}\) \(x\) decreases from \(1\) to \(0\text{,}\) while \(y\) is held fixed at \(y=\pi\text{.}\) So we may parametrize this segment by \(\vr(x) = x\,\hi+\pi\,\hj\) with \(x\) running from \(1\) to \(0\text{.}\)

Hence

\begin{align*} \int_C \vV\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\int_0^1 \vV(x,0)\cdot\hi\ \dee{x}+\int_0^\pi \vV(1,y)\cdot\hj\ \dee{y} +\int_1^0 \vV(x,\pi)\cdot\hi\ \dee{x}\\ &=\int_0^1 (e^x+x^2)\ \dee{x} +\int_0^\pi (y+3)\ \dee{y}+\int_1^0 (-e^x+x^2)\ \dee{x}\cr &=2\int_0^1 e^x\ \dee{x}+\int_0^\pi (y+3)\ \dee{y}\\ &=2(e-1)+\frac{\pi^2}{2}+3\pi \end{align*}

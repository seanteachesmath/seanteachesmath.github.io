We shall apply Stokes' Theorem. The curl of \(\vF\) is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF&=\det\left[\begin{matrix}\hi & \hj & \hk \\ \pdiff{}{x} & \pdiff{}{y} & \pdiff{}{z} \\ e^{x^2} - yz & \sin y - yz & xz + 2y\end{matrix}\right|\\ &=(2+y)\,\hi-(z+y)\,\hj+(0+z)\,\hk \end{align*}

The curve \(C\) is a triangle. All three vertices of the triangle obey \(x+y+z=1\text{.}\) So the triangle is the boundary of the surface \(S=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x\ge 0,\ y\ge 0,\ z=1-x-y\ge 0}\text{.}\)

The equation of the surface is \(z=f(x,y)=1-x-y\text{.}\) So, by (14.3.2),

\begin{align*} \hn\ \dee{S} &=\big(-f_x\,\hi-f_y\,\hj+\hk\big)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &=(\hi+\hj+\hk)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{align*}

Here \(\hn\) is the upward pointing unit normal. The set of points \((x,y)\) for which there is a corresponding \((x,y,z)\) in \(S\) is \(T=\Set{(x,y)}{x\ge 0,\ y\ge 0,\ x+y\le 1}\text{,}\) which is a triangle of area \(\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Since

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=[(2+y)\hi-(z+y)\hj+(0+z)\hk]\cdot(\hi+\hj+\hk)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &= 2\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{align*}

we have

\begin{align*} \oint_C\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}&=\dblInt_S\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_T 2\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} = 2\,\text{Area}(T) =1 \end{align*}

Consider the surface \(S\) given by \(z=e^{x^2+y^2}\text{.}\)

  1. Compute the volume under \(S\) and above the disk \(x^2+y^2\le 9\) in the \(xy\)-plane.
  2. The volume under \(S\) and above a certain region \(R\) in the \(xy\)-plane is
    \begin{equation*} \int_0^1\bigg(\int_0^y e^{x^2+y^2}\,\dee{x}\bigg)\dee{y} +\int_1^2\bigg(\int_0^{2-y} e^{x^2+y^2}\,\dee{x}\bigg)\dee{y} \end{equation*}
    Sketch \(R\) and express the volume as a single iterated integral with the order of integration reversed. Do not compute either integral in part (b).

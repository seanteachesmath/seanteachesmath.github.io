(b) This time we'll use the divergence theorem. The surface \(S\) is not closed. So we'll use the auxilary surface formed by “topping \(S\) off” with the cap \(T=\Set{(5,y,z)}{y^2+z^2\le 9}\text{.}\) If we give \(T\) the normal vector \(\hi\text{,}\) this auxiliary surface, the union of \(S\) and \(T\text{,}\) is the boundary of \(V=\Set{(x,y,z)}{y^2+z^2\le 9,\ 0\le x\le 5}\text{.}\) So the divergence theorem gives

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} +\dblInt_T \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &= \dblInt_{\partial V} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot \vF\ \dee{V}\\ &=0 \end{align*}

since \(\vnabla\cdot \vF = 0\text{.}\) Thus the flux of interest is

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=-\dblInt_T \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =-\dblInt_T \vF\cdot\hi\,\dee{S} =-\dblInt_T (2+z)\,\dee{S}\\ &=-2\dblInt_T \,\dee{S} \qquad\text{since } \dblInt_T z\,\dee{S}=0, \text{ because $z$ is odd}\\ &= -18\pi \qquad\text{since $T$ has area $9\pi$} \end{align*}

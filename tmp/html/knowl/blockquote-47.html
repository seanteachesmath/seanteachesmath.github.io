Problem: Evaluate \(\displaystyle\int_{1}^{\pi} \dfrac{\cos(\log t)}{t}\dee{t}\text{.}\)

Work: We use the substitution \(u=\log t\text{,}\) so \(\dee{u}=\frac{1}{t}\dee{t}\text{.}\) Then:

\begin{align*} \int_{1}^{\pi} \dfrac{\cos(\log t)}{t}\dee{t}&=\int_1^{\pi}\cos(u) \dee{u}\\ &=\sin(\pi)-\sin(1)=-\sin(1)\, . \end{align*}

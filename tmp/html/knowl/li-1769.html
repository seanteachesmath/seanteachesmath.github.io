The first time interval runs from \(a\) to \(a+\frac{b-a}{n}\text{.}\) If we think of \(n\) as some large number, the width of this interval, \(\frac{b-a}{n}\) is very small and over this time interval, the velocity does not change very much. Hence we can approximate the velocity over the first subinterval as being essentially constant at its value at the start of the time interval â€” \(v(a)\text{.}\) Over the subinterval the \(x\)-coordinate changes by velocity times time, namely \(v(a) \cdot \frac{b-a}{n}\text{.}\)

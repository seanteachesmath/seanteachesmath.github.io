A car drives down the track so that its position at time \(t\) is given by \(\vr(t^2)\text{.}\) (Note the relationship between \(t\) and \(\theta\) is \(\theta = t^2\)). Compute the following quantities.

  1. The speed at the point \(\big(-4, 0, \frac{1}{4}\big)\text{.}\)
  2. The acceleration at the point \(\big(-4, 0, \frac{1}{4}\big)\text{.}\)
  3. The magnitude of the normal component of the acceleration at the point
    \begin{equation*} \big(-4, 0, \frac{1}{4}\big)\text{.} \end{equation*}

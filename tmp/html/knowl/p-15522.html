The circle of radius \(r\) centred at \((0,r)\) is \(x^2+(y-r)^2 = r^2\text{.}\) The bottom half of this circle is

\begin{equation*} y = g(x) = r - \sqrt{r^2-x^2} \end{equation*}

So

\begin{alignat*}{2} g'(x) &=\frac{x}{\sqrt{r^2-x^2}} & g'(0) &=0\\ g''(x)&=\frac{1}{\sqrt{r^2-x^2}} +\frac{x^2}{[{r^2-x^2]}^{3/2}}\qquad & g''(0)&=\frac{1}{r} \end{alignat*}

As \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) have the same second order Taylor approximation at \(x=0\text{,}\) \(f''(0) = g''(0) = \frac{1}{r}\text{.}\)

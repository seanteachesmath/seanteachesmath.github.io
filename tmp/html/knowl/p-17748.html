Then the boundary, \(\partial V\text{,}\) of \(V\) consists of two parts, namely \(S\) (with normal pointing upwards) and the disk

\begin{equation*} D=\Set{(x,y,0)}{x^2+y^2\le 1} \end{equation*}

(with normal \(-\hk\)). The divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.2) gives

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot \hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} -\dblInt_D \vF \cdot (-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &=\tripInt_V 2\,\dee{V} +\dblInt_D \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &=2\ \frac{1}{2}\ \frac{4}{3}\pi 1^3 +\dblInt_D \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

Switching to polar coordinates, the flux is

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\frac{4}{3}\pi+\int_0^1\dee{r}\,r\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ r =\frac{4}{3}\pi + 2\pi \int_0^1 \dee{r}\, r^2\\ &=\frac{4}{3}\pi + 2\pi \frac{1}{3} =2\pi \end{align*}

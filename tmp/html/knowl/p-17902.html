(b) Similarly, “the total flux of \(\vG = u\nabla u\) out through the boundary of \(\cR\)” equals

\begin{equation*} J = \dblInt_\cS \vG\cdot \hn\,\dee{S} = \tripInt_\cR \nabla\cdot\vG\,\dee{V} \end{equation*}

Here \(\vG = u\vF\) (using the notation from part (a)), so by the vector identity of Theorem 15.1.4.c,

\begin{equation*} \nabla\cdot\vG = \nabla\cdot(u\vF) = (\nabla u)\cdot\vF + u(\nabla\cdot\vF) \end{equation*}

But \(\vF = \nabla u\text{,}\) so \(\nabla\cdot\vF=\Delta u=0\) as in part (a), giving

\begin{equation*} \nabla\cdot\vG = |\nabla u|^2 + 0 \end{equation*}

In conclusion,

\begin{equation*} J = \tripInt_\cR \nabla\cdot\vG\,\dee{V} = \tripInt_\cR \Big[ \Big(\frac{\partial u}{ \partial x}\Big)^2 + \Big(\frac{\partial u}{\partial y}\Big)^2 + \Big(\frac{\partial u}{\partial z}\Big)^2\Big]\,\dee{V} \end{equation*}

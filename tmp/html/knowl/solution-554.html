Solution 4.4.11.9.1.1.

The third Maclaurin polynomial for \(f(x)\) is

\begin{align*} f(0) + f'(0)x +\frac{f''(0)}{2}\cdot x^2 + \frac{f'''(0)}{6}\cdot x^3 &=4+3x^2+\frac{1}{2}x^3. \end{align*}

The coefficient of \(x\) is \(f'(0)\) on one side, and \(0\) on the other, so \(f'(0)=0\text{.}\)

The coefficient of \(x^2\) is \(\dfrac{1}{2}f''(0)\) on one side, and \(3\) on the other, so \(f''(0)=6\text{.}\)

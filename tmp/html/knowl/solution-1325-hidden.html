For large \(n\text{,}\) the numerator \(n^3-4\approx n^3\) and the denominator \(2n^5-6n\approx 2n^5\text{,}\) so the \(n\)th term is approximately \(\frac{n^3}{2n^5}=\frac{1}{2n^2}\text{.}\) So we apply the limit comparison test with \(a_n=\frac{n^3-4}{2n^5-6n}\) and \(b_n=\frac{1}{n^2}\text{.}\) Since

\begin{equation*} \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{a_n}{b_n} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{(n^3-4)/(2n^5-6n)}{1/n^2} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{1-\frac{4}{n^3}}{2-\frac{6}{n^4}} =\frac12 \end{equation*}

exists and is nonzero, the given series \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty a_n\) converges if and only if the series \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty b_n\) converges. Since the series \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty b_n =\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1}{n^2}\) is a convergent \(p\)-series (with \(p=2\)), both series converge.

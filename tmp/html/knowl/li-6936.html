Item b.
Implicitly: We can also specify that the surface is the set of points \((x,y,z)\) that satisfy the equation \(G(x,y,z)=0\text{,}\) or, more generally 1 , satisfy the equation \(G(x,y,z)=K\text{,}\) with \(K\) a constant. For example, the sphere of radius one centred on the origin is the set of points that obey
\begin{equation*} x^2+y^2+z^2=1 \end{equation*}
We shall explore this surface a little more in Example 14.1.2 below.

Exercise 24. M317 2008A.

A physicist studies a vector field \(\vF\text{.}\) From experiments, it is known that \(\vF\) is of the form

\begin{equation*} \vF = (x - a)ye^x\,\hi + (xe^x + z^3 )\,\hj + byz^2\,\hk \end{equation*}

where \(a\) and \(b\) are some real numbers. From theoretical considerations, it is known that \(\vF\) is conservative.

  1. Determine \(a\) and \(b\text{.}\)
  2. Find a potential \(f(x,y,z)\) such that \(\vnabla f = \vF\text{.}\)
  3. Evaluate the line intgeral \(\int_C\vF\cdot\dee{\vr}\) where \(C\) is the curve defined by
    \begin{equation*} \vr(t) = \big(t\,,\, \cos 2t\,,\, \cos t\big), \qquad 0 \le t \le \pi \end{equation*}
  4. Evaluate the line integral
    \begin{equation*} I = \int_C (x + 1)ye^x \,\dee{x} + (xe^x + z^3 ) \,\dee{y} + 4yz^2 \,\dee{z}, \end{equation*}
    where \(C\) is the same curve as in part (c). [Note: the “4” in the last term is not a misprint!].

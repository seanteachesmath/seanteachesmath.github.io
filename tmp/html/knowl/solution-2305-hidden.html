Let \(S'\) be the disk \(x^2+y^2\le 3,\ z=0\) (with \(\hn\) the downward pointing normal) and let \(V\) be the portion of the ball \(x^2+y^2+(z-1)^2\le 4\) with \(z\ge 0\text{.}\) Then, by the divergence theorem,

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_{V}\vnabla\cdot\vF\, \dee{V} -\dblInt_{S'}\vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &=\tripInt_{V}(2x+2y)\, \dee{V} +\dblInt_{S'}(4+5x)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

Because \(x\) is odd under \(x\rightarrow-x\) and \(y\) is odd under \(y\rightarrow -y\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \tripInt_{V}x\, \dee{V}=\tripInt_{V}y\, \dee{V}=\dblInt_{S'}x\,\dee{x}\dee{y}=0 \end{equation*}

so that

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_{S}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =4\dblInt_{S'}\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =4\,\text{Area}(S') =4\times \pi\big(\sqrt{3}\big)^2=12\pi \end{gather*}

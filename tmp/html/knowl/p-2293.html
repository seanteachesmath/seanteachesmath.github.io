The position of the (centre of the) ball at time \(t\) is \(x(t)=d-vt\text{,}\) where \(d\) is the distance from your head to the pitcher's mound and \(v\) is the ball's velocity. Your eye sees the ball filling  3  an angle \(2\theta(t)\) with

\begin{gather*} \sin\big(\theta(t)\big)=\frac{r}{d-vt} \end{gather*}

where \(r\) is the radius of the baseball. The question is “How fast is \(\theta\) growing at time \(t\text{?}\)” That is, what is \(\diff{\theta}{t}\text{?}\)

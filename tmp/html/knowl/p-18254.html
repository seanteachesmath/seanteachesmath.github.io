Note that \(\cC\) is the boundary curve for both \(\cS\) and \(\cD\text{.}\) So, by Stokes' theorem, twice

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{\cS}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\int_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\dblInt_{\cD}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_{\cD}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hk\,\dee{S} \end{align*}

Now the \(\hk\) component of \(\vnabla\times\vF\) is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hk &=\pdiff{}{x}\big[x(3+y)\big] - \pdiff{}{y}\big[x\ln(1+z)\big] =3+y \end{align*}

Solution 1: Substituting \(u=\cos x\text{,}\) \(\dee{u}=-\sin x\,\dee{x}\text{,}\) \(\sin^2 x= 1-\cos^2x=1-u^2\text{,}\) gives
\begin{align*} \int \tan^3 x \sec^5x \,\dee{x} &=\int\frac{\sin^3 x}{\cos^8 x}\,\dee{x} =\int\frac{(1-\cos^2 x)\sin x}{\cos^8 x}\,\dee{x}\\ &=-\int\frac{1-u^2}{u^8}\,\dee{u} =-\Big[\frac{u^{-7}}{-7}-\frac{u^{-5}}{-5}\Big]+C\\ &=\frac{1}{7}\sec^7 x -\frac{1}{5}\sec^5 x + C \end{align*}

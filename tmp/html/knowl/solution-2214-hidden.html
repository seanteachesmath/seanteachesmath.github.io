The surface is \(z=f(x,y)\) with \(f(x,y)=xy\text{.}\) So, by (14.3.2),

\begin{equation*} \dee{S}=\sqrt{1+f_x^2+f_y^2}\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} =\sqrt{1+x^2+y^2}\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{align*} I &= \dblInt_S (x^2 + y^2)\ \dee{S} = \dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 3} (x^2 + y^2)\ \sqrt{1+x^2+y^2}\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &= \int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\int_0^{\sqrt{3}} \dee{r}\ r\ r^2\sqrt{1+r^2} \end{align*}

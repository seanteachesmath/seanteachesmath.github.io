Answer 3.14.2.17.1.
  • 3.14.2.17.a \(f'(x)=(1+2x)e^{x+x^2}\) \(f''(x)=(4x^2+4x+3)e^{x+x^2}\) \(h'(x)=1+3x\) \(h''(x)=3\)
  • 3.14.2.17.b \(f(0)=h(0)=1\text{;}\) \(f'(0)=h'(0)=1\text{;}\) \(f''(0)=h''(0)=3\)
  • 3.14.2.17.c \(f\) and \(h\) “start at the same place”, since \(f(0)=h(0)\text{.}\) Also \(f'(0)=h'(0)\text{,}\) and \(f''(x)=(4x^2+4x+3)e^{x+x^2} \gt 3e^{x+x^2} \gt 3=h''(x)\) when \(x \gt 0\text{.}\) Since \(f'(0)=h'(0)\text{,}\) and since \(f'\) grows faster than \(h'\) for positive \(x\text{,}\) we conclude \(f'(x) \gt h'(x)\) for all positive \(x\text{.}\) Now we can conclude that (since \(f(0)=h(0)\) and \(f\) grows faster than \(h\) when \(x \gt 0\)) also \(f(x) \gt h(x)\) for all positive \(x\text{.}\)

We cannot apply Green's theorem in this way for parts (a) and (b) of Q[15.3.1.3] because the singularity at \((0,0)\) is inside the curve \(C\) for both parts (a) and (b). On the other hand suppose, for simplicity, that \(0 \lt a \lt 1\text{.}\) Denote by \(C_a\text{,}\) \(C_b\) the curves of parts (a) and (b), respectively. Define \(R\) to be the set of points that are inside \(C_b\) and outside \(C_a\text{.}\) That is,

\begin{equation*} R=\Set{(x,y)}{-1\le x \le 1,\ -1\le y\le 1,\ x^2+y^2\ge a^2 } \end{equation*}

Then the boundary, \(\partial R\text{,}\) of \(R\) consists of two parts. One part is \(C_b\text{.}\) The other part is \(C_a\text{,}\) but oriented clockwise rather than counterclockwise. We'll call it \(-C_a\text{.}\)

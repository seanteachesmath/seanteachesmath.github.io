If \(x(t),\ y(t)\) and \(z(t)\) are the distances shown, at time \(t\text{,}\) then

\begin{align*} x(t)^2+y(t)^2&=z(t)^2\\ \end{align*}

Differentiating with respect to \(t\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} 2x(t)x'(t)+2y(t)y'(t)&=2z(t)z'(t)\\ x(t)x'(t)+y(t)y'(t)&=z(t)z'(t)\\ \end{align*}

At the specified time, \(x(t)\) is decreasing, so \(x'(t)\) is negative.

\begin{align*} (300)(-15)+(400)(20) &= \sqrt{300^2+400^2}z'(t)\\ 500 z'(t)&=3500\\ z'(t)&=7 \mbox{ mph} \end{align*}

(a) Integrate by parts with \(u=x\) and \(\dee{v}=\sin x\,\dee{x}\) so that \(\dee{u}=\dee{x}\) and \(v=-\cos x\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \int x\sin x\,\dee{x}&= -x\cos x-\int (-\cos x)\,\dee{x} =-x\cos x+\sin x+C \end{align*}

So

\begin{align*} \int_0^{\pi/2} x\sin x\,\dee{x}&=\Big[-x\cos x+\sin x\Big]_0^{\pi/2} =1 \end{align*}

(b) The power of cosine is odd, so we can reserve one cosine for \(\dee{u}\) and change the rest into sines. Make the substitution \(u=\sin x\text{,}\) \(\dee{u}=\cos x\,\dee{x}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \int_0^{\pi/2} \cos^5 x\,\dee{x} &=\int_0^{\pi/2} \big(1-\sin^2x\big)^2\cos x\,\dee{x}\\ \amp=\int_0^1\big(1-u^2\big)^2\,\dee{u} =\int_0^1\big(1-2u^2+u^4\big)\,\dee{u}\cr &=\left[u-\frac{2}{3}u^3+\frac{1}{5}u^5\right]_0^1 =1-\frac{2}{3}+\frac{1}{5} =\frac{8}{15} \end{align*}

Solution 8.6.7.16.1.

Substituting \(y=-3x^3\) into \(\dfrac{1}{1-y} = \sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty y^n\) gives

\begin{gather*} \diff{f}{x} = x\cdot\frac{1}{1+3x^3} =x\sum_{n=0}^\infty {\big(-3x^3\big)}^n =\sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n 3^n x^{3n+1} \end{gather*}

Now integrating,

\begin{gather*} f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n 3^n \frac{x^{3n+2}}{3n+2} +C \end{gather*}

To have \(f(0)=1\text{,}\) we need \(C=1\text{.}\) So, finally

\begin{gather*} f(x) = 1+ \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n \frac{3^n }{3n+2} x^{3n+2} \end{gather*}

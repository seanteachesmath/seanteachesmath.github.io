As was the case in Examples 10.4.9 and 10.4.10, figuring out what the question is asking is half the battle. By Definition 10.5.4, the gradient of a function \(g(x,y)\) is the vector \(\llt g_x(x,y),g_y(x,y)\rgt\text{.}\) In this question we are told that we are given some function \(f(r,\theta)\) of the polar coordinates 3  \(r\) and \(\theta\text{.}\) We are supposed to convert this function to Cartesian coordinates.

In this subsection we fill that gap by describing the general  12  form of partial fraction decompositions. The justification of these forms is not part of the course, but the interested reader is invited to read the next (optional) subsection where such justification is given. In the following it is assumed that

  • \(N(x)\) and \(D(x)\) are polynomials with the degree of \(N(x)\) strictly smaller than the degree of \(D(x)\text{.}\)
  • \(K\) is a constant.
  • \(a_1\text{,}\) \(a_2\text{,}\) \(\cdots\text{,}\) \(a_j\) are all different numbers.
  • \(m_1\text{,}\) \(m_2\text{,}\) \(\cdots\text{,}\) \(m_j\text{,}\) and \(n_1\text{,}\) \(n_2\text{,}\) \(\cdots\text{,}\) \(n_k\) are all strictly positive integers.
  • \(x^2+b_1x+c_1\text{,}\) \(x^2+b_2x+c_2\text{,}\) \(\ \cdots\ \text{,}\) \(x^2+b_kx+c_k\) are all different.

The volume of the pan from part (a) is

\begin{equation*} V(p)=\left(\sqrt{\dfrac{A}{3p}}\right)\left(p\sqrt{\dfrac{A}{3p}}\right) \dfrac{\sqrt{Ap}}{\sqrt{3}(1+p)} =\left(\dfrac{A}{3}\right)^{3/2}\dfrac{\sqrt{p}}{1+p} \end{equation*}

Since

\begin{equation*} \diff{}{p}\left\{\dfrac{\sqrt{p}}{1+p}\right\}=\dfrac{\half (1+p)/\sqrt{p}-\sqrt{p}}{(1+p)^2} =\dfrac{\sqrt{p}\left(\dfrac{1}{p}-1\right)}{2(1+p)^2} \end{equation*}

the volume is increasing with \(p\) for \(p \lt 1\) and decreasing with \(p\) for \(p \gt 1\text{.}\) So the maximum volume is achieved for \(p=1\) (a square base).

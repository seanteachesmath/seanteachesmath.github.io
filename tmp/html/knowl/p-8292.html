Second, we need to antidifferentiate. The substitution \(u=\sin x\text{,}\) \(\dee{u}=\cos x\dee{x}\) fits just right.

\begin{align*} \int_{\pi/2}^\pi \frac{\cos x}{\sqrt{\sin x}}\dee{x}&=\lim_{b \to \pi^-}\int_{\pi/2}^b \frac{\cos x}{\sqrt{\sin x}}\dee{x} = \lim_{b \to \pi^-}\int_{1}^{\sin b} \frac{1}{\sqrt{u}}\dee{u}\\ &=\lim_{b \to \pi^-}\Big[2\sqrt{u}\Big]_{1}^{\sin b} = 2\sqrt{0} - 2\sqrt{1}=-2 \end{align*}

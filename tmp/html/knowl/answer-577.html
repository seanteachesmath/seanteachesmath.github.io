Answer 4.5.4.1.2.1.

The \(x\)-coordinate corresponding to the blue dot (let's call it \(a\)) is a critical point, and \(f(x)\) has a local and global minimum at \(x=a\text{.}\) The \(x\)-coordinate corresponding to the discontinuity (let's call it \(b\)) is a singular point, but there is no a global or local extremum at \(x=b\text{.}\)

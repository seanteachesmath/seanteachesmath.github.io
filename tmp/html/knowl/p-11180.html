(a) Using the Taylor series expansion of \(\sin x\) with \(x=t\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \sin t =\sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\frac{t^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!} &\implies \frac{\sin t}{t} =\sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\frac{t^{2n}}{(2n+1)!} \end{align*}

So

\begin{align*} \Si(x) &=\int_0^x\frac{\sin t}{t}\,\dee{t} =\sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\int_0^x \frac{t^{2n}}{(2n+1)!}\,\dee{t}\\ \amp=\sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\ \frac{x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)(2n+1)!} \end{align*}

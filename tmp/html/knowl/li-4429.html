The distance between the two lines is the length of the projection of \(\vv\) on \(\vn\text{.}\) In this example, by 9.2.14, the distance is
\begin{align*} \big|{\rm proj}_{\vn}\,\vv\big| &=\left|\frac{\vv\cdot\vn}{|\vn|^2}\,\vn\right| =\frac{|\vv\cdot\vn|}{|\vn|}\\ &= \frac{|\llt 0,0,2\rgt\cdot\llt 1,1,1\rgt|}{|\llt 1,1,1\rgt|}\\ &= \frac{2}{\sqrt{3}} \end{align*}
just as we found in Example 9.5.6

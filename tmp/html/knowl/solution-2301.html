Solution 15.2.6.26.1.

Let's try the divergence theorem. Set

\begin{equation*} V = \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2+z^2\le 3} \end{equation*}

Then the boundary of \(V\) is \(S\text{,}\) but with outward pointing normal. Since

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &= \pdiff{}{x} \big(xy^2 + y^4z^6\big) + \pdiff{}{y} \big(yz^2+x^4z\big) + \pdiff{}{z} \big(zx^2+xy^4\big)\\ &=y^2+z^2+x^2 \end{align*}

and because \(S\) is oriented inward, the divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.2) gives

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=-\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} = -\tripInt_V (x^2+y^2+z^2)\,\dee{V} \end{align*}

Switching to spherical coordinates (see Appendix [cross-reference to target(s) "ap_spherCoord" missing])

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=-\int_0^{\sqrt{3}}\dee{\rho}\int_0^\pi\dee{\varphi} \int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta} \ \rho^4\sin\varphi\\ &=-2\pi\left[\int_0^{\sqrt{3}}\dee{\rho}\ \rho^4\right] \left[\int_0^\pi\dee{\varphi}\ \sin\varphi\right]\\ &=-2\pi\left[\frac{\rho^5}{5}\right]_0^{\sqrt{3}} \Big[-\cos\varphi\Big]_0^\pi\\ &=-\frac{36\sqrt{3}}{5}\pi \end{align*}

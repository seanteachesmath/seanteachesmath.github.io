(a) Using the geometric series expansion with \(r=-t^4\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{1-r} =\sum_{n=0}^\infty r^n &\implies\frac{1}{1+t^4} =\sum_{n=0}^\infty {(-t^4)}^n =\sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^nt^{4n} \end{align*}

Substituting this into our integral,

\begin{align*} I(x) &=\int_0^x\frac{1}{1+t^4}\ \dee{t}\\ &=\int_0^x \left( \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n t^{4n}\right)\dee{t}\\ &=\left[\sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\ \frac{t^{4n+1}}{4n+1}\right]_{t=0}^{t=x}\\ &=\sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\ \frac{x^{4n+1}}{4n+1} \end{align*}

Let \(F(x,y,z)=x^2+z^2\) and \(C \gt 0\text{.}\) Consider the level surface \(x^2+z^2=C\text{.}\) The variable \(y\) does not appear in this equation. So for any fixed \(y_0\text{,}\) the intersection of the our surface \(x^2+z^2=C\) with the plane \(y=y_0\) is the circle of radius \(\sqrt{C}\) centred on \(x=z=0\text{.}\) Here is a sketch of the first quadrant part of one such circle.

The full surface is the horizontal stack of all of those circles with \(y_0\) running over \(\bbbr\text{.}\) It is the cylinder of radius \(\sqrt{C}\) centred on the \(y\)-axis.

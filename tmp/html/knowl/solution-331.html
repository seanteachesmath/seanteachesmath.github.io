Solution 3.11.2.8.1.
  • First we find the \(x\)-coordinates where \(y=0\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} x^2e^0+4x\cos(0) &= 5\\ x^2 +4x - 5 &=0\\ (x+5)(x-1)&=0 \end{align*}
    So \(x=1,-5\text{.}\)
  • Now we use implicit differentiation to get \(y'\) in terms of \(x,y\text{.}\) Differentiate both sides of
    \begin{align*} x^2e^y+4x\cos(y) &= 5 \end{align*}
    to get
    \begin{align*} x^2 \cdot e^y \cdot y' + 2x e^y + 4x(-\sin(y)) \cdot y' + 4\cos(y) &= 0 \end{align*}
  • Now set \(y=0\) to get
    \begin{align*} x^2 \cdot e^0 \cdot y' + 2x e^0 + 4x(-\sin(0)) \cdot y' + 4\cos(0) &= 0\\ x^2y' + 2x + 4 &=0\\ y' &= - \frac{4+2x}{x^2}. \end{align*}
  • So at \((x,y)=(1,0)\) we have \(y' = -6\text{,}\)
  • and at \((x,y)=(-5,0)\) we have \(y' = \frac{6}{25}\text{.}\)

(b) (ii) Note that the question did not specify the orientation of \(C\text{.}\) It should have. We'll stick with the most commonly used orientation â€” counterclockwise when viewed from high on the \(z\)-axis. The vector field \(\vG= 3 e^z\,\hk\) is conservative (with potential \(3e^z\)). So \(\oint_C\vG\cdot\dee{\vr}=0\) and, using the parametrization

\begin{align*} \vr(\theta) &= \Big[\frac{1}{2}\!+\!\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cos\theta\Big]\hi +\Big[\frac{1}{2}\! +\!\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sin\theta\Big]\hj +\Big[1 \!+\!\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sin\theta \!+\!\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cos\theta\Big]\hk\\ \vr'(\theta) &= -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sin\theta\,\hi +\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cos\theta\,\hj +\Big[\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cos\theta -\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sin\theta\Big]\hk \end{align*}

of part (a), we have

\begin{align*} &\oint_C\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} = \oint_C(\vF-\vG)\cdot\dee{\vr}\\ &\hskip0.25in= \int_0^{2\pi} \big[y(\theta)^2x'(\theta) +x(\theta)^2y'(\theta)\big] \dee{\theta}\\ &\hskip0.25in= \int_0^{2\pi} \Big\{-\Big[\frac{1}{2} +\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sin\theta\Big]^2 \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\sin\theta +\Big[\frac{1}{2} +\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cos\theta\Big]^2 \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\cos\theta\Big\} \dee{\theta} \end{align*}

Because the integral of any odd power of \(\sin\theta\) or \(\cos\theta\) over \(0\le\theta\le 2\pi\) is zero (see Example 15.4.6 in the text),

\begin{align*} \oint_c\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} &= \int_0^{2\pi} \Big\{-\frac{1}{2} \sin^2\theta +\frac{1}{2} \cos^2\theta\Big\} \dee{\theta}\\ &=0 \end{align*}

since (see Example 13.4.4 in the text)

\begin{equation*} \int_0^{2\pi}\cos^2\theta\ \dee{\theta} =\int_0^{2\pi}\sin^2\theta\ \dee{\theta} =\pi \end{equation*}

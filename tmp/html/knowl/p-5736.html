For simplicity, you may assume that \(a \leq c \leq b\text{,}\) and that \(f(x),g(x)\) give positive values.

  1. \(\displaystyle\int_a^a f(x)\,\dee{x}=0\text{,}\) (Theorem 6.2.3, part (a))
  2. \(\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,\dee{x}= \displaystyle\int_a^c f(x)\,\dee{x} + \int_c^b f(x)\dee{x} \text{,}\) (Theorem 6.2.3, part (c))
  3. \(\displaystyle\int_a^b \left( f(x) + g(x) \right)\,\dee{x} = \displaystyle\int_a^b f(x)\,\dee{x} + \displaystyle\int_a^b g(x)\,\dee{x}\text{,}\) (Theorem 6.2.1, part (a))

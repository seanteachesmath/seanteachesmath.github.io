Solution 2.4.2.49.1.

2.4.2.49.a When we evaluate the limit from the left, we only consider values of \(x\) that are less than zero. For these values of \(x\text{,}\) our function is \(x^2-3\text{.}\) So, \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} f(x)=\lim_{x \rightarrow 0^-} (x^2-3)=-3\text{.}\)

2.4.2.49.b When we evaluate the limit from the right, we only consider values of \(x\) that are greater than zero. For these values of \(x\text{,}\) our function is \(x^2+3\text{.}\) So, \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} f(x)=\lim_{x \rightarrow 0^+} (x^2+3)=3\text{.}\)

2.4.2.49.c Since the limits from the left and right do not agree, \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} f(x)=\)DNE.

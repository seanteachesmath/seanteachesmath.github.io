Solution 15.2.6.24.1.

The vector field \(\vF\) looks pretty complicated. But its divergence

\begin{equation*} \vnabla\cdot\vF = 2 + 3 + 1 = 6 \end{equation*}

is very simple. So let's use the divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.9). It says

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_E \vnabla\cdot\vF\ \dee{V} =\tripInt_E 6\ \dee{V} =6\ \text{Volume}(E) \end{align*}

For any fixed \(0\le X\le 2\text{,}\) the cross-section of \(E\) with \(x=X\) has side view

That cross-section has area \(2\times \frac{2+4}{2}=6\text{.}\) Consequently the volume of \(E\) is \(2\times 6=12\) and

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =6\times 12 =72 \end{equation*}

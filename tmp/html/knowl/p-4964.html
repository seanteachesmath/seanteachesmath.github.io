To prove part (b) we must work around the possibility that \(f'(a)\) and \(g'(a)\) do not exist or that \(f'(x)\) and \(g'(x)\) are not continuous at \(x=a\text{.}\) To do this, we make use of the Generalised Mean-Value Theorem (Theorem 4.4.38) that was used to prove Equation 4.4.33. We recommend you review the GMVT before proceeding.

(a) For large \(n\text{,}\) \(n^2\gg n+1\) and so the numerator \(n^2+n+1\approx n^2\text{.}\) For large \(n\text{,}\) \(n^5\gg n\) and so the denominator \(n^5-n\approx n^5\text{.}\) So, for large \(n\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \frac{n^2+n+1}{n^5-n}\approx \frac{n^2}{n^5} =\frac{1}{n^3}. \end{equation*}

This suggests that we apply the limit comparison test with \(a_n=\frac{n^2+n+1}{n^5-n}\) and \(b_n=\frac{1}{n^3}\text{.}\) Since

\begin{align*} \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{(n^2+n+1)/(n^5-n)}{1/n^3} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{n^5+n^4+n^3}{n^5-n}\\ \amp=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{1+1/n+1/n^2}{1-1/n^4}\\ &=1 \end{align*}

exists and is nonzero, and since \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1}{n^3}\) converges (by the \(p\)-test with \(p=3 \gt 1\)), the given series converges.

(b) For large \(m\text{,}\) \(3m\gg |\sin\sqrt{m}|\) and so

\begin{equation*} \frac{3m+\sin\sqrt{m}}{m^2}\approx \frac{3m}{m^2} =\frac{3}{m}. \end{equation*}

This suggests that we apply the limit comparison test with \(a_m=\frac{3m+\sin\sqrt{m}}{m^2}\) and \(b_m=\frac{1}{m}\text{.}\) (We could also use \(b_m=\frac{3}{m}\text{.}\)) Since

\begin{align*} \lim_{m\rightarrow\infty} \frac{a_m}{b_m} &=\lim_{m\rightarrow\infty} \frac{(3m+\sin\sqrt{m})/m^2}{1/m} =\lim_{m\rightarrow\infty} \frac{3m+\sin\sqrt{m}}{m}\\ \amp=\lim_{m\rightarrow\infty} 3+\frac{\sin\sqrt{m}}{m}\\ &=3 \end{align*}

exists and is nonzero, and since \(\sum\limits_{m=1}^\infty\frac{1}{m}\) diverges (by the \(p\)-test with \(p=1\)), the given series diverges.

\begin{align*} T(t)&=[T(0)-A]e^{Kt}+A\\ &=[500-0]e^{Kt}+0\\ &=500e^{Kt}\\ \end{align*}

for some constant \(K\text{.}\) We are given that \(T(10)=100\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 100=T(10)&=500e^{10K}\\ e^{10K}&=\frac{1}{5}\\ e^K&=5^{-\tfrac{1}{10}}\\ \end{align*}

This gives us the more complete picture for the temperature of the stone.

\begin{align*} T(t)&=500\left(e^K\right)^t=500\cdot 5^{-\tfrac{t}{10}}\\ \end{align*}

If \(T(t)=50:\)

\begin{align*} 50=T(t)&=500\cdot 5^{-\tfrac{t}{10}}\\ \frac{1}{10}=10^{-1}&=5^{-\tfrac{t}{10}}\\ 10&=5^{\tfrac{t}{10}}\\ \log(10)&=\frac{t}{10}\log(5)\\ t&=10\frac{\log(10)}{\log(5)}\approx 14.3 \end{align*}

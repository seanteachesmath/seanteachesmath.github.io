  1. Average velocity:
    \begin{align*} \dfrac{\text{change in position}}{\text{change in time}} \amp= \dfrac{s(9)-s(1)}{9-1} = \dfrac{3-1}{9-1}\\ \amp=\dfrac{1}{4}\text{ units per second}. \end{align*}
  2. From the notes, we know the velocity of an object at time \(a\) is
    \begin{equation*} v(a)=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{s(a+h)-s(a)}{h} \end{equation*}
    So, in our case:
    \begin{align*} v(1)\amp=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{s(1+h)-s(1)}{h} =\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{\sqrt{1+h}-1}{h}\\ &=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{\sqrt{1+h}-1}{h}\cdot \left( \dfrac{\sqrt{1+h}+1}{\sqrt{1+h}+1}\right)\\ &=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{(1+h)-1}{h(\sqrt{1+h}+1)}\\ &=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{1}{\sqrt{1+h}+1}=\frac{1}{2}\mbox{ units per second} \end{align*}
  3. \begin{align*} v(9)\amp=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{s(9+h)-s(9)}{h} =\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{\sqrt{9+h}-3}{h}\\ &=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{\sqrt{9+h}-3}{h}\cdot \left( \dfrac{\sqrt{9+h}+3}{\sqrt{9+h}+3}\right)\\ &=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{(9+h)-9}{h(\sqrt{9+h}+3)}\\ &=\lim_{h \rightarrow 0}\frac{1}{\sqrt{9+h}+3}=\frac{1}{6}\mbox{ units per second} \end{align*}

Remark: the average velocity is not the average of the two instantaneous velocities.

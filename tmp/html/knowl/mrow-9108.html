\begin{align*} \diff{B}{t}&=(0.06+0.02\sin t)B\\ \frac{\dee{B}}{B}&=(0.06+0.02\sin t)\,\dee{t}\\ \int \frac{\dee{B}}{B}&=\int (0.06+0.02\sin t)\,\dee{t}\\ \log |B(t)|&= 0.06 t-0.02\cos t +C'\\ \end{align*}

Since \(B(t)\) is our bank account balance and we're not withdrawing money, \(B(t)\) is positive, so we can drop the absolute value signs.

\begin{align*} \log B(t)&= 0.06 t-0.02\cos t +C'\\ B(t)&= e^{0.06 t-0.02\cos t}e^{C'}\\ B(t)&=Ce^{0.06 t-0.02\cos t} \end{align*}

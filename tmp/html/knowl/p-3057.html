Suppose the quantities \(P\) and \(Q\) are related by the formula \(P=Q^3\text{.}\) \(P\) and \(Q\) are changing with respect to time, \(t\text{.}\) Given this information, which of the following are problems you could solve?

  1. Given \(\ds\diff{P}{t}(0)\text{,}\) find \(\ds\diff{Q}{t}(0)\text{.}\) (Remember: the notation \(\ds\diff{P}{t}(0)\) means the derivative of \(P\) with respect to \(t\) at the time \(t=0\text{.}\))
  2. Given \(\ds\diff{P}{t}(0)\) and the value of \(Q\) when \(t=0\text{,}\) find \(\ds\diff{Q}{t}(0)\text{.}\)
  3. Given \(\ds\diff{Q}{t}(0)\text{,}\) find \(\ds\diff{P}{t}(0)\text{.}\)
  4. Given \(\ds\diff{Q}{t}(0)\) and the value of \(P\) when \(t=0\text{,}\) find \(\ds\diff{P}{t}(0)\text{.}\)

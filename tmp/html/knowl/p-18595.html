(a) False. \(\vr''(t)\) is the full acceleration. So \(|\vr''(t)|\) is the magnitude of the full acceleration, not just the tangential component of acceleration. For example, if \(\vr(t) =\cos t\,\hi+\sin t\,\hj\) (i.e. the particle is just going around in circles), the acceleration \(\vr''(t) = -\cos t\,\hi-\sin t\,\hj\) is perpendicular to the direction of motion. So the tangential component of acceleration is zero, while \(|\vr''(t)|=1\text{.}\)

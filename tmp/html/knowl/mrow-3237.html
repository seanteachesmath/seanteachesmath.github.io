\begin{align*} f(x)&=x(\log x -1)&f(1)&=-1\\ f'(x)&=x\left(\frac{1}{x}\right)+\log x -1 = \log x&f'(1)&=0\\ f''(x)&=\frac{1}{x}=x^{-1}&f''(1)&=1\\ f^{(3)}(x)&=(-1)x^{-2}&f^{(3)}(1)&=-1\\ f^{(4)}(x)&=(-2)(-1)x^{-3}=2!x^{-3}&f^{(4)}(1)&=2!\\ f^{(5)}(x)&=(-3)(-2)(-1)x^{-4}=-3!x^{-4}&f^{(4)}(1)&=-3!\\ f^{(6)}(x)&=(-4)(-3)(-2)(-1)x^{-5}=4!x^{-5}&f^{(4)}(1)&=4!\\ f^{(7)}(x)&=(-5)(-4)(-3)(-2)(-1)x^{-6}=-5!x^{-6}&f^{(7)}(1)&=-5!\\ f^{(8)}(x)&=(-6)(-5)(-4)(-3)(-2)(-1)x^{-7}=6!x^{-7}&f^{(8)}(1)&=6!\\ \end{align*}

When \(k \geq 2\text{,}\) making use of the fact that \(0!=1\) and \((-1)^{k-2}=(-1)^k\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} f^{(k)}(x)&=(-1)^{k-2}(k-2)!x^{-(k-1)}&f^{(k)}(1)&=(-1)^k(k-2)!\\ \end{align*}

Now we use the standard form of a Taylor polynomial. Since the first two terms don't fit the pattern, we add those outside of the sigma.

\begin{align*} T_{100}(x)&=\sum_{k=0}^{100}\frac{f^{(k)}(1)}{k!}(x-1)^k\\ &=f(1)+f'(1)(x-1)+\sum_{k=2}^{100}\frac{f^{(k)}(1)}{k!}(x-1)^k\\ &=-1+0(x-1)+\sum_{k=2}^{100}\frac{(-1)^k(k-2)!}{k!}(x-1)^k\\ &=-1+\sum_{k=2}^{100}\frac{(-1)^k}{k(k-1)}(x-1)^k \end{align*}

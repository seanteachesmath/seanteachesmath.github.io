Item 7.4.7.1.c.
If \(y=x^{3/2}+x\text{,}\) then \(\diff{y}{x} = \frac{3}{2}\sqrt{x}+1\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} \left(\diff{y}{x}\right)^2+\diff{y}{x}&=\left(\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{x}+1\right)^2+\frac{3}{2}\sqrt{x}+1\\ &=\frac{9}{4}x+\frac{9}{2}\sqrt{x}+2\\ &\neq \diff{y}{x} \end{align*}
So, \(y=x^{3/2}+x\) is not a solution to the differential equation \(\left(\diff{y}{x}\right)^2+\diff{y}{x}=y\text{.}\)

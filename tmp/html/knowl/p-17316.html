In Example 13.1.2, we found that the vector field of a point source 1  (in three dimensions) that creates \(4\pi m\) liters per second is

\begin{equation*} \vv(x,y,z) = \frac{m}{r(x,y,z)^2}\,\hat\vr(x,y,z) \end{equation*}

where

\begin{equation*} r(x,y,z) = \sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}\qquad \hat\vr(x,y,z) = \frac{x\hi + y\hj + z\hk}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}} \end{equation*}

We sketched it in Figure 13.1.3. We'll now compute the flux of this vector field across a sphere centred on the origin. Suppose that the sphere has radius \(R\text{.}\)

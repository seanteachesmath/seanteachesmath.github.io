Solution 15.4.3.30.1.

(a) The curl is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi &\hj &\hk\\ \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial y} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial z}\\ 2+x^2+z & 0 & 3+x^2z \end{matrix} \right] =(1-2xz)\,\hj \end{align*}

(b) We are going to use Stokes' theorem. The specified curve \(C\) is not closed and so is not the boundary of a surface. So we extend \(C\) to a closed curve \(\tilde C\) by appending to \(C\) the line segment \(L\) from \((2,0,0)\) to \((0,0,0)\text{.}\) In the figure below, \(C\) is the red curve and \(\tilde C\) is \(C\) plus the blue line segment.

The closed curve \(\tilde C\) is boundary of the surface \(S\) that is the union of

  • the triangle \(T_1\) in the \(yz\)-plane with vertices \((0,0,0)\text{,}\) \((0,0,3)\) and \((0,1,0)\) and with normal vector \(-\hi\) and
  • the triangle \(T_2\) in the \(xy\)-plane with vertices \((0,0,0)\text{,}\) \((0,1,0)\) and \((2,0,0)\) and with normal vector \(-\hk\text{.}\)

So, by Stokes' theorem

\begin{align*} &\int_C \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} + \int_L \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} = \int_{\partial S} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} = \dblInt_S \vnabla\times \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &\hskip0.5in= \dblInt_{T_1} \vnabla\times \vF\cdot(-\hi)\,\dee{S} + \dblInt_{T_2} \vnabla\times \vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &\hskip0.5in= \dblInt_{T_1} (1-2xz)\,\hj\cdot(-\hi)\,\dee{S} + \dblInt_{T_2} (1-2xz)\,\hj\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &\hskip0.5in=0 \end{align*}

Consequently the integral of interest

\begin{align*} \int_C \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} &=- \int_L \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} =- \int_2^0 (2+x^2)\,\dee{x} \quad\text{since } \dee{y}=\dee{z}=z=0 \text{ on } L\\ &=\int_0^2 (2+x^2)\,\dee{x} ={\left[2x+\frac{x^3}{3}\right]}_0^2 =\frac{20}{3} \end{align*}

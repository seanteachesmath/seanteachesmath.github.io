Let \(S'\) be the bottom surface of the cube, oriented with normal \(\hk\text{.}\) Then, by Stokes' theorem, since \(\partial S=\partial S'\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} =\oint_{\partial S}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\oint_{\partial S'}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\dblInt_{S'}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} \end{equation*}

Since

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF=\det\left[\begin{matrix}\hi & \hj & \hk \\ \pdiff{}{x} & \pdiff{}{y} & \pdiff{}{z} \\ xyz & xy^2 & x^2yz \end{matrix}\right] =\big(\cdots\,,\,\cdots\,,\,y^2-xz\big) \end{align*}

and \(\hn=\hk\) on \(S'\) and \(z=-1\) on \(S'\)

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S'}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} &=\int_{-1}^1 \dee{x}\int_{-1}^1 \dee{y}\ \big(\cdots,\cdots,y^2-xz\big)\cdot\hk\Big|_{z=-1}\\ &=\int_{-1}^1 \dee{x}\int_{-1}^1 \dee{y}\ (y^2+x)\\ &=\int_{-1}^1 \dee{x}\int_{-1}^1 \dee{y}\ y^2 =2\times 2\int_0^1\dee{y}\ y^2\\ &=\frac{4}{3} \end{align*}

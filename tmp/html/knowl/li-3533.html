Item 7.4.7.1.a.
If \(y=5(e^x-3x^2-6x-6)\text{,}\) then \(\diff{y}{x} = 5(e^x-6x-6)\text{.}\) Let's see whether this is equal to \(y+15x^2\text{:}\)
\begin{align*} y+15x^2&=5(e^x-3x^2-6x-6)+15x^2\\ &=5(e^x-3x^2-6x-6+3x^2)\\ &=5(e^x-6x-6)\\ &=\diff{y}{x} \end{align*}
So, \(y=5(e^x-3x^2-6x-6)\) is indeed a solution to the differential equation \(\diff{y}{x}=y+15x^2\text{.}\)

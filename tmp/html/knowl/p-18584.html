(i) False. For example, the vector field \(\vF = x\,\hk\) is always parallel to the \(z\)-axis. So its flow lines are also all parallel to the \(z\)-axis. But if the closed curve \(C\) consists of the line segments

  • \(L_1\) from \((0,0,0)\) to \((1,0,0)\text{,}\) followed by
  • \(L_2\) from \((1,0,0)\) to \((1,0,1)\text{,}\) followed by
  • \(L_3\) from \((1,0,1)\) to \((0,0,1)\text{,}\) followed by

  • \(L_4\) from \((0,0,1)\) back to \((0,0,0)\text{.}\)

then

  • \(\int_{L_1} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} = \int_0^1 (x\hk)\cdot\hi\,\dee{x} = 0\) since \(\hk\perp\hi\text{,}\) \(\dee{\vr}=\hi\,\dee{x}\) on \(L_1\) and
  • \(\int_{L_2} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} = \int_0^1 (1\hk)\cdot\hk\,\dee{z} = 1\) since \(x=1\) and \(\dee{\vr}=\hk\,\dee{z}\) on \(L_2\) and
  • \(\int_{L_3} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} = -\int_0^1 (x\hk)\cdot\hi\,\dee{x} = 0\) since \(\hk\perp\hi\) and
  • \(\int_{L_4} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} = -\int_0^1 (0\hk)\cdot\hk\,\dee{z} = 0\) since \(x=0\) on \(L_4\text{.}\)

All together

\begin{equation*} \int_{C} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} =\int_{L_1} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} +\int_{L_2} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} +\int_{L_3} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} +\int_{L_4} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} =1 \end{equation*}

Alternatively, notice that \(e^{-c}\) is a decreasing function of \(c\text{,}\) while for \(0 \lt c\) \(8+c^2\) is an increasing function of \(c\text{.}\) Hence the fraction is a decreasing function of \(c\) and takes its largest value at \(c=0\text{.}\) Hence
\begin{align*} \left|\frac{e^c}{6(8+c^2)}\right| & \leq \frac{1}{6\times 8} = \frac{1}{48} \lt \frac{1}{40}. \end{align*}

So the area of our piece is

\begin{equation*} \dee{S} = \big(a\dee{\varphi}\big)\big(a\sin\varphi\dee{\theta}\big) = a^2\sin\varphi\,\dee{\theta}\dee{\varphi} \end{equation*}

This is exactly the same formula that we found for \(\dee{S}\) in Example 14.3.6 so that we will, yet again, get that the area of a hemisphere of radius \(a\) is \(2\pi a^2\text{.}\) (Phew!)

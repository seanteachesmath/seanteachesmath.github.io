Suppose that we drop a tiny stick into a river 1  with the velocity field of the flowing water being \(\vv(x,y)\text{.}\) We are assuming, for simplicity, that the velocity field does not depend 2  on time \(t\text{.}\) The stick will move along with the water 3 . When the stick is at \(\vr\text{,}\) its velocity will be the same as the velocity of the water at \(\vr\text{,}\) which is \(\vv(\vr)\text{.}\) Thus if the stick is at \(\vr(t)\) at time \(t\text{,}\) we will have

\begin{equation*} \diff{\vr}{t} = \vv\big(\vr(t)\big) \end{equation*}

The stick will trace out a path, parametrized by \(\vr(t)\text{.}\)

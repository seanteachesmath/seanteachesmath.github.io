The slope of \(y=3x^2\) at \(x=a\) is \(6a\text{.}\) The tangent line to \(y=3x^2\) at \(x=a, y=3a^2\) is \(y-3a^2=6a(x-a)\text{.}\) This tangent line passes through \((2,9)\) if

\begin{align*} 9-3a^2&=6a(2-a)\\ 3a^2-12a+9&=0\\ a^2-4a+3&=0\\ (a-3)(a-1)&=0\\ \implies \;\; a&=1,3 \end{align*}

The points are {\((1,3),\ (3,27)\)}.

In the context of Equation 12.7.1, the only forces acting on the particle are gravity, \(-mg\hj\text{,}\) and the normal force, \(W\hN\text{.}\)

We make no such constraints on the force in Example 13.3.3. Certainly \(\vF\) could arise from gravity and the normal force of a track, but there's nothing saying it has to. For example, suppose \(\varphi\) is an equation that does not depend on \(m\) and/or \(g\text{.}\) Alternately, suppose the \(y\)-coordinate of our three-dimensional system is not “up.”

As a check, we compute:

\begin{align*} x\diff{y}{x}+y&=x\diff{}{x}\left\{\frac{1}{1-2x}\right\}+y\\ &=x\frac{2}{(1-2x)^2}+\frac{1}{1-2x}\\ &=\frac{2x+(1-2x)}{(1-2x)^2}\\ &=\frac{1}{(1-2x)^2}\\ &=y^2\\ \end{align*}

So, our differential equation is satisfied. Furthermore:

\begin{align*} y(1)&=\frac{1}{1-2\times 1}=-1 \end{align*}

as desired. This confirms that our solution is correct.

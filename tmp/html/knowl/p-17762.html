(a) Since

\begin{align*} \pdiff{}{x}\frac{x}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} -\frac{3}{2}\frac{x(2x)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ &=\frac{-2x^2+y^2+z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ \pdiff{}{y}\frac{y}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} -\frac{3}{2}\frac{y(2y)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ &=\frac{x^2-2y^2+z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ \pdiff{}{z}\frac{z}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} -\frac{3}{2}\frac{z(2z)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ &=\frac{x^2+y^2-2z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}} \end{align*}

the specified divergence is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &= \frac{(-2x^2+y^2+z^2) + (x^2-2y^2+z^2) + (x^2+y^2-2z^2)} {{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}} =0 \end{align*}

if \((x,y,z)\ne\vZero\) and is not defined if \((x,y,z)=\vZero\text{.}\)

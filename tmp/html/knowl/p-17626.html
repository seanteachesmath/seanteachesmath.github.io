(a) The divergence of \(\phi\,\va\) is \(\vnabla\cdot(\phi\,\va) =\vnabla\phi\cdot\va+\phi\vnabla\cdot\va=\vnabla\phi\cdot\va\text{,}\) since \(\va\) is constant. So, by the divergence theorem,

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{\partial V}\phi\,\va\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V\vnabla\cdot(\phi\,\va)\ \dee{V} =\tripInt_V \vnabla\phi\cdot\va\ \dee{V}\\ &\implies \left[\dblInt_{\partial V}\phi\hn\,\dee{S} -\tripInt_V \vnabla\phi\,\dee{V}\right]\cdot\va=0 \end{align*}

This is true for all vectors \(\va\text{.}\) In particular, applying this with \(\va=\hi,\hj,\hk\text{,}\) we have that all three components of \(\left[\dblInt_{\partial V}\phi\hn\,\dee{S} -\tripInt_V \vnabla\phi\,\dee{V}\right]\) are zero. So

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_{\partial V}\phi\,\hn\,\dee{S}-\tripInt_V \vnabla\phi\,\dee{V}=0 \end{equation*}

Theorem 6.4.2, the substitution rule for indefinite integrals, tells us that if \(F(u)\) is any antiderivative for \(f(u)\text{,}\) then \(F\big(u(x)\big)\) is an antiderivative for \(f\big(u(x)\big) u'(x)\text{.}\) So the fundamental theorem of calculus gives us

\begin{align*} \int_a^b f\big(u(x)\big) u'(x)\,\dee{x} &= F\big(u(x)\big)\bigg|_{x=a}^{x=b}\\ &= F\big(u(b)\big) - F\big(u(a)\big)\\ &= \int_{u(a)}^{u(b)} f(u)\,\dee{u} & \text{since $F(u)$ is an antiderivative for $f(u)$} \end{align*}

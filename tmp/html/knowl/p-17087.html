So we approximated

\begin{align*} \big|\overrightarrow{P_0P_1}\times\overrightarrow{P_0P_2}\big| &=\Big|\Big[\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial u}(u_0\,,\,v_0)\,\dee{u} + E_1\Big] \times\Big[\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial v}(u_0\,,\,v_0)\,\dee{v} + E_2\Big] \Big|\\ &=\Big|\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial u}(u_0\,,\,v_0)\,\dee{u} \times\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial v}(u_0\,,\,v_0)\,\dee{v} + E_3 \Big|\\ &\approx \Big|\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial u}(u_0\,,\,v_0)\,\dee{u} \times\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial v}(u_0\,,\,v_0)\,\dee{v} \Big| \end{align*}

where the length of the vector \(E_3\) is bounded by a constant times \(\dee{u}^2\,\dee{v}+\dee{u}\,\dee{v}^2\text{.}\) We'll now see why dropping terms like \(E_3\) does not change the value of the integral at all 15 .

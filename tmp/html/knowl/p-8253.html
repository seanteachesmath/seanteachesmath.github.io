(a) We substitute \(y=\sin(2x)\text{,}\) \(\dee{y}=2\cos(2x)\ \dee{x}\text{.}\) Note \(\sin(2\cdot 0)=0\) and \(\sin(2\cdot\frac{\pi}{4}) = 1.\)

\begin{equation*} \int_0^{\pi/4}\sin^5(2x)\,\cos(2x)\ \dee{x} =\int_{0}^{1} y^5\,\frac{\dee{y}}{2} =\frac{1}{12}\bigg[y^6\bigg]_0^1 =\frac{1}{12} \end{equation*}

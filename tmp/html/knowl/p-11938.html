The right hand side of the parametric equation \((x,y,z)=(1,1,0)+t\llt 1,2,-2\rgt\) that we just saw in Warning 9.5.3 is a vector-valued function of the one real variable \(t\text{.}\) We are now going to study more general vector-valued functions of one real variable. That is, we are going to study functions that assign to each real number \(t\) (typically in some interval) a vector \(\vr(t)\text{.}\) For example

\begin{equation*} \vr(t) = \big( x(t), y(t), z(t)\big) \end{equation*}

might be the position 1  of a particle at time \(t\text{.}\) As \(t\) varies \(\vr(t)\) sweeps out a curve.

Here is a sketch of the part of \(S\) that is in the first octant.

For each fixed \(y\text{,}\) \(x^2+z^2=\sin^2y\) is a circle of radius \(\sin y\text{.}\) (It's the blue circle in the sketch above.) So we may parametrize the surface by

\begin{gather*} \vr(\theta,y) =\big(\sin y\,\cos\theta\,,\, y \,,\, \sin y\,\sin\theta\big)\qquad 0\le\theta \lt 2\pi,\ 0\le y\le \pi \end{gather*}

Then, by (14.3.1),

\begin{align*} \frac{\partial \vr}{\partial\theta} &= \big(-\sin y\,\sin\theta\,,\, 0 \,,\, \sin y\,\cos\theta\big)\\ \frac{\partial \vr}{\partial y} &= \big(\cos y\,\cos\theta\,,\, 1 \,,\, \cos y\,\sin\theta\big)\\ \frac{\partial \vr}{\partial\theta} \times \frac{\partial \vr}{\partial y} &=\big(-\sin y\,\cos\theta\,,\, \sin y\,\cos y\,,\,-\sin y\,\sin\theta \big)\\ \dee{S} &= \left|\frac{\partial \vr}{\partial\theta} \times \frac{\partial \vr}{\partial y}\right|\dee{\theta}\,\dee{y} =\sin y\sqrt{1+\cos^2 y}\ \dee{\theta}\ \dee{y} \end{align*}

So the specified integral is

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S\sqrt{1 + \cos^2y}\, \dee{S} &= \int_0^\pi\dee{y} \int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ \sin y\big\{1+\cos^2 y\big\}\\ &= 2\pi \int_0^\pi\dee{y}\ \sin y\big\{1+\cos^2 y\big\}\\ &= - 2\pi \int_1^{-1} \dee{u}\ \big\{1+u^2\big\}\\ &\hskip1in\text{with }u=\cos y,\ \dee{u}=-\sin y\,\dee{y}\\ &= 4\pi \int_0^1 \dee{u}\ \big\{1+u^2\big\}\\ &=4\pi\left[u+\frac{u^3}{3}\right]_0^1\\ &= \frac{16}{3}\pi \end{align*}

(a) By the divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.2), the outward flux of \(\vF\) through the boundary of \(E\) is

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{\partial E}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_E \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V}\\ &=\tripInt_E \big(-x^2-y^2+4\big)\,\dee{V} \end{align*}

To evaluate this integral we switch to cylindrical coordinates. In cylindrical coordinates

\begin{equation*} E=\Set{(r\cos\theta\,,\,r\sin\theta\,,\,z)}{0\le z\le 4,\ r^2\le z} \end{equation*}

So

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{\partial E}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\int_0^4\dee{z}\int_0^{\sqrt{z}}\dee{r}\,r\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ \big(-r^2+4\big)\\ &=2\pi \int_0^4\dee{z}\int_0^{\sqrt{z}}\dee{r}\ \big(4r-r^3\big)\\ &=2\pi \int_0^4\dee{z}\ \Big(2z-\frac{z^2}{4}\Big)\\ &= 2\pi\Big[z^2-\frac{z^3}{12}\Big]_0^4 =2\pi\Big[16-\frac{16}{3}\Big] =\frac{64}{3}\pi \end{align*}

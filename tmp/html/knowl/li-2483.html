This leaves the case \(n\) odd and \(m\) even. There are strategies like those above for treating this case. But they are more complicated and also involve more tricks (that basically have to be memorized). Examples using them are provided in the optional section entitled “Integrating \(\sec x\text{,}\) \(\csc x\text{,}\) \(\sec^3 x\) and \(\csc^3 x\)”, below. A more straight forward strategy uses another technique called “partial fractions”. We shall return to this strategy after we have learned about partial fractions. See Example 6.10.5 and 6.10.6 in Section 6.10.

(a) The surface is \(G(x,y,z)=z-x^2+2xy-y^2=0\text{.}\) When \(x=a\) and \(y=2a\) and \((x,y,z)\) is on the surface, we have \(z= a^2-2(a)(2a) +(2a)^2=a^2\text{.}\) So, by part (c) of Theorem 14.2.1, a normal vector to this surface at \((a,2a,a^2)\) is

\begin{gather*} \vnabla G(a,2a,a^2) = ( -2x+2y\,,\,2x-2y\,,\,1)\Big|_{(x,y,z)=(a,2a,a^2)} = ( 2a\,,\,-2a\,,\,1) \end{gather*}

and the equation of the tangent plane is

\begin{gather*} ( 2a\,,\,-2a\,,\,1)\cdot( x-a\,,\,y-2a\,,\,z-a^2) =0 \quad\text{or}\quad 2ax -2ay +z = -a^2 \end{gather*}

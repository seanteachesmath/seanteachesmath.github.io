We can use \(x\) and \(y\) as parameters. As we can rewrite the equation of the plane as \(z = \frac{1}{3}\big(16-2x-4y\big)\text{,}\) we have the parametrization

\begin{equation*} \vr(x,y) = x\,\hi +y\,\hj +\frac{1}{3}\big(16-2x-4y\big)\,\hk \end{equation*}

In terms of \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) the condition \(z= \frac{1}{3}\big(16-2x-4y\big)\ge 0\) is \(16-2x-4y\ge 0\) or \(x+2y\le 8\text{.}\) So the domain is

\begin{equation*} \Set{(x,y)}{x\ge 0,\ y\ge 0,\ x+2y\le 8} \end{equation*}

Renaming \(x\) to \(u\) and \(y\) to \(v\text{,}\) the parametrization is also

\begin{equation*} \vr(u,v) = \Big(u\,,\, v\,,\, \frac{1}{3}(16-2u-4v)\Big)\,\hk\qquad u\ge 0,\ v\ge 0,\ u+2v\le 8 \end{equation*}

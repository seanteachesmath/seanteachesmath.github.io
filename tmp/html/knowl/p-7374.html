The method of partial fractions is not just confined to the problem of integrating rational functions. There are other integrals — such as \(\int\sec x\dee{x}\) and \(\int \sec^3 x\dee{x}\) — that can be transformed (via substitutions) into integrals of rational functions. We encountered both of these integrals in Sections 6.8 and 6.9 on trigonometric integrals and substitutions.

To find the tangent plane we have to find a normal vector to the surface at \((2,2,0)\text{.}\) Since

\begin{align*} \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial u} &=\big(1\,,\, 2u\,,\, 1 \big)\\ \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial v} &=\big(1\,,\, 2v\,,\, -1 \big) \end{align*}

a normal vector to the surface at \(\vr(u,v)\) is

\begin{align*} \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial u} \times \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial v} &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi & \hj & \hk\\ 1 & 2u & 1\\ 1 & 2v & -1 \end{matrix} \right]\\ &=\big(-2u-2v\,,\, 2\,,\, 2v-2u\big) \end{align*}

As \(\vr(u,v) = (2,2,0)\) when (the \(x\)-coordinate) \(u+v=2\) and (the \(z\)-coordinate) \(u-v=0\text{,}\) i.e when \(u=v=1\text{,}\) a normal vector to the surface at \((2,2,0)=\vr(1,1)\) is

\begin{gather*} (-4,2,0)\qquad\text{or}\qquad (-2,1,0) \end{gather*}

and the equation of the specified tangent plane is

\begin{gather*} -2(x-2) +(y-2) +0z= 0\qquad\text{or} \qquad y=2x-2 \end{gather*}

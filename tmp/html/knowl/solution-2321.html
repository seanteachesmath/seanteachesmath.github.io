Solution 15.2.6.46.1.

Let \(S_a\) denote the sphere \(x^2+y^2+z^2=a^2\) and \(V_a\) denote the solid inside it, which is the ball \(x^2+y^2+z^2\le a^2\text{.}\) Then, by the divergence theorem, Theorem 15.2.2,

\begin{equation*} \pi(a^3+2a^4) =\dblInt_{S_a}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\tripInt_{V_a}\vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} \end{equation*}

Now, for very small \(a\text{,}\) \(\vnabla\cdot\vF\) is almost equal to \(\vnabla\cdot\vF(0,0,0)\) on all of \(V_a\text{,}\) and the integral \(\tripInt_{V_a}\vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V}\) will be

\begin{equation*} \vnabla\cdot\vF(0,0,0){\rm Volume}(V_a)+O(a^4) =\frac{4}{3}\pi a^3\vnabla\cdot\vF(0,0,0)+O(a^4) \end{equation*}

Here \(O(a^4)\) is an error term that is bounded by a constant times \(a^4\text{.}\) This is consistent with the above equation if and only if \(\vnabla\cdot\vF(0,0,0)=\frac{3}{4}\text{.}\)

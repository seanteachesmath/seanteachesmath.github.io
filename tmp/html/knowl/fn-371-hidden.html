We are hiding some mathematics behind this “consequently”. What we are really using our knowledge of Taylor polynomials to write
\begin{equation*} f(x) = \sin(x) = x-\frac{1}{3!}x^3+\frac{1}{5!}x^5 + E_5(x) \end{equation*}
where \(E_5(x) = \frac{f^{(6)}(c)}{6!} x^6\) and \(c\) is between 0 and \(x\text{.}\) We are effectively hiding “\(E_5(x)\)” inside the “\(\cdots\)”. Now we can divide both sides by \(x\) (assuming \(x \neq 0\)):
\begin{align*} \frac{\sin(x)}{x} &= 1-\frac{1}{3!}x^2+\frac{1}{5!}x^4 + \frac{E_5(x)}{x}. \end{align*}
and everything is fine provided the term \(\frac{E_5(x)}{x}\) stays well behaved.

In the very special (but not that uncommon) case that \(\cR\) is the rectangle

\begin{equation*} \cR=\Set{(x,y)}{a\le x\le b,\ c\le y\le d} \end{equation*}

and the integrand is the product \(f(x,y)=g(x)h(y)\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \dblInt_\cR f(x,y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} &=\int_a^b\dee{x} \int_c^d\dee{y}\ g(x) h(y)\\ &=\int_a^b\dee{x}\ g(x) \int_c^d\dee{y}\ h(y)\\ &\hskip0.25in\text{since $g(x)$ is a constant as far as the $y$-integral is concerned}\\ &=\left[ \int_a^b\dee{x}\ g(x)\right]\ \left[\int_c^d\dee{y}\ h(y)\right] \end{align*}

since \(\int_c^d\dee{y}\ h(y)\) is a constant as far as the $x$-integral is concerned.

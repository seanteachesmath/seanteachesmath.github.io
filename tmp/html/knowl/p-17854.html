(c) To start, consider any closed surface \(S\) that is the boundary of a solid \(V\text{.}\) Use

  • the outward pointing normal for \(S\text{,}\)
  • \(|V|\) to denote the volume of \(V\text{,}\) and
  • \(\bar z=\frac{1}{|V|}\tripInt_V z\ \dee{V}\) to denote the \(z\)-component of the centroid (i.e. centre of mass with constant density) of \(V\text{.}\)

Then, by the divergence theorem

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} =\tripInt_V (2+2z)\,\dee{V}\\ &=2\tripInt_V \,\dee{V} +2\tripInt_V z\,\dee{V}\\ &=2|V| +2|V|\bar z \end{align*}

This takes the value \(-9\) if and only if

\begin{gather*} 2|V|\bar z =-9-2|V| \iff \bar z = -\frac{9}{2|V|}-1 \end{gather*}

One surface which obeys this condition is the unit cube (with outward normal) centred on \(\big(0,0, -\frac{11}{2}\big)\text{.}\)

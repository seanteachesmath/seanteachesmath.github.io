By Green's theorem (Theorem 15.3.2), using \(D\) to denote the half-disk \(0\le y\le \sqrt{4-x^2}\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} &\ds\oint_{\cC} \Big(\frac{1}{3}x^2y^3-x^4y\Big)\,\dee{x} +\big(xy^4+x^3y^2\big)\,\dee{y}\\ &\hskip1in =\dblInt_D\Big[\pdiff{}{x}\big(xy^4+x^3y^2\big) -\pdiff{}{y}\Big(\frac{1}{3}x^2y^3-x^4y\Big) \Big] \dee{x} \dee{y}\\ &\hskip1in=\dblInt_D\big(x^4+2x^2y^2+y^4\big)\ \dee{x} \dee{y}\\ &=\dblInt_D\big(x^2+y^2\big)^2\ \dee{x} \dee{y} \end{align*}

Switching to polar coordinates

\begin{align*} \ds\oint_{\cC} \Big(\frac{1}{3}x^2y^3-x^4y\Big)\,\dee{x} +\big(xy^4+x^3y^2\big)\,\dee{y} &=\int_0^2 dr\ r\int_0^{\pi}\dee{\theta}\ r^4 ={\pi\frac{r^6}{6}\bigg|}_0^2\\ &=\frac{32}{3}\pi \end{align*}

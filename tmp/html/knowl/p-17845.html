(c) using the divergence theorem: Note that if \(x^2+y^2\le 9\text{,}\) then \(|x|\le 3\) and \(y\le 3\) so that \(|xy|\le 9 \lt 10\text{.}\) Set

\begin{align*} \tilde S&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{ x^2+y^2 = 9,\ xy\le z\le 10}\\ V&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{ x^2+y^2\le 9,\ xy\le z\le 10} \end{align*}

Note that the boundary, \(\partial V\text{,}\) of \(V\) consists of three parts:

  • the side \(\tilde S\text{,}\) with outward pointing normal (which is the surface and the normal specified in part (c) of the question)
  • the bottom, which is the surface \(S\) of part (b), with downward pointing normal (which is opposite the normal specified in part (b)) and
  • the top, which is the surface \(S_T=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2\le 9,\ z=10}\text{,}\) with normal \(\hn=\hk\text{.}\)

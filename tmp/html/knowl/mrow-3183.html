\begin{align*} f(x)&=\frac{1}{1-x}=(1-x)^{-1}\\ \end{align*}

Using the chain rule,

\begin{align*} f'(x)&=(-1)(1-x)^{-2}(-1)=(1-x)^{-2}\\ f''(x)&=(-2)(1-x)^{-3}(-1)=2(1-x)^{-3}\\ f^{(3)}(x)&=(-3)(2)(1-x)^{-4}(-1)=2(3)(1-x)^{-4}\\ f^{(4)}(x)&=(-4)(2)(3)(1-x)^{-5}(-1)=2(3)(4)(1-x)^{-5}\\ f^{(5)}(x)&=(-5)(2)(3)(4)(1-x)^{-6}(-1)=2(3)(4)(5)(1-x)^{-6}\\ \end{align*}

Recognizing the pattern,

\begin{align*} f^{(k)}(x)&=k!(1-x)^{-(k+1)}\\ f^{(k)}(0)&=k!(1)^{-(k+1)}=k!\\ \end{align*}

The \(n\)th degree Maclaurin polynomial for \(f(x)\) is

\begin{align*} T_n(x)&=\sum_{k=0}^n \frac{f^{(k)}(0)}{k!}x^k\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^n \frac{k!}{k!}x^k\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^n x^k \end{align*}

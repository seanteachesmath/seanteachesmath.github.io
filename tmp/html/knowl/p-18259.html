Let \(S\) be the portion of the paraboloid \(z=f(x,y)=4-x^2-y^2\) with \(x^2+(y-1)^2\le 1\) and let \(\hn\) be the upward normal to \(S\text{.}\) For this surface

\begin{equation*} \hn\,\dee{S}=\big(-f_x(x,y)\,\hi -f_y(x,y)\,\hj +\hk\big)\dee{x}\dee{y} = \big(2x\,\hi+2y\,\hj+\hk\big)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{equation*}

by (14.3.2). As \((x,y,z)\) runs over \(S\text{,}\) \((x,y)\) runs over the circular disk

\begin{equation*} D = \Set{(x,y)}{x^2+(y-1)^2\le 1} \end{equation*}

For the given vector field

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi &\hj &\hk\\ \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial y} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial z}\\ xz & x & yz \end{matrix} \right]\\ &=z\,\hi+x\,\hj+\hk \end{align*}

so that, by Stokes' theorem (Theorem 15.4.1),

\begin{align*} \oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\dblInt_S \vnabla\times\vF\cdot \hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_D \big[2x\overbrace{(4-x^2-y^2)}^{z=f(x,y)}+2xy+1\big]\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

By oddness under \(x\rightarrow -x\text{,}\) all terms integrate to zero except for the last. So

\begin{equation*} \oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\dblInt_D\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =\text{Area}(D) =\pi \end{equation*}

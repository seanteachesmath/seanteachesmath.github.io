The curve is a circle of radius 3, centred at the origin. So, the “circle of best fit” is just the curve itself. \(\hT\) is the unit vector tangent to the circle in direction of increasing \(t\text{,}\) and \(\hN\) is the unit vector pointing towards the origin.

The radius of the (osculating) circle is 3, so \(\rho=3\) and \(\ka=\frac1\rho=\frac13\text{.}\)

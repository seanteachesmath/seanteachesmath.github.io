By the divergence test, the fact that \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^\infty\frac{na_n-2n+1}{n+1}\) converges guarantees that \(\lim\limits_{n\rightarrow\infty}\dfrac{na_n-2n+1}{n+1}=0\text{,}\) or equivalently, that

\begin{gather*} 0=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{n}{n+1}a_n -\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{2n-1}{n+1} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}a_n -2 \iff \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}a_n = 2 \end{gather*}

The series of interest can be written \(-\log a_1 + \sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty \big[\log(a_n)-\log(a_{n+1})\big]\) which looks like a telescoping series. So we'll compute the partial sum

\begin{align*} S_N&=-\log a_1 + \sum_{n=1}^N \big[\log(a_n)-\log(a_{n+1})\big]\\ &=-\log a_1 + \big[\log(a_1)-\log(a_2)\big] + \big[\log(a_2)-\log(a_3)\big] + \cdots\\ \amp\hskip1in + \big[\log(a_N)-\log(a_{N+1})\big]\\ &=-\log(a_{N+1}) \end{align*}

and then take the limit \(N\rightarrow\infty\)

\begin{align*} -\log a_1 + \sum_{n=1}^\infty \big[\log(a_n)-\log(a_{n+1})\big] \amp=\lim_{N\rightarrow\infty} S_N =- \lim_{N\rightarrow\infty} \log(a_{N+1})\\ \amp=-\log 2 =\log\frac{1}{2} \end{align*}

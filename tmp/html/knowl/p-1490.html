Let \(f(t)=t^3-4t^2+1\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} f'(t)&=3t^2-8t & f'(2)&=3\times 4-8\times 2=-4\cr f''(t)&=6t-8 & f''(2)&=6\times 2-8=4\cr \end{align*}

Hence at \(t=2\text{,}\) 3.4.2.9.a the particle has speed of magnitude {4}, and 3.4.2.9.b is moving {towards the left}. At \(t=2\text{,}\) \(f''(2) \gt 0\text{,}\) so \(f'\) is increasing, i.e. becoming less negative. Since \(f'\) is getting closer to zero, 3.4.2.9.c the magnitude of the speed is {decreasing}.

At time \(t=0\) a particle has position and velocity vectors \(\vr(0)=\llt -1,0,0\rgt\) and \(\vv(0)=\llt 0,-1,1\rgt\text{.}\) At time \(t\text{,}\) the particle has acceleration vector

\begin{equation*} \va(t)=\llt \cos t,\sin t,0\rgt \end{equation*}
  1. Find the position of the particle after \(t\) seconds.
  2. Show that the velocity and acceleration of the particle are always perpendicular for every \(t\text{.}\)
  3. Find the equation of the tangent line to the particle's path at \(t=-\pi/2\text{.}\)
  4. True or False: None of the lines tangent to the path of the particle pass through \((0,0,0)\text{.}\) Justify your answer.

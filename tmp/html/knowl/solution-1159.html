Solution 7.3.3.7.1.
  1. A thin slice of \(S\) at position \(x\) has height \(\frac{1}{x}\text{,}\) so if its width is \(\dee{x}\text{,}\) its area is \(\frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}\text{.}\)
  2. A small piece of \(R\) at position \(x\) has density \(\frac{1}{x}\text{,}\) so if its length is \(\dee{x}\text{,}\) its mass is \(\frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}\text{.}\)
  3. Adding up all our tiny slices from (a) gives us the total area of \(S\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} \int_1^3 \frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x} = \log 3 \end{equation*}
  4. Adding up all our tiny pieces from (b) gives us the total mass of \(R\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} \int_1^3 \frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}=\log 3 \end{equation*}
  5. Using Equation 7.3.5, the \(x\)-coordinate of the centroid of \(S\) is
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\int_1^3 x\cdot\frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}}{\int_1^3\frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}} = \frac{\int_1^3 1\,\dee{x}}{\log 3} = \frac{2}{\log 3} \end{equation*}
  6. Using Equation 7.3.4, the centre of mass of \(R\) is
    \begin{equation*} \frac{\int_1^3 x\cdot\frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}}{\int_1^3\frac{1}{x}\,\dee{x}} = \frac{\int_1^3 1\,\dee{x}}{\log 3} = \frac{2}{\log 3} \end{equation*}

Remark: following the derivation of Equation 7.3.5, if we wanted to find the \(x\)-coordinate of the centroid of \(S\text{,}\) we would set up a rod that had exactly the characteristics of \(R\text{.}\) That's why all the answers were repeated.

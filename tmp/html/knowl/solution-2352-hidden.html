Observe that

\begin{align*} \frac{\partial F_2}{\partial x} -\frac{\partial F_1}{\partial y} &=\frac{\partial }{\partial x}\Big( \frac{x}{x^2+y^2}\Big) -\frac{\partial }{\partial y}\Big( \frac{-y}{x^2+y^2}\Big)\\ &=\frac{(x^2+y^2)-x(2x)}{{(x^2+y^2)}^2} +\frac{(x^2+y^2)-y(2y)}{{(x^2+y^2)}^2}=0 \end{align*}

except at \((0,0)\text{,}\) where \(\vF\) is not defined. Hence by Green's theorem (Theorem 15.3.2), \(\oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}=0\) for any closed curve that does not contain \((0,0)\) in its interior. In particular, \(\oint_{\cC_1}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}=0\text{.}\) On the other hand, \((0,0)\) is contained in the interior of \(\cC_2\text{,}\) so we cannot use Green's theorem to conclude that \(\oint_{\cC_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}=0\text{.}\)

Let \(\cC_3\) be the circle of radius one centred on \((0,0)\) and denote by \(W\) the washer shaped region between the circle \(\cC_2\) and the circle \(\cC_3\text{.}\) It is sketched below.

By Green's theorem (Theorem 15.3.2),

\begin{gather*} \oint_{\cC_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}-\oint_{\cC_3}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\dblInt_W \Big[\pdiff{}{x}\vF_2 -\pdiff{}{y}\vF_1\Big]\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =0 \end{gather*}

So \(\oint_{\cC_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}=\oint_{\cC_3}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}\text{.}\) Parameterize \(\cC_3\) by \(x=\cos\theta\text{,}\) \(y=\sin\theta\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \vr(\theta)&=\cos\theta\,\hi+\sin\theta\,\hj\qquad 0\le \theta\le 2\pi\\ \vr'(\theta)&=-\sin\theta\,\hi+\cos\theta\,\hj\\ \vF\big(\vr(\theta)\big)&=-\sin\theta\,\hi+\cos\theta\,\hj\\ \vF\big(\vr(\theta)\big)\cdot\vr'(\theta) &=1 \end{align*}

so that

\begin{equation*} \oint_{\cC_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\oint_{\cC_3}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\int_0^{2\pi} \dee{\theta}\ 1 =2\pi \end{equation*}

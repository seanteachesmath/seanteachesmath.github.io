(b)

  • The radius of the circle we want is \(\rho=\frac{1}{\ka}=2^{3/2}\text{.}\) If its centre is at \((a,b)\text{,}\) then the circle will have equation \((x-a)^2+(y-b)^2=2^3\text{.}\) So, we will find its centre.
  • The unit vector \(\hN\) points from our point \((0,1)\) towards the centre of the circle. Since the radius of the circle is \(2^{3/2}\text{,}\) the centre of the circle will be at \((0,1)+2^{3/2}\hN\text{.}\) So, we'll find \(\hN\text{.}\)
  • Since \(\hN\) is a unit vector perpendicular to \(\hT=\dfrac{\hi+\hj}{\sqrt 2}\text{,}\) we know \(\hN\) will be either \(\dfrac{\hi-\hj}{\sqrt 2}\) or \(\dfrac{-\hi+\hj}{\sqrt 2}\text{.}\)
  • Using Part (d) of the proof of Theorem 12.3.3:
    \begin{align*} \vv(t) \times \va(t) &= \ka\left(\diff{s}{t}\right)^3\hT \times \hN\\ (\hi+\hj)\times(\hj)&=2^{-3/2}\left(\sqrt2 \right)^3\frac{\hi+\hj}{\sqrt 2}\times\hN\\ \hk &=\frac{1}{\sqrt2}(\hi + \hj)\times\hN\\ \hN&=\frac{-\hi+\hj}{\sqrt2} \end{align*}
    So, the centre of our circle is at point \((0,1)+\rho\hN=(0,1)+2^{3/2}\frac{-\hi+\hj}{2^{1/2}}=(-2,3)\text{.}\) Then the equation of the circle is \((x+2)^2+(y-3)^2=8\text{.}\)

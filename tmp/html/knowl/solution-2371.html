Solution 15.4.3.7.1.

The boundary of \(\cS\) is the circle \(x^2+y^2=4\text{,}\) \(z=0\text{.}\) Let \(\cC\) be this circle, oriented by the parametrization \(x(t)=2\cos t\text{,}\) \(y(t)=2\sin t\text{,}\) \(z(t)=0\text{.}\) By Stokes' theorem

\begin{align*} &\dblInt_{\cS}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\int_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\int_0^{2\pi}\vF(2\cos t,2\sin t,0)\cdot \diff{\vr}{t}(t)\ \dee{t}\\ &\hskip0.25in=\int_0^{2\pi}\big[0\,\hi+2\cos t(3+2\sin t)\,\hj+2\sin t\,\hk\big]\cdot \big[-2\sin t\,\hi+2\cos t\hj\big]\ \dee{t}\\ &\hskip0.25in=\int_0^{2\pi}\big[12\cos^2 t+8\cos^2t\sin t\big]\ \dee{t}\\ &\hskip0.25in=\int_0^{2\pi}\big[6+6\cos(2t)+8\cos^2t\sin t\big]\ \dee{t}\\ &\hskip0.25in=\Big[6t+3\sin(2t) -\frac{8}{3}\cos^3 t\Big]_0^{2\pi} =12\pi \end{align*}

For an efficient, sneaky, way to evaluate \(\int_0^{2\pi} \cos^2 t\,\dee{t}\text{,}\) see Example 13.4.4.

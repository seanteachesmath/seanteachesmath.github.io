Set \(S=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+z^2=4,\ 0\le y\le 3}\text{.}\) We are to compute \(\dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\text{,}\) with \(\hn\) denoting the outward normal to \(S\text{.}\) \(S\) is not the boundary of a solid, so we cannot compute \(\dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\) by applying the divergence theorem directly.

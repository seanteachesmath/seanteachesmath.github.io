The volume of the ellipsoid \(V\) can also be found by observing that, in \(V\text{,}\)

  • \(x\) runs from \(-1\) to \(1\) and
  • for each fixed \(-1\le x\le 1\text{,}\) \((y,z)\) runs over the disk \(y^2+z^2\le 4(1-x^2)\text{,}\) which has area \(4\pi (1-x^2)\text{.}\)

That is

\begin{equation*} V=\Set{(x,y,z)} {-1\le x\le 1,\ y^2+z^2 \le 4(1-x^2)} \end{equation*}

so that

\begin{align*} \text{Volume}(V) &=\int_{-1}^1 \dee{x} \dblInt_{y^2+z^2 \le 4(1-x^2)}\dee{y}\,\dee{z}\\ &= \int_{-1}^1 \dee{x}\ 4\pi(1-x^2) =2\times 4\pi \int_0^1 \dee{x}\ (1-x^2) =8\pi\left[1-\frac{1}{3}\right]\\ &=\frac{16\pi}{3} \end{align*}

Suppose that we wish to find the work done by a force \(\vF(\vr)\) moving a particle along a path \(\vr(t)\text{.}\) During the “infinitesimal time interval” 1  from \(t\) to \(t+\dee{t}\) the particle moves from \(\vr(t)\) to \(\vr(t)+\dee{\vr}\) with \(\dee{\vr}=\diff{\vr}{t}(t)\,\dee{t}\text{.}\) By definition, the work done during that infinitesimal time interval is

\begin{equation*} \vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\dee{\vr} = \vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot \diff{\vr}{t}(t)\,\dee{t} \end{equation*}

The total work done during the time interval from \(t_0\) to \(t_1\) is then

\begin{equation*} \text{Work} = \int_{t_0}^{t_1}\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\diff{\vr}{t}(t)\,\dee{t} \end{equation*}

There are some useful shorthand notations for this work.

There is another, often more efficient, method to find the torsion \(\tau\) that uses

\begin{align*} \diff{\va}{t} &= \diff{}{t}\Big(\difftwo{s}{t}\,\hat\vT +\ka\Big(\diff{s}{t}\Big)^2\hat\vN\Big)\\ &= \ddiff{3}{s}{t}\,\hat\vT +\difftwo{s}{t}\,\diff{s}{t}\,\ka\hat\vN +\diff{}{t}\Big(\ka\Big(\diff{s}{t}\Big)^2\Big)\hat\vN +\ka\Big(\diff{s}{t}\Big)^3 \big(\tau\hat\vB-\ka\hat\vT\big) \end{align*}

While this looks a little complicated, notice that, with just one exception, namely \(\ka\big(\diff{s}{t}\big)^3\tau(s)\hat\vB(s)\text{,}\) every term on the right hand side is either in the direction \(\hat\vT\) or in the direction \(\hat\vN\) and so is perpendicular to \(\hat\vB\text{.}\) So, dotting with \(\vv\times\va = \ka \big(\diff{s}{t}\big)^3\hat\vB\) gives

\begin{gather*} \big(\vv\times\va\big)\cdot \diff{\va}{t} = \ka^2 \Big(\diff{s}{t}\Big)^6\,\tau = |\vv\times\va|^2\,\tau \end{gather*}

and hence

\begin{gather*} \tau = \frac{\big(\vv\times\va\big)\cdot \diff{\va}{t} }{|\vv\times\va|^2} \end{gather*}

On \(\cT_1\text{,}\) \(z=0\text{,}\) so \(\int_{\cT_1}z\hi\cdot \dee{\vr}=0\text{.}\) On \(\cT_2\text{,}\) \(x=0\text{,}\) so \(\hi\cdot \dee{\vr}=\dee{x}=0\) and \(\int_{\cT_2}z\hi\cdot \dee{\vr}=0\text{.}\) Parametrize \(\cT_3\) by \(\vr(t)=t\hi+(1-t)\hk\text{,}\) \(0\le t\le 1\text{.}\) Then \(\diff{\vr}{t}=\hi-\hk\) and the \(z\)-coordinate of the path is parametrized by \(1-t\text{.}\) So,

\begin{align*} \int_\cT(z\hi+\vF)\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\int_{\cT_3}z\hi\cdot \dee{\vr}=\int_{0}^1\overbrace{(1-t)\hi}^{z\hi}\cdot\overbrace{(\hi-\hk)\ \dee{t}}^{\dee{\vr}}\\ &=\int_0^1 (1-t) \ \dee{t} =\frac{1}{2} \end{align*}

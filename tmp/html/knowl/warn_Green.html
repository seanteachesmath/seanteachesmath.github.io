Warning 15.3.3.

Note that in Theorem 15.3.2 we are assuming that \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) have continuous first partial derivatives at every point of \(R\text{.}\) If that is not the case, for example because \(F_1\) or \(F_2\) is not defined on all of \(R\text{,}\) then the conclusion of Green's theorem can fail. An example is \(F_1=-\frac{y}{x^2+y^2}\text{,}\) \(F_2=\frac{x}{x^2+y^2}\text{,}\) \(R=\Set{(x,y)}{x^2+y^2\le 1}\text{.}\) See ExamplesÂ 15.3.7 and 15.3.8.

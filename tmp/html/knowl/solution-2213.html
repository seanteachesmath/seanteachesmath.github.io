Solution 14.3.6.3.1.

(a) Think of the cylinder as being a piece of paper that has been partially rolled up. If you flatten the piece of paper out, you get a rectangle with the length of one side being \(h\) and the length of the other side being one quarter of the circumference of a circle of radius \(a\text{,}\) i.e. \(\frac{1}{4}(2\pi a)=\frac{\pi a}{2}\text{.}\) So the area of \(S\) is \(\frac{\pi a h}{2}\text{.}\)

(b) \(S\) is parametrized by

\begin{equation*} x(\theta,y)=a\cos\theta\qquad y(\theta,y)=y\qquad z(\theta,z)=a\sin\theta \end{equation*}

with \((\theta,y)\) running over \(0\le \theta\le \frac{\pi}{2},\ 0\le y\le h\text{.}\) Then, by (14.3.1),

\begin{align*} \Big(\pdiff{x}{\theta},\pdiff{y}{\theta},\pdiff{z}{\theta}\Big) &=(-a\sin\theta,0,a\cos\theta)\\ \Big(\pdiff{x}{y},\pdiff{y}{y},\pdiff{z}{y}\Big) &=(0,1,0)\\ \dee{S} &= \left|\Big(\pdiff{x}{\theta},\pdiff{y}{\theta},\pdiff{z}{\theta}\Big) \times\Big(\pdiff{x}{y},\pdiff{y}{y},\pdiff{z}{y}\Big)\right|\, \dee{\theta}\, \dee{y}\\ &=\big|(-a\cos\theta,0,-a\sin\theta)\big|\,\dee{\theta}\,\dee{y}\\ &=a\,\dee{\theta}\,\dee{y} \end{align*}

So

\begin{align*} \text{Area}(S)&=\dblInt_S\dee{S} =\int_0^{\pi/2}\dee{\theta}\int_0^h\dee{y}\ a =a\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)h \end{align*}

Remark: it's easy to check that this equation satisfies the two properties we desire. Since the \(x\)- and \(y\) coordinates match, it's in the plane \(x=y\text{.}\) To check that it's a circle centred at \((2,2,4)\text{,}\) we note the distance from \((x,y,z)\) to \((2,2,4)\) is:

\begin{align*} d&=\sqrt{(x-2)^2+(y-2)^2+(z-4)^2}\\ &=\sqrt{\left(\frac1{\sqrt 2}\cos\theta\right)^2+\left(\frac1{\sqrt 2}\cos\theta\right)^2+\left(\sin\theta\right)^2}\\ &=\sqrt{\frac12\cos^2\theta+\frac12\cos^2\theta+\sin^2\theta}=\sqrt{\cos^2\theta+\sin^2\theta}\\ &=1 \end{align*}

So, our points all have distance one from the same point â€” that is, they lie on a circle of radius 1.

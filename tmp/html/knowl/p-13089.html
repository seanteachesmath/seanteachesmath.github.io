A surface is defined implicitly by \(z^4 - xy^2 z^2 + y = 0\text{.}\)

  1. Compute \(\pdiff{z}{x}\text{,}\) \(\pdiff{z}{y}\) in terms of \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(z\text{.}\)
  2. Evaluate \(\pdiff{z}{x}\) and \(\pdiff{z}{y}\) at \((x, y, z) = (2, -1/2, 1)\text{.}\)
  3. If \(x\) decreases from \(2\) to \(1.94\text{,}\) and \(y\) increases from \(-0.5\) to \(-0.4\text{,}\) find the approximate change in \(z\) from \(1\text{.}\)
  4. Find the equation of the tangent plane to the surface at the point \((2, -1/2, 1)\text{.}\)

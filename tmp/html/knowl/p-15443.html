The chain rule gives us a relationship between \(\vb'(s)\) and \(\va'(t)\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \diff{\vb}{s}&=\diff{}{s}[\va(t(s))]=\diff{\va}{t}\,\diff{t}{s} \end{align*}

So, the vectors \(\diff{\vb}{s}\) and \(\diff{\va}{t}\) differ only by the scalar function \(\diff{t}{s}\text{.}\) So, at any point along the curve, these vectors are parallel.

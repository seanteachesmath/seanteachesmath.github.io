Long discussion: First consider part (c) of Q[15.3.1.3]. The curve \(C\) is the boundary of the region

\begin{equation*} R = \Set{(x,y)}{1\le x^2+y^2\le 2,\ y\ge 0} \end{equation*}

The partial derivatives \(\pdiff{}{x}\Big( \frac{x}{x^2+y^2}\Big)\) and \(\pdiff{}{y}\Big( \frac{-y}{x^2+y^2}\Big)\) are well-defined and equal everywhere in \(R\text{.}\) So by Green's theorem

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{2\pi}\oint_C \frac{x\,\dee{y}-y\,\dee{x}}{x^2+y^2} &=\frac{1}{2\pi}\dblInt_R\left[ \pdiff{}{x}\Big( \frac{x}{x^2+y^2}\Big) -\pdiff{}{y}\Big( \frac{-y}{x^2+y^2}\Big)\right]\dee{x}\, \dee{y}\\ &=0 \end{align*}

which is the answer we got before.

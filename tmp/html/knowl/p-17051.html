If, for some point \((x_0,y_0,z_0)\) we have \(G_x(x_0,y_0,z_0)= G_y(x_0,y_0,z_0)= G_z(x_0,y_0,z_0)= 0\text{,}\) we also have problem! Often this is a sign that our surface is not smooth at \((x_0,y_0,z_0)\) and in fact does not have a normal vector there. For an example of this, see Example 14.2.2.

(b) Consider the square in the figure (b) on the right above, oriented counterclockwise. It consists of the four line segments \(L_1\text{,}\) \(L_2\text{,}\) \(L_3\) and \(L_4\text{.}\) On all of \(L_1\text{,}\) \(L_2\text{,}\) \(L_3\) we have that \(\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\vr'(t)=0\) because the vector field is perpendicular to the line segment. On \(L_4\) we have \(\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\vr'(t) \gt 0\text{.}\) So

\begin{align*} \oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &= \int_{L_1}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_3}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_4}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}\\ & =0+0+0+\int_{L_4}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} \gt 0 \end{align*}

So \(\cC\) is a closed curve with \(\oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} \gt 0\) and \(\vF\) is not conservative.

The centre of curvature for the curve at \(\vr(t_0)\) is \(\vc = \vr(t_0)+\rho(t_0)\hat\vN(t_0)\text{.}\) It is the red dot in the figure.

  • The radius of the osculating circle is the distance from its centre, \(\vc\text{,}\) to any point of the circle, like \(\vr(t_0)\text{.}\) That's \(|\vr(t_0) -\vc| = |\rho(t_0)\hat\vN(t_0)| = \rho(t_0)\text{.}\) The curvature of the osculating circle is one over its radius. So its curvature is \(\frac{1}{\rho(t_0)}=\ka(t_0)\text{.}\)
  • The unit normal to the osculating circle at \(\vr(t_0)\) is a unit vector in the opposite direction to the radius vector from the centre \(\vc\) to \(\vr(t_0)\text{.}\) The radius vector is \(\vr(t_0)-\vc_0=-\rho(t_0)\hat\vN(t_0)\text{,}\) so the unit normal is \(\hat\vN(t_0)\text{.}\)
  • The osculating circle lies in the plane that best fits the curve near \(\vr(t_0)\text{.}\) (See the beginning of ยง12.4.) So the unit tangents to the osculating circle at \(\vr(t_0)\) are perpendicular to both \(\hat\vN(t_0)\) and \(\hat\vB(t_0)\) and so are either \(\hat\vT(t_0)\) or \(-\hat\vT(t_0)\text{,}\) depending on how we orient the osculating circle.

This suggests that we apply the limit comparison test with \(a_n=\frac{n^2+n+1}{n^5-n}\) and \(b_n=\frac{1}{n^3}\text{.}\) Since

\begin{align*} \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{(n^2+n+1)/(n^5-n)}{1/n^3} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{n^5+n^4+n^3}{n^5-n}\\ \amp=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{1+1/n+1/n^2}{1-1/n^4}\\ &=1 \end{align*}

exists and is nonzero, and since \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1}{n^3}\) converges (by the \(p\)-test with \(p=3 \gt 1\)), the given series converges.

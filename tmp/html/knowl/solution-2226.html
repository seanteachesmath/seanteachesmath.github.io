Solution 14.3.6.16.1.

(a) The surface is \(g(x,y,z)=x^2+y^2+z^2-a^2=0\text{.}\) So, on the surface of the sphere,

\begin{align*} &\hn =\frac{\vnabla g}{|\vnabla g|}=\frac{x\hi+y\hj+z\hk}{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}}\\ &\implies\ \vF\cdot\hn = \big(x^2+y^2+z^2\big)^{n++1-1/2} ={\big(a^2\big)}^{n+1/2}=a^{2n+1}\\ &\implies\ \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn \,\dee{S}\ =a^{2n+1}\dblInt_S\,\dee{S} =a^{2n+1}\text{Area}(S) =4\pi a^{2n+3} \end{align*}

since the surface area of a sphere of radius \(a\) is \(4\pi a^2\text{.}\)

(b) The box has six faces.

\begin{align*} S_1&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{0\le x\le a,\ 0\le y\le b,\ z=c}\ \text{with outward normal } \hn=\hk\\ S_2&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{0\le x\le a,\ 0\le y\le b,\ z=0}\ \text{with outward normal } \hn=-\hk\\ S_3&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{0\le x\le a,\ 0\le z\le c,\ y=b}\ \text{with outward normal } \hn=\hj\\ S_4&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{0\le x\le a,\ 0\le z\le c,\ y=0}\ \text{with outward normal } \hn=-\hj\\ S_5&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{0\le y\le b,\ 0\le z\le c,\ x=a}\ \text{with outward normal } \hn=\hi\\ S_6&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{0\le y\le b,\ 0\le z\le c,\ x=0}\ \text{with outward normal } \hn=-\hi \end{align*}

For \(S_1\text{,}\) i.e. the \(z=c\) face, and \(S_2\text{,}\) i.e. the \(z=0\) face,

\begin{alignat*}{2} \int_{\Atop{z=c}{\rm face}}\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} &=\int_{\Atop{z=c}{\rm face}}\big(x\,\hi+y\,\hj+c\,\hk\big)\cdot \hk \ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} &&=c\int_{\Atop{z=c}{\rm face}}\dee{x}\,\dee{y} =abc\\ \int_{\Atop{z=0}{\rm face}}\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} &=\int_{\Atop{z=0}{\rm face}}\big(x\,\hi+y\,\hj+0\,\hk\big)\cdot(-\hk)\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} &&=0 \end{alignat*}

because the \(z=c\) face has area \(ab\text{.}\) Similarly,

\begin{equation*} \int_{\Atop{x=0}{\rm face}}\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} =\int_{\Atop{y=0}{\rm face}}\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} =0\qquad \int_{\Atop{x=a}{\rm face}}\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} =\int_{\Atop{y=b}{\rm face}}\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} =abc \end{equation*}

The total flux is \(3abc\text{.}\)

(c) The base of the cone is \(\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2\le1,\ z=0}\) and has (outward) normal \(\hn=-\hk\text{.}\) So The flux through the base is

\begin{equation*} \int_{{\rm base}}\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} =\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1}(y\hi)\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}=0 \end{equation*}

In cylindrical coordinates \(x=r\cos\theta\text{,}\) \(y=r\sin\theta\text{,}\) \(z=z\) and the equation \(z=1-\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) of the top part of the cone becomes \(z=1-r\text{.}\) So we may parametrize the top part of the cone by

\begin{equation*} \vr(r,\theta)=r\cos\theta\,\hi+r\sin\theta\,\hj +(1-r)\,\hk \qquad\text{with } 0\le\theta\le 2\pi,\ 0\le r\le 1 \end{equation*}

Then

\begin{align*} \pdiff{\vr}{r}&=\cos\theta\,\hi+\sin\theta\,\hj-\hk\\ \pdiff{\vr}{\theta}&=-r\sin\theta\,\hi+r\cos\theta\,\hj\\ \pdiff{\vr}{r}\times\pdiff{\vr}{\theta} &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi & \hj & \hk\\ \cos\theta & \sin\theta & -1\\ -r\sin\theta & r\cos\theta & 0 \end{matrix}\right]\\ &= -r\cos\theta\,\hi-r\sin\theta\,\hj+r\,\hk\\ \implies \hn\,\dee{S}&=\pdiff{\vr}{r}\times\pdiff{\vr}{\theta} \, \dee{r}\,\dee{\theta}\\ &=\big(-r\cos\theta\,\hi-r\sin\theta\,\hj+r\hk\big)\, \dee{r}\,\dee{\theta} \end{align*}

by (14.1.1). We have the orientation correct because the \(\hk\) component of \(\hn\) is positive. The flux through the top, as well as the total flux, is

\begin{align*} &\int_{{\rm top}}\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S}\\ &\hskip0.25in=\int_0^1 \dee{r}\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ \big(\overbrace{r\sin\theta}^{y}\,\hi +\overbrace{(1-r)}^{z}\,\hk\big) \cdot\big(-r\cos\theta\,\hi-r\sin\theta\,\hj+r\,\hk\big)\\ &\hskip0.25in=\int_0^1 \dee{r}\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ \big(-r^2\sin\theta\cos\theta+r(1-r)\big)\\ &\hskip0.25in= -\bigg[\int_0^1 \dee{r}\ r^2\bigg] \bigg[\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ \frac{1}{2}\sin(2\theta)\bigg] +2\pi \int_0^1 \dee{r}\big[r-r^2\big]\\ &\hskip0.25in=-\frac{1}{3}\times 0+2\pi\Big[\frac{1}{2}-\frac{1}{3}\Big] =\frac{\pi}{3} \end{align*}

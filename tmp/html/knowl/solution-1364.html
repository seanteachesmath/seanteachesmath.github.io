Solution 8.5.3.12.1.

We apply the ratio test with \(a_n= \frac{4^n}{n}(x-1)^n\text{.}\) Since

\begin{align*} \lim_{n\to\infty} \bigg| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \bigg| &= \lim_{n\to\infty} \bigg| \frac{4^{n+1}(x-1)^{n+1}/(n+1)}{4^n(x-1)^n/n} \bigg|\\ &= \lim_{n\to\infty} 4|x-1| \frac{n}{n+1}\\ &= 4|x-1| \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{n}{n+1} = 4|x-1|\cdot1. \end{align*}

the series converges if

\begin{align*} 4|x-1| \lt 1 \amp\iff -1 \lt 4(x-1) \lt 1 \iff -\frac{1}{4} \lt x-1 \lt \frac{1}{4}\\ \amp\iff \frac{3}{4} \lt x \lt \frac{5}{4} \end{align*}

and diverges if \(4|x-1| \gt 1\text{.}\) Checking the right endpoint \(x=\frac{5}{4}\text{,}\) we see that

\begin{gather*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{4^n}{n}\bigg( \frac{5}{4}-1 \bigg)^n = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{n} \end{gather*}

is the divergent harmonic series. At the left endpoint \(x=\frac{3}{4}\text{,}\)

\begin{gather*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{4^n}{n}\bigg( \frac34-1 \bigg)^n = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{n} \end{gather*}

converges by the alternating series test. Therefore the interval of convergence of the original series is \(\frac{3}{4}\le x \lt \frac{5}{4}\text{,}\) or \(\big[\frac{3}{4},\frac{5}{4}\big)\text{.}\)

Finally, the third equation is also satisfied if and only if \(M(x,y)\) and \(N(x,y)\) obey

\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial }{\partial x}\Big(-\frac{xz^2}{2} + N(x,y)\Big) -\frac{\partial }{\partial y}\Big(xe^yz^2-\frac{yz^2}{2} + M(x,y)\Big) =y^2 - xe^y z^2 \end{equation*}

which simplifies to

\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial N}{\partial x}(x,y) -\frac{\partial M}{\partial y}(x,y) =y^2 \end{equation*}

This is one linear equation in two unknowns, \(M\) and \(N\text{.}\) Typically, we can easily solve one linear equation in one unknown.

In fact, we shall see later 5 , in Examples 10.9.13 and 10.9.15, critical points that are neither local maxima nor a local minima. None-the-less, Theorem 10.9.2 is very useful because often functions have only a small number of critical points. To find local maxima and minima of such functions, we only need to consider its critical and singular points. We'll return later to the question of how to tell if a critical point is a local maximum, local minimum or neither. For now, we'll just practice finding critical points.

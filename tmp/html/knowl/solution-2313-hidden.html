Denote by \(V\) the cube specified in the problem. Then \(\partial V\) consists of \(S\) together with the face \(F\) in the plane \(z=0\text{,}\) oriented with the normal being \(-\hk\text{.}\)

As

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &= \pdiff{}{x}\big(y \cos(y^2) + z - 1\big) +\pdiff{}{y}\left(\frac{z}{x+1}+1\right) +\pdiff{}{z}\big(xy e^{z^2}\big)\\ &= \pdiff{}{z}\big(xy e^{z^2}\big) \end{align*}

the divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.2) gives

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot \hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} -\dblInt_F \vF \cdot (-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &=\int_0^1\dee{x}\int_0^1\dee{y}\int_0^1\dee{z}\ \pdiff{}{z}\big(xy e^{z^2}\big) +\int_0^1\dee{x}\int_0^1\dee{y}\ xy e^{z^2}\Big|_{z=0}\\ &=\int_0^1\dee{x}\int_0^1\dee{y}\ xy e^{z^2}\Big|_{z=0}^{z=1} +\int_0^1\dee{x}\int_0^1\dee{y}\ xy e^{z^2}\Big|_{z=0}\\ &=\int_0^1\dee{x}\int_0^1\dee{y}\ xy e^{z^2}\Big|_{z=1}\\ &=e\left[\frac{x^2}{2}\right]_0^1\ \left[\frac{y^2}{2}\right]_0^1\\ &=\frac{e}{4} \end{align*}

(c) We'll use the integal test. The \(k^{\rm th}\) term in the series is \(a_k=\frac{1}{k(\log k) (\log\log k)}=f(k)\) with \(f(x)=\frac{1}{x(\log x) (\log\log x)}\text{,}\) which is continuous, positive and decreasing for \(x\ge 3\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \int_3^\infty f(x)\ \dee{x} &= \int_3^\infty \frac{\dee{x}}{x(\log x) (\log\log x)} =\lim_{R\rightarrow\infty} \int_3^R \frac{\dee{x}}{x(\log x) (\log\log x)}\\ &=\lim_{R\rightarrow\infty} \int_{\log 3}^{\log R} \frac{\dee{y}}{y\log y} \qquad\text{with }y=\log x,\ \dee{y}=\frac{\dee{x}}{x}\\ &= \lim_{R\rightarrow\infty}\int_{\log \log 3}^{\log \log R} \frac{\dee{t}}{t} \qquad\text{with }t=\log y,\ \dee{t}=\frac{\dee{y}}{y}\\ &=\lim_{R \to \infty}\Big[\log t\Big]_{\log \log 3}^{\log\log R}=\infty \end{align*}

Since the integral is divergent, the series is divergent as well by the integral test.

(e) The equation \(z^2 - x^2 - y^2 + 4y - 3 = 0\) can be rewritten as

\begin{equation*} x^2 + (y-2)^2 = 1+z^2 \end{equation*}

As is part (d), for each \(z_0\text{,}\) the \(z=z_0\) cross-section of this surface is a circle \(x^2+(y-2)^2=1+z_0^2\) of radius \(\sqrt{1+z_0^2}\text{.}\) But this circle is centred at \((0,2,z_0)\text{,}\) whereas the corresponding circle in part (d) was centred at \((0,0,z_0)\text{.}\) The solid \(R\) again has the shape of an hourglass drum. But while the origin \((0,0,0)\) was in \(E\text{,}\) it is not in

\begin{equation*} R=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+(y-2)^2\le 1+z^2,\ -1\le z\le 1} \end{equation*}

So \(\vnabla \cdot \vF=0\) throughout all of \(R\) and the divergence theorem gives

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_{\Sigma} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_{\partial R} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\tripInt_{R} \vnabla \cdot \vF\,\dee{V} =0 \end{gather*}

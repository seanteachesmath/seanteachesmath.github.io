If \(f(x,y)\) is continuous in a region \(\cR\) described by both

\begin{equation*} \left\{\begin{aligned} a&\le x\le b \\ B(x)&\le y\le T(x) \end{aligned}\right\} \qquad\text{and}\qquad \left\{ \begin{aligned} c&\le y\le d\\ L(y)&\le x\le R(y) \end{aligned} \right\} \end{equation*}

for continuous functions \(B(x)\text{,}\) \(T(x)\text{,}\) \(L(y)\text{,}\) \(R(y)\text{,}\) then both

\begin{equation*} \int_a^b \dee{x} \bigg[\int_{B(x)}^{T(x)}\dee{y}\ f(x,y)\bigg] \qquad\text{and}\qquad \int_c^d \dee{y} \bigg[\int_{L(y)}^{R(y)}\dee{x}\ f(x,y)\bigg] \end{equation*}

exist and are equal.

Solution 7.1.2.6.1.

DefinitionÂ 7.1.1 tells us the work done by the force is \(W(b) = \int_1^b F(x) \dee{x}\text{,}\) where \(F(x)\) is the force on the object at position \(x\text{.}\) So, by the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus Part 1,

\begin{align*} \diff{}{b}\{W(b)\} = \diff{}{b}\left\{\int_1^b F(x) \dee{x}\right\} &= F(b)\\ \diff{}{b}\left\{-b^3+6b^2-9b+4\right\}&=F(b)\\ -3b^2+12b-9&=F(b)\\ -3(b-1)(b-3)&=F(b) \end{align*}

So, \(F(x)\) is the quadratic polynomial \(-3(x-1)(x-3)\text{.}\)

The largest absolute value of \(F(x)\) over \([1,3]\) occurs at \(x=2\text{.}\) At this point, we have our strongest force.

This isn't a rational sequence, but factoring out \(\sqrt{n}\) from the top and bottom will still clear things up.

\begin{align*} b_n &= \dfrac{\sqrt[4]{n}+1}{\sqrt{9n+3}}\left(\dfrac{\frac{1}{\sqrt n}}{\frac{1}{\sqrt n}}\right)= \dfrac{\frac{1}{\sqrt[4]{n}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}}{\sqrt{9+\frac{3}{n}}}\\ \lim_{n \to \infty}b_n &= \lim_{n \to \infty} \dfrac{\frac{1}{\sqrt[4]{n}}+\frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}}{\sqrt{9+\frac{3}{n}}} = \dfrac{0+0}{\sqrt{9+0}}=0 \end{align*}

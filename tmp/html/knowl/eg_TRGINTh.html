Example 6.8.12. \(\int \tan^3 x\dee{x}\).

Solution:

  • Write the integrand \(\tan^3 x=\frac{\sin^2x}{\cos^3 x}\sin x\text{.}\)
  • Again substitute \(u=\cos x\text{,}\) \(\dee{u}=-\sin x\,\dee{x}\text{.}\) We rewrite the remaining even powers of \(\sin x\) using \(\sin^2x=1-\cos^2x=1-u^2\text{.}\)
  • Hence
    \begin{align*} \int \tan^3 x\,\dee{x} &=\int \frac{\sin^2x}{\cos^3x}\sin x\,\dee{x} \qquad \text{substitute $u=\cos x$}\\ &=\int \frac{1-u^2}{u^3} (-1) \dee{u}\\ &=\frac{u^{-2}}{2}+\log|u|+C\\ &=\frac{1}{2 \cos^2 x}+\log\left|\cos x\right|+C \qquad \text{rewrite in terms of secant}\\ &= \frac{1}{2}\sec^2 x - \log\left|\sec x\right|+C \end{align*}

Item.
As \(x\) tends to \(a\text{,}\) the numerator and denominator both tend to zero. But \(\tfrac{0}{0}\) is not defined. So to get a well defined limit we need to exhibit a cancellation between the numerator and denominator — just as we saw in Examples 2.4.12 and 2.4.17. Now there are two equivalent ways to proceed from here, both based on a similar “trick”.

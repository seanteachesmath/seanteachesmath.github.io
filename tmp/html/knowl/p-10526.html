Now, to confirm that our intuition is correct, we'll first try setting \(a_n=\left| \frac{\sqrt{n}\cos(n)}{n^2-1}\right|\) and \(b_n = \frac{1}{n^{3/2}}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{a_n}{b_n}&=\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\left|\frac{\sqrt{n}\cos(n)}{n^2-1}\right| }{\frac{1}{n^{3/2}}} = \lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{\sqrt{n}\cdot\sqrt{n}^3|\cos n|}{n^2-1}\\ &=\lim_{n \to \infty}\frac{n^2|\cos n|}{n^2-1} = \lim_{n \to \infty}\left(\frac{n^2}{n^2-1}\right)|\cos n|\\ & = \lim_{n \to \infty}1\cdot|\cos n| \end{align*}

Unfortunately, this limit doesn't exist, so we can't use the limit comparison theorem. We'll need a slightly different tactic.

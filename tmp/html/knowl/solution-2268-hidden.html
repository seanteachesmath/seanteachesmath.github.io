(a) \(\vF\) is well-defined wherever the denominator \(x^2+z^2\) is nonzero. So the (largest possible) domain is

\begin{gather*} D=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+z^2\ne 0} \end{gather*}

(b) As preliminary computations, let's find

\begin{align*} \pdiff{}{z}\left(\frac{-z}{x^2+z^2}\right) &=\frac{-1}{x^2+z^2}-\frac{2z(-z)}{{(x^2+z^2)}^2} =\frac{-x^2+z^2}{{(x^2+z^2)}^2}\\ \pdiff{}{x}\left(\frac{x}{x^2+z^2}\right) &=\frac{1}{x^2+z^2}-\frac{2x(x)}{{(x^2+z^2)}^2} =\frac{-x^2+z^2}{{(x^2+y^2)}^2} \end{align*}

So the curl of \(\vF\) is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi &\hj &\hk \\ \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial y} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial z} \\ \frac{-z}{x^2+z^2} & y & \frac{x}{x^2+z^2} \end{matrix} \right] =-\left(\frac{-x^2+z^2}{{(x^2+y^2)}^2} -\frac{-x^2+z^2}{{(x^2+y^2)}^2}\right)\hj\\ &=\vZero \end{align*}

on the domain of \(\vF\).

(c) As preliminary computations, let's find

\begin{align*} \pdiff{}{x}\left(\frac{-z}{x^2+z^2}\right) &=-\frac{2x(-z)}{{(x^2+z^2)}^2} =\frac{2xz}{{(x^2+z^2)}^2}\\ \pdiff{}{z}\left(\frac{x}{x^2+z^2}\right) &=-\frac{2z(x)}{{(x^2+z^2)}^2} =\frac{-2xz}{{(x^2+y^2)}^2} \end{align*}

So the divergence of \(\vF\) is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &=\pdiff{}{x}\left(\frac{-z}{x^2+z^2}\right) +\pdiff{}{y}\left(y\right) + \pdiff{}{z}\left(\frac{x}{x^2+z^2}\right) =1 \end{align*}

(d) By part (b), the vector field passes the conservative field screening test \(\vnabla\times\vF=\vZero\text{.}\) But we should still be suspicious because of the similarity of \(\vF\) to the vector field of Examples 13.3.14 and 15.3.8.

So let's compute the line integral of \(\vF\) around the (closed) circle \(y=0\text{,}\) \(x^2+z^2=1\text{,}\) parametrized by

\begin{equation*} \vr(t) = \cos t\,\hi +\sin t\,\hk\qquad \vr'(t) = -\sin t\,\hi +\cos t\,\hk \end{equation*}

The line integral is

\begin{align*} \int_C \vF \cdot \dee{\vr} &=\int_0^{2\pi}\!\!\big\{ \overbrace{-\sin t}^{\frac{-z}{x^2+z^2}}\,\hi +\overbrace{\cos t}^{\frac{x}{x^2+y^2}}\,\hk\big\} \cdot \big\{\overbrace{-\sin t\,\hi +\cos t\,\hk}^{\vr'(t)}\big\}\dee{t}\\ &= \int_0^{2\pi}\dee{t} =2\pi \end{align*}

As the integral of \(\vF\) around the simple closed curve \(C\) is not zero, \(\vF\) cannot be conservative on \(D\text{.}\) See Theorem 13.4.7 and Examples 13.3.14 and 15.3.8.

1. When \(a_1=4\text{,}\) we see \(a_2=\dfrac{4+8}{3}=4\text{,}\) and so on. That is, \(a_n=4\) for every \(n\text{.}\) So, \(\lim\limits_{n \to \infty} a_n=4\text{.}\)
  2. Cross-multiplying, we see \(3x=x+8\text{,}\) hence \(x=4\text{.}\)
  3. In order for our sequence to converge to 4, the terms should be getting infinitely close to 4. So, we find the relationship between \(\textcolor{red}{a_{n+1}-4}\) and \(\textcolor{blue}{a_n-4}\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} a_{n+1}&= \frac{a_n+8}{3}\\ \textcolor{red}{a_{n+1}-4}&= \frac{a_n+8}{3} -4=\frac{\textcolor{blue}{a_n-4}}{8} \end{align*}
    So, the distance between our sequence terms and the number 4 is decreasing by a factor of 8 each term. This implies that the terms get infinitely close to 4 as \(n\) grows. That is, \(\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}a_n=4\text{.}\)

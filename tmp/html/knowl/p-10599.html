  1. Let \(0 \lt R \lt \infty\text{.}\) If \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty A_n(x-c)^n\) converges for \(|x-c| \lt R\text{,}\) and diverges for \(|x-c| \gt R\text{,}\) then we say that the series has radius of convergence \(R\text{.}\)
  2. If \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty A_n(x-c)^n\) converges for every number \(x\text{,}\) we say that the series has an infinite radius of convergence.
  3. If \(\sum_{n=0}^\infty A_n(x-c)^n\) diverges for every \(x\ne c\text{,}\) we say that the series has radius of convergence zero.

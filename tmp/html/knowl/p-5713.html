Applying Theorem 6.2.3 yet again, we have, for all real numbers \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \int_a^b x\dee{x} &= \int_a^0 x\dee{x} + \int_0^b x\dee{x} & \text{by Theorem }\knowl{./knowl/thm_Intdomain.html}{\text{6.2.3}}(c)\text{ with $c=0$}\\ &= \int_0^b x\dee{x} - \int_0^a x\dee{x} & \text{by Theorem }\knowl{./knowl/thm_Intdomain.html}{\text{6.2.3}}\text{(b)}\\ &=\frac{b^2-a^2}{2} & \text{by Example }\knowl{./knowl/eg_INTPROPxa.html}{\text{6.2.5}}\text{, twice} \end{align*}

We can also understand this result geometrically.

1. By inspection, when \(y=0\text{,}\) then \(f(x,y)=1\) as long as \(x \neq 0\text{.}\) So, if we follow the \(x\)-axis in towards the origin, \(f(x,y)=1\) along this route.
  2. Also by inspection, when \(x=0\text{,}\) then \(f(x,y)=0\) as long as \(y \neq 0\text{.}\) So, if we follow the \(y\)-axis in towards the origin, \(f(x,y)=0\) along this route.

  3. Since two different directions give us different values as we approach the origin, \(\lim\limits_{(x,y)\to(0,0)}f(x,y)\) does not exist.

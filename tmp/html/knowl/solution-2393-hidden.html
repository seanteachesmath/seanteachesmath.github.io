The boundary of \(S\) is the square \(C\text{,}\) with sides \(C_1\text{,}\) \(\cdots\text{,}\) \(C_4\text{,}\) in the sketch

By Stokes' theorem,

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} =\oint_{C}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} \end{equation*}

Parametrize \(C_1\) by \(x\text{.}\) That is, \(\vr(x)= x\hi - \hj -\hk\text{,}\) \(-1\le x\le 1\text{.}\) Since \(\vr'(x) = \hi\text{,}\) and \(y=z=-1\) on \(C_1\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \int_{C_1}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\int_{-1}^1 \vF\big(\vr(x)\big)\cdot\vr'(x)\ \dee{x} =\int_{-1}^1 \vF\big(\vr(x)\big)\cdot\hi\ \dee{x}\\ &=\int_{-1}^1\overbrace{x(-1)(-1)}^{xyz}\ \dee{x}\\ &=0\qquad\text{(since $x$ is odd)} \end{align*}

Parametrize \(C_2\) by \(y\text{.}\) That is, \(\vr(y)= \hi + y\,\hj -\hk\text{,}\) \(-1\le y\le 1\text{.}\) Since \(\vr'(y) = \hj\text{,}\) and \(x=1\) on \(C_2\text{,}\)

\begin{gather*} \int_{C_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\int_{-1}^1 \vF\big(\vr(y)\big)\cdot\hj\ \dee{y} =\int_{-1}^1\overbrace{y^2}^{xy^2}\ \dee{y} =\left[\frac{y^3}{3}\right]_{-1}^1 =\frac{2}{3} \end{gather*}

Parametrize \(C_3\) by \(x\text{.}\) That is, \(\vr(x)= x\hi + \hj -\hk\) with \(x\) running from \(1\) to \(-1\text{.}\) (If you're nervous about this, parametrize by \(t=-x\text{.}\) That is \(\vr(t)= -t\,\hi + \hj -\hk\text{,}\) \(-1\le t\le 1\text{.}\)) Since \(\vr'(x) = \hi\text{,}\) and \(y=1\text{,}\) \(z=-1\) on \(C_3\text{,}\)

\begin{gather*} \int_{C_3}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\int^{-1}_1\!\! \vF\big(\vr(x)\big)\cdot\hi\ \dee{x} =\int^{-1}_1\overbrace{x\ (1)(-1)}^{xyz}\ \dee{x} =0\quad\text{(since $x$ is odd)} \end{gather*}

Parametrize \(C_4\) by \(y\text{.}\) That is, \(\vr(y)= -\hi + y\,\hj -\hk\text{,}\) with \(y\) running from \(1\) to \(-1\text{.}\) Since \(\vr'(y) = \hj\text{,}\) and \(x=-1\) on \(C_4\text{,}\)

\begin{gather*} \int_{C_4}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\int^{-1}_1 \vF\big(\vr(y)\big)\cdot\hj\ \dee{y} =\int^{-1}_1\overbrace{(-1)y^2}^{xy^2}\ \dee{y} =-\left[\frac{y^3}{3}\right]^{-1}_1 =\frac{2}{3} \end{gather*}

All together

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} =\int_{C_1}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} + \int_{C_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{C_3}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} + \int_{C_4}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\frac{4}{3} \end{equation*}

  1. Sketch the region \(\cL\) (in the first quadrant of the \(xy\)--plane) with boundary curves
    \begin{equation*} x^2 + y^2 = 2,\ x^2 + y^2 = 4,\ y = x,\ y = 0. \end{equation*}
    The mass of a thin lamina with a density function \(\rho(x,y)\) over the region \(\cL\) is given by
    \begin{equation*} M =\dblInt_\cL \rho(x,y)\,\dee{A} \end{equation*}
  2. Find an expression for \(M\) as an integral in polar coordinates.
  3. Find M when
    \begin{equation*} \rho(x,y) = \frac{2xy}{x^2+y^2} \end{equation*}

We are told that

\begin{equation*} \vr(t)= a \cos t\,\hi+a\sin t\,\hj+ct\,\hk \end{equation*}

So, by definition,

\begin{align*} \text{velocity}&= \vv(t)= \vr'(t)=-a \sin t\,\hi+a\cos t\,\hj+c\,\hk\\ \text{speed}&=\diff{s}{t}(t) = |\vr'(t)| = \sqrt{a^2+c^2}\\ \text{acceleration}&=\va(t)= \vr''(t)=-a \cos t\,\hi-a\sin t\,\hj \end{align*}

As \(t\) runs over an interval of length \(2\pi\text{,}\) \((x,y)\) traces out a circle of radius \(a\) and \(z\) increases by \(2\pi c\text{.}\) The path is a helix with radius \(a\) and with each turn having height \(2\pi c\text{.}\)

Item.
The boundary of the square consists of its four sides. One side is
\begin{equation*} \Set{(x,y)}{x=1,\ -1\le y\le 1} \end{equation*}
On this side \(f=\sqrt{1+y^2}\text{.}\) As \(\sqrt{1+y^2}\) increases with \(|y|\text{,}\) the smallest value of \(f\) on that side is \(1\) (when \(y=0\)) and the largest value of \(f\) is \(\sqrt{2}\) (when \(y=\pm 1\)). The same thing happens on the other three sides. The maximum value of \(f\) is achieved at the four corners. Note that \(f_x\) and \(f_y\) are both nonzero at all four corners.

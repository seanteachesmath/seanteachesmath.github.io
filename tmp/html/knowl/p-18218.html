It is correct because

  • if you walk along \(\partial S\) in the direction of the arrow on \(\partial S\text{,}\)
  • with the vector from your feet to your head having direction \(\hn\)
  • then \(S\) is on your left hand side.

Now pretend that the surface \(S\) is made of rubber and that \(\hn\) is glued to \(S\text{.}\) We can push on this \(S\) to deform it to the \(S\) of part (a) or to the \(S\) of part (b). This gives the solutions to parts (a) and (b).

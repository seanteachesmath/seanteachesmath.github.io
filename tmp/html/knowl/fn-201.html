Footnote 6.3.4.
The integral \(\int_0^x e^{-t^2} \dee{t}\) is closely related to the “error function” which is an extremely important function in mathematics. While we cannot express this integral (or the error function) as a finite combination of polynomials, exponentials etc, we can express it as an infinite series
\begin{align*} \int_0^x e^{-t^2}\dee{t} &= x - \frac{x^3}{3\cdot 1} + \frac{x^5}{5\cdot 2} - \frac{x^7}{7\cdot 3!} + \frac{x^9}{9\cdot 4!} +\cdots \\ &\hskip1in+ (-1)^k \frac{x^{2k+1}}{(2k+1)\cdot k!} + \cdots \end{align*}
But more on this in Chapter 8.

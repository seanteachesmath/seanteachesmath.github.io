Solution 2: Alternately, we could have just listed odd multiple of \(\pi\) until we found three that are close to integers.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c|c} \hline \mathbf{2k+1}&\mathbf{(2k+1)\pi}\\ \hline 1&3.14\\ 3&9.42\\ 5&15.71\\ 7&\textcolor{red}{21.99}\\ 9&28.27\\ 11&34.56\\ 13&40.84\\ 15&47.12\\ 17&53.41\\ 19&59.69\\ 21&\textcolor{red}{65.97}\\ 23&72.26\\ 25&78.54\\ 27&84.82\\ 29&91.11\\ 31&97.39\\ 33&103.67\\ 35&\textcolor{red}{109.96} \end{array} \end{equation*}

Some earlier odd multiples of \(\pi\) (like \(15\pi\) and \(29\pi\)) get fairly close to integers, but not within 0.1.

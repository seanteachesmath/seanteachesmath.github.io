Remark: In Example 6.8.23, and in many tables of integrals, the antiderivative of \(\csc^3 x\) is given as \(-\frac{1}{2}\cot x \csc x + \frac{1}{2}\log|\csc x - \cot x|+C\text{.}\) This is equivalent to our result. Recall in the remark after the solution to Question 18, we saw \(\frac{1}{2}\log\left| \frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}\right|=\log|\csc x - \cot x|\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} &-\frac{1}{2}\cot x \csc x + \frac{1}{2}\log|\csc x - \cot x|\\ &\hskip0.5in=-\frac{1}{2}\cot x \csc x + \frac{1}{4}\log\left| \frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}\right|\\ &\hskip0.5in=-\frac{1}{2}\left(\frac{\cos x}{\sin x}\right)\left(\frac{1}{\sin x}\right) + \frac{1}{4}\log\left| \frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}\right|\\ &\hskip0.5in=\frac{-\cos x}{2\sin^2 x }+ \frac{1}{4}\log\left| \frac{1-\cos x}{1+\cos x}\right| \end{align*}

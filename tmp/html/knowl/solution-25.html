Solution 2.3.2.14.1.

\(\displaystyle\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{1}{x}=\) DNE: as \(x\) gets closer and closer to 0 from the left, \(\dfrac{1}{x}\) becomes a larger and larger negative number; but as \(x\) gets closer and closer to 0 from the right, \(\dfrac{1}{x}\) becomes a larger and larger positive number. So the limit from the left is not the same as the limit from the right, and so \(\displaystyle\lim_{x \rightarrow 0} \dfrac{1}{x}=\) DNE. Contrast this with QuestionÂ 2.3.2.15.

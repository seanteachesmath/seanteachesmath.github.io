Take another look at Theorem 12.3.3 and note that

  • the tangential component of acceleration, i.e. \(\difftwo{s}{t}\text{,}\) arises purely from change in speed while
  • the normal component of acceleration, i.e. \(\ka\big(\diff{s}{t}\big)^2\text{,}\) arises from curvature and is proportional to the square of the speed \(\diff{s}{t}\text{.}\) Think about what you feel when you are driving. That's why velodromes and (car) race tracks often have banked corners.

The most-commonly used word makes up \(\alpha\) percent of all the words. So, we want to find \(\alpha\text{.}\)

If we add together the frequencies of all the words, they should amount to 100%. That is,

\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=1}^{20,000} \frac{\alpha}{n}=100 \end{equation*}

We can approximate the sum (with \(\alpha\) left as a parameter) using the ideas behind the integral test. (See ExampleÂ 8.3.4.)

As we see in the diagram above, \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^N \frac{\alpha}{n}\) (which is the sum of the areas of the rectangles) is greater than \(\displaystyle\int_1^{N+1}\frac{\alpha}{x}\dee{x}\) (the area under the curve). That is,

\begin{equation*} \int_1^{N+1} \frac{\alpha}{x}\dee{x} \lt \sum_{n=1}^N \frac{\alpha}{n}\,. \end{equation*}

Using the fact that our language's 20,000 words make up 100% of the words used, we can find a lower bound for \(\alpha\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 100&= \sum_{n=1}^{20,000} \frac{\alpha}{n} \gt \int_1^{20,001} \frac{\alpha}{x}\dee{x} =\Big[\alpha \log(x)\Big]_{1}^{20,001} =\alpha\log(20,001)\\ \alpha& \lt \frac{100}{\log(20,001)} \end{align*}

We can find an upper bound for \(\alpha\) in a similar manner.

From the diagram, we see \(\displaystyle\sum_{n=2}^N \frac{\alpha}{n}\) (which is the sum of the areas of the rectangles, excluding the first) is less than \(\displaystyle\int_1^{N}\frac{\alpha}{x}\dee{x}\text{.}\) (The reason for excluding the first rectangle is to avoid comparing our series to an integral that diverges.) That is,

\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=2}^N \frac{\alpha}{n} \lt \int_1^{N} \frac{\alpha}{x}\dee{x}\,. \end{equation*}

Therefore,

\begin{align*} 100&= \sum_{n=1}^{20,000} \frac{\alpha}{n}= \alpha+\sum_{n=2}^{20,000} \frac{\alpha}{n}\\ & \lt \alpha+\int_1^{20,00} \frac{\alpha}{x}\dee{x}=\alpha+\alpha\log(20,000)=\alpha\big[1+\log(20,000)\big]\\ \alpha& \gt \frac{100}{1+\log(20,000)} \end{align*}

Using a calculator, we see

\begin{equation*} 9.17 \lt \alpha \lt 10.01 \end{equation*}

So, the most-commonly used word makes up about 9-10 percent of the total words.

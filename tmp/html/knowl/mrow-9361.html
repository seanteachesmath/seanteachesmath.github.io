\begin{align*} S_N &= \sum_{n=0}^Nar^n = a + ar + ar^2 + \cdots + ar^N.\\ \end{align*}

The secret to evaluating this sum is to see what happens when we multiply it \(r\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} rS_N &= r\big(a + ar + ar^2 + \cdots + ar^N\big)\\ & = ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + \cdots + ar^{N+1} \end{align*}

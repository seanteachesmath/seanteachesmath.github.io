Let \(f(x,y)=x^2-y^2\)

  1. Express the function in terms of the polar coordinates \(r\) and \(\theta\text{,}\) and simplify.
  2. Suppose \((x,y)\) is a distance of 1 from the origin. What are the largest and smallest values of \(f(x,y)\text{?}\)
  3. Let \(r \gt 0\text{.}\) Suppose \((x,y)\) is a distance of \(r\) from the origin. What are the largest and smallest values of \(f(x,y)\text{?}\)
  4. Let \(\epsilon \gt 0\text{.}\) Find a positive value of \(r\) that guarantees \(|f(x,y)| \lt \epsilon\) whenever \((x,y)\) is at most \(r\) units from the origin.

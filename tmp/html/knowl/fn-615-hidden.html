As we have noted before, the spherical coordinate system really breaks down at \(\varphi=0\text{,}\) because \(\rho=1\text{,}\) \(\varphi=0\) gives the same point, namely the north pole \((0,0,1)\text{,}\) for all values of \(\theta\text{.}\) We should really treat our integral like an improper integral, first integrating over \(\veps \lt \varphi\le\frac{\pi}{2}\) and then taking the limit \(\veps\rightarrow 0^+\text{.}\) However the breakdown of the spherical coordinate system at \(\varphi=0\text{,}\) just like the breakdown of polar coordinates at \(r=0\text{,}\) rarely causes problem and it is routine to skip the “improper integral” step.

(b) We are not told explicitly what \(C_2\) is, so we certainly can't do a direct evaluation. Instead, let's use Stokes' theorem (Theorem 15.4.1). The curl of \(\vF\) is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi &\hj &\hk\\ \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial y} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial z}\\ z^2 & x^2 & y^2 \end{matrix} \right]\\ &= 2y\,\hi +2z\,\hj +2x\,\hk \end{align*}

The upward pointing unit normal to \(E\) is \(\hn=\frac{\hi+\hj+\hk}{\sqrt{3}}\text{.}\) So, by Stokes' theorem,

\begin{align*} I_2 &= \int_{C_2}\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} =\dblInt_R \vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ & =\dblInt_R \big(2y\,\hi +2z\,\hj +2x\,\hk\big)\cdot \frac{\hi+\hj+\hk}{\sqrt{3}}\,\dee{S}\\ &=\frac{2}{\sqrt{3}}\dblInt_R \big(\overbrace{y + z + x}^ {=2\ {\rm on}\ R}\big)\,\dee{S} =\frac{4}{\sqrt{3}}\text{Area}(R) =4\sqrt{3} \end{align*}

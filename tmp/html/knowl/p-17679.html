Let \(V\) be the solid \(x^2+y^2+2z^2\le 2\text{,}\) \(z\ge 0\text{.}\) The surface of \(V\) consists of the half-ellipsoid \(S=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2+2z^2=2,\ z\ge 0}\text{,}\) on top with upward pointing normal, and the disk \(D=\set{(x,y,z)}{z=0,\ x^2+y^2\le 2}\text{,}\) on the bottom with normal \(-\hk\text{.}\) Call the vector field \(\vF\text{.}\) By the divergence theorem

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\ \dee{S} +\dblInt_D\vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S} =\tripInt_V\vnabla\cdot\vF\ \dee{V} =\tripInt_V 4\ \dee{V} \end{equation*}

The ellipsoid has \(a=\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) \(b=\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) \(c=1\) and volume \(\frac{4}{3}\pi abc=\frac{8}{3}\pi\text{.}\) So

\begin{equation*} \tripInt_V 4\ \dee{V}=4\times\half({\rm Volume\ of\ the\ ellipsoid})=\frac{16\pi}{3} \end{equation*}

On \(D\text{,}\) \(z=0\) and \(\dblInt_D x\,\dee{S}=\dblInt_D y\,\dee{S}=0\) because \(x\) and \(y\) are odd. So

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_D\vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_D(x\hi+y\hj+0\hk)\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S} =0 \end{equation*}

and the desired flux is

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\ \dee{S}=\tripInt_V 4\ \dee{V} =\frac{16}{3}\pi \end{equation*}

For the specified \(\vF\) and the surface \(x=f(x,y)=1-\frac{1}{4}x^2-y^2\text{,}\) by (14.3.2),

\begin{align*} \hn\,\dee{S}&=\big(-f_x\,\hi-f_y\,\hj+\hk\big)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =\Big(\frac{x}{2}\,\hi+2y\,\hj+\hk\Big)\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ \nabla\times\vF&=\det\left[\begin{matrix}\hi&\hj&\hk \\ \frac{\partial }{\partial x}& \frac{\partial }{\partial y}& \frac{\partial }{\partial z} \\ 3y^2+z&x-x^2&1\end{matrix}\right]\\ &=\hj+(1-2x-6y)\,\hk\\ \nabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}&= (2y+1-2x-6y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y}= (1-2x-4y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

The domain of integration is \(1-\frac{1}{4}x^2-y^2\ge 0\) or \(\frac{1}{4}x^2+y^2\le 1\text{.}\) This is an ellipse. Call it \(D\text{.}\) So

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\nabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_D (1-2x-4y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{equation*}

The integrals over \(D\) of \(x\text{,}\) which is odd under \(x\rightarrow-x\text{,}\) and of \(y\text{,}\) which is odd under \(y\rightarrow -y\text{,}\) are both zero. As the ellipse \(D\) has area \(A=\pi\times 2\times 1=2\pi\)

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\nabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_D (1-2x-4y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =A =2\pi \end{equation*}

Item.
We are to consider only curves on the surface \(S\text{.}\) For any such curve \(C\text{,}\) say parametrized by \(\vr(t)\) with \(a\le t\le b\text{,}\) the integral
\begin{equation*} \int_C \vG \cdot \dee{\vr} =\int_a^b \vG\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot \diff{\vr}{t}(t)\ \dee{t} \end{equation*}
depends only on the values of \(\vG\) on the surface \(S\text{.}\) In particular, if another vector field \(\vH\) obeys \(\vH(x,y,z)=\vG(x,y,z)\text{,}\) for all points \((x,y,z)\) on \(S\text{,}\) we have
\begin{align*} \int_C \vG \cdot \dee{\vr} &=\int_a^b \vG\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot \diff{\vr}{t}(t)\ \dee{t} =\int_a^b \vH\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot \diff{\vr}{t}(t)\ \dee{t}\\ &=\int_C \vH \cdot \dee{\vr} \end{align*}
So we may replace \(\vG\) with
\begin{align*} \vH(x,y,z) &= \vG(x,y,2+x^2-3y^2)\\ &= \big[x(2+x^2-3y^2) +axy^2\big]\hi +y(2+x^2-3y^2)\hj\\ &= (2x+x^3 -3xy^2 +axy^2)\hi +(2y+yx^2-3y^3)\hj \end{align*}
Note that \(\vH(x,y,z)\) is defined on all of \(\bbbr^3\text{.}\) It just happens to not depend on \(z\text{.}\)

Note that the the paraboloid \(z = 2 - x^2 - y^2\) and the cone \(z = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) intersect along the circle \(x^2+y^2=1\text{,}\) \(z=1\text{.}\) Probably the simplest solid whose boundary includes \(S\) is

\begin{equation*} V=\Set{(x,y,z)}{1\le z\le 2-x^2-y^2,\ x^2+y^2\le 1} \end{equation*}

The boundary \(\partial V\) of \(V\) consists of \(S\) (with upward pointing normal) and the disk

\begin{equation*} D = \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2\le 1,\ z=1} \end{equation*}

with normal \(-\hk\text{.}\) So the divergence theorem gives

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\ \dee{V} -\dblInt_D \vF \cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &=\tripInt_V \overbrace{1}^{\vnabla\cdot\vF}\dee{V} +\dblInt_D \overbrace{1}^{\vF \cdot \hk = z}\,\dee{S} \end{align*}

As \(D\) is a disk of radius 1, \(\dblInt_D \dee{S}=\pi\text{.}\) To compute the volume of \(V\text{,}\) we'll slice it into a stack of horizontal “pancakes”. Since \(z=2-x^2-y^2\) is equivalent to \(\sqrt{x^2+y^2}=\sqrt{2-z}\text{,}\) the pancake at height \(z\) is a circular disk of radius \(\sqrt{2-z}\) and hence of cross-sectional area \(\pi(2-z)\text{.}\) So the volume of \(V\) is

\begin{align*} \tripInt_V \dee{V} &=\int_1^2 \pi(2-z) \ \dee{z} = -\frac{\pi}{2}(2-z)^2\Big|_1^2 =\frac{\pi}{2} \end{align*}

and the flux

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\frac{\pi}{2} +\pi =\frac{3}{2}\pi \end{gather*}

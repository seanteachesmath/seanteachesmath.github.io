\begin{align*} y^2 \diff{y}{x} &= e^x -2x\\ y^2 \,\dee{y} &= (e^x - 2x ) \,\dee{x}.\\ \end{align*}

Integrating both sides, we find

\begin{align*} \int y^2 \,\dee{y} &=\int (e^x - 2x ) \,\dee{x}\\ \frac{1}{3}y^3 &= e^x - x^2 + C\\ \end{align*}

Setting \(x = 0\) and \(y = 3\text{,}\) we find \(\frac{1}{3}3^3=e^0-0^2+C\) and hence \(C=8\text{.}\)

It is completely contained in \(\cD\text{.}\) The boundary of \(S\) consists of two parts. The outside part is \(\cC_\veps\text{,}\) oriented counterclockwise as usual. The inside part is \(\cC_{\veps'}\text{,}\) but oriented clockwise. It is usually denoted \(-\cC_{\veps'}\text{.}\) So, by Stokes' theorem,

\begin{align*} \oint_{\cC_\veps}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} -\oint_{\cC_{\veps'}}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\oint_{\cC_\veps}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\oint_{-\cC_{\veps'}}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\oint_{\partial S}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}\\ &=\dblInt_S \vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\ \dee{S} =0 \end{align*}

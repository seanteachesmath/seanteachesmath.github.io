consider the figure on the lower left. In that figure,

  • the \(x\) component of \(\hat\vT\) is negative (\(\hat\vT\) is leftward pointing in the figure),

    • which makes \(\cos\phi\) negative (see (\(**\))),
    • which makes the \(y\) component of \(\diff{\hat\vT}{\phi}\) negative (see (\(**\)) again),
    • so \(\diff{\hat\vT}{\phi}\) is downward pointing,

    so \(\diff{\hat\vT}{\phi}=-\hat\vN\) (the centre of curvature is the red dot above the curve) and

  • as \(s\) increases (i.e. as you move in the direction of the arrow on the curve), \(\phi\) decreases (on the far right hand part of the curve \(\phi\approx\frac{3\pi}{2}\text{,}\) while on the far left hand part of the curve \(\phi\approx\pi\)), so \(\diff{\phi}{s} \lt 0\) and \(\ka = \big|\diff{\phi}{s}\big| = - \diff{\phi}{s}\text{.}\)
  • So by (\(*\)), \(\diff{\hat\vT}{s} = \diff{\hat\vT}{\phi} \diff{\phi}{s} =\big(-\hat\vN)(-\ka) = \ka\hat\vN\text{.}\)

In each of the three other figures we also end up with \(\diff{\hat\vT}{s} = \ka(s)\hat\vN(s)\text{.}\)

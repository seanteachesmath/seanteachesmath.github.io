Solution 15.2.6.50.1.

The circle \(x^2+y^2=4y\text{,}\) or equivalently, \(x^2+(y-2)^2=4\text{,}\) has radius \(2\) and centre \((0,2)\text{.}\) On the bottom surface, \(z=0\) and the outward normal is \(-\hk\text{,}\) so that

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_{D}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =-\dblInt_{D}\vF\cdot\hk\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =-\dblInt_D(2x+3y)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{equation*}

By symmetry, the centre of mass, \((\bar x,\bar y)\text{,}\) of the circle is \((0,2)\text{.}\) Here \(\bar x\) and \(\bar y\) are the average values

\begin{gather*} \bar x = \frac{\dblInt_D x\,\dee{x}\dee{y}}{\dblInt_D \dee{x}\dee{y}}\qquad \bar y = \frac{\dblInt_D y\,\dee{x}\dee{y}}{\dblInt_D \dee{x}\dee{y}} \end{gather*}

of \(x\) and \(y\) over \(D\text{.}\) As the disk \(D\) has area \(4\pi\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_D x\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =4\pi\bar x =0\qquad \dblInt_D y\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =4\pi\bar y =8\pi \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_{D}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =-4\pi(2\bar x+3\bar y) =-4\pi(2\times 0+3\times 2) =-24\pi \end{equation*}

As

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &=\pdiff{}{x}(x+x^2y) +\pdiff{}{y}(y-xy^2) +\pdiff{}{z}(z+2x+3y)\\ &=(1+2xy)+(1-2xy)+(1)\\ &=3 \end{align*}

the divergence theorem gives

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{\cS}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_R\vnabla\cdot\vF\ \dee{V} -\dblInt_{D}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\tripInt_R3\ \dee{V} -(-24\pi) =3\text{Vol}(R)+24\pi =3\times10+24\pi\\ &=30+24\pi \end{align*}

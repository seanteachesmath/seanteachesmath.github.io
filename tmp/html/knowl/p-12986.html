We have just found linear and quadratic approximations to the function \(f(x,y)\text{,}\) for \((x,y)\) near the point \((x_0,y_0)\text{.}\) In CLP-1, we found not only linear and quadratic approximations, but in fact a whole hierarchy of approximations. For each integer \(n\ge 0\text{,}\) the \(n^\mathrm{th}\) degree Taylor polynomial for \(f(x)\) about \(x=a\) was defined, in Definition 3.4.11 of the CLP-1 text, to be

\begin{gather*} \sum_{k=0}^n \frac{1}{k!} f^{(k)}(a) \cdot (x-a)^k \end{gather*}

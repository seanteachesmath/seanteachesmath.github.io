Set \(u=\sec x\text{,}\) \(\dee{v}=\sec^2 x\dee{x}\text{.}\) Hence \(\dee{u}=\sec x\tan x\dee{x}\text{,}\) \(v=\tan x\) and
\begin{align*} \int \sec^3 x\dee{x} &=\int \underbrace{\sec x}_{u}\ \underbrace{\sec^2 x\dee{x}}_{dv}\\ &=\underbrace{\sec x}_{u}\ \underbrace{\tan x}_{v} -\int \underbrace{\tan x}_{v}\ \underbrace{\sec x\tan x\dee{x}}_{\dee{u}} \end{align*}

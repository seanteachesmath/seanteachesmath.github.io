Furthermore, we know that \(t\) and \(s\) are positively correlated: as \(t\) increases, so does \(s\text{,}\) because we're covering more arclength. So, \(\diff{t}{s}\) is nonnegative. Furthermore, since the derivatives are nonzero, \(\diff{t}{s}\) is nonzero. So, \(\vb'(s_0)\) and \(\va'(t_0)\) are positive scalar multiples of each other. That is, they are parallel, and pointing in the same direction. However, unless \(\diff{t}{s}=1\) (that is, \(t(s)=s+C\) for some constant \(C\)), the vectors do not have the same magnitude, and hence are not equal.

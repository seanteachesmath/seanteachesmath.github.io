(d) Yes. Note that

  • \(\displaystyle \big(\sqrt{2}\sin \phi\cos\theta\big)^2 +\big(\sqrt{2}\sin \phi\sin\theta\big)^2 +\big(\sqrt{2}\cos \phi\big)^2=2\)
  • For \(0\le\phi\le\frac{\pi}{4}\text{,}\) we have \(z=\sqrt{2}\cos \phi \gt 0\text{.}\)
  • As \(\phi\) runs from \(0\) to \(\frac{\pi}{4}\text{,}\) \(r(\phi)=\sqrt{2}\sin\phi\) runs from \(0\) to \(1\text{,}\) so that \(\big(x=r(\phi)\cos\theta\,,\,y=r(\phi)\sin\theta\big)\) covers all of \(x^2+y^2\le1\) as \(\phi\) runs from \(0\) to \(\frac{\pi}{4}\) and \(\theta\) runs from \(0\) to \(2\pi\text{.}\)

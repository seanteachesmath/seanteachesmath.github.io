Let \(S_t\text{,}\) \(S_b\) and \(S_c\) denote the top, bottom and curved surfaces of \(D\) respectively. On the top surface, \(z=5\) and the outward normal to \(D\) is \(\hk\text{,}\) so that

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_{\cS_t}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1}(15-5ye^5)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =15\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1}\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =15\pi \end{equation*}

The integral over \(y\) was zero because \(y\) is odd under \(y\rightarrow -y\text{.}\) On the bottom surface, \(z=0\) and the outward normal to \(D\) is \(-\hk\text{,}\) so that

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_{\cS_b}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =-\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1}(3\times 0-0\times ye^0)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =0 \end{equation*}

Again, the integral over \(y\) was zero because \(y\) is odd under \(y\rightarrow -y\text{.}\) As

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &=\pdiff{}{x}(x+xye^z) +\half\pdiff{}{y}\big( y^2ze^z\big) +\pdiff{}{z}(3z-yze^z)\\ &=(1+ye^z)+yze^z+(3-yze^z-ye^z) =4 \end{align*}

the divergence theorem gives

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{\cS_c}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_D\vnabla\cdot\vF\ \dee{V} -\dblInt_{\cS_t}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} -\dblInt_{\cS_b}\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\tripInt_D4\ \dee{V} -15\pi-0\\ &=4\times\pi 1^2\times 5-15\pi =5\pi \end{align*}

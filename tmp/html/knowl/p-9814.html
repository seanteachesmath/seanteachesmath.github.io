Let \(a\) and \(r\) be real numbers and let \(N \geq 0\) be an integer then

\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^N ar^n = \begin{cases} a\dfrac{1-r^{N+1}}{1-r} & \text{if $r\ne 1$} \\ a(N+1) & \text{if $r=1$} \end{cases}. \end{equation*}

Further, if \(|r|\lt1 \) then

\begin{equation*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty ar^n = \dfrac{a}{1-r}. \end{equation*}

\begin{align*} f(x)&=2^x\\ f'(x)&=2^x\log 2\\ f''(x)&=2^x\left(\log 2\right)^2\\ f^{(3)}(x)&=2^x\left(\log 2\right)^3\\ f^{(4)}(x)&=2^x\left(\log 2\right)^4\\ f^{(5)}(x)&=2^x\left(\log 2\right)^5\\ \end{align*}

So, in general,

\begin{align*} f^{(k)}(x)&=2^x\left(\log 2\right)^k\\ \end{align*}

We notice that this formula works even when \(k=0\) and \(k=1\text{.}\) When \(x=1\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} f^{(k)}(1)&=2\left(\log 2\right)^k\\ \end{align*}

The \(n\)th degree Taylor polynomial of \(f(x)\) about \(x=1\) is

\begin{align*} T_{n}(x)&=\sum_{k=0}^n \frac{f^{(k)}(1)}{k!}(x-1)^k\\ &=\sum_{k=0}^n \frac{2(\log 2)^k}{k!}(x-1)^k \end{align*}

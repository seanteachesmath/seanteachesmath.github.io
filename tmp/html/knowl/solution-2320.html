Solution 15.2.6.45.1.

Pressure is force per unit surface area acting normally into a surface. So the force per unit surface area is \(-p\hn\text{.}\) The total force acting on \(S\) is

\begin{equation*} -\dblInt_S p\hn\,\dee{S}=-\tripInt_E \nabla p\ \dee{V} \end{equation*}

We are assuming that \(p\) is a constant, so that \(\nabla p=0\) and the total force is zero.

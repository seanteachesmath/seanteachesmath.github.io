\begin{align*} \frac{1}{2}&=\frac{2r}{h}\\ r&=\frac{h}{4}\\ \end{align*}

Using this, we find our equation for the volume of the water in terms of \(h\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} V(h)&=\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{h}{4}\right)^2h=\frac{h^3}{48}\\ \end{align*}

Differentiating,

\begin{align*} V'(h)&=\frac{h^2}{16}\\ V'(0.5)&=\frac{0.25}{16}=\frac{1}{64}\\ \end{align*}

Finally, using the approximation \(\Delta V \approx -0.05V'(0.5)\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \Delta V &\approx \frac{-0.05}{64}=-\frac{1}{1280}\approx -0.00078\;\mathrm{m}^3 \end{align*}

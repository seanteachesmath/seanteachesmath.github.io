Let us return to limits (Chapter 2) and see how we can use derivatives to simplify certain families of limits called indeterminate forms. We know, from Theorem 2.4.3 on the arithmetic of limits, that if

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\rightarrow a}f(x) &= F & \lim_{x\rightarrow a}g(x) &= G\\ \end{align*}

and \(G\ne 0\text{,}\) then

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\rightarrow a}\frac{f(x)}{g(x)} &= \frac{F}{G} \end{align*}

The requirement that \(G\ne 0\) is critical — we explored this in Example 2.4.7. Please reread that example.

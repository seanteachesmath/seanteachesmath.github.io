(a) Consider the circle \(\cC\) in the figure (a) on the left below, oriented clockwise. The vector field \(\vF\) is in the same direction as \(\diff{\vr}{t}\) at every point of the curve. So \(\vF\cdot\diff{\vr}{t} \gt 0\) at every point of \(\cC\) and \(\cC\) is a closed curve with \(\oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} \gt 0\text{.}\) As a consequence \(\vF\) is not conservative.

(a)

(b)

(b) Consider the square in the figure (b) on the right above, oriented counterclockwise. It consists of the four line segments \(L_1\text{,}\) \(L_2\text{,}\) \(L_3\) and \(L_4\text{.}\) On all of \(L_1\text{,}\) \(L_2\text{,}\) \(L_3\) we have that \(\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\vr'(t)=0\) because the vector field is perpendicular to the line segment. On \(L_4\) we have \(\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\vr'(t) \gt 0\text{.}\) So

\begin{align*} \oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &= \int_{L_1}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_3}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_4}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}\\ & =0+0+0+\int_{L_4}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} \gt 0 \end{align*}

So \(\cC\) is a closed curve with \(\oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} \gt 0\) and \(\vF\) is not conservative.

(c) Consider the square in the figure (c) on the left below, oriented counterclockwise. It consists of the four line segments \(L_1\text{,}\) \(L_2\text{,}\) \(L_3\) and \(L_4\text{.}\) On \(L_1\) and \(L_3\) we have that the dot product \(\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\vr'(t)=0\) because the vector field is perpendicular to the line segment. On \(L_2\) we have \(\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\vr'(t) \lt 0\) while on \(L_4\) we have \(\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\vr'(t) \gt 0\text{.}\) The vector field \(\vF\) is longer on \(L_4\) than on \(L_2\text{.}\) So \(\vF\big(\vr(t)\big)\cdot\vr'(t)\) has a larger magnitude on \(L_4\) than \(L_2\) and

\begin{align*} \oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &= \int_{L_1}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_3}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} +\int_{L_4}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}\\ & =0+\int_{L_2}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}+0 +\int_{L_4}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} \gt 0 \end{align*}

So \(\cC\) is a closed curve with \(\oint_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} \gt 0\) and \(\vF\) is not conservative.

(c)

(d)

(d) We are told that one of the four vector fields is conservative. Only the vector field in (d) is left, so it is conservative.

Remark: We can verify that vector field (d) is indeed conservative by observing (look at the figure (d) on the right above) that the \(\hi\) component of the vector field is exactly zero and that the \(\hj\) component depends only on \(y\text{.}\) So the vector field is of the form

\begin{equation*} \vF(x,y) = a(y)\,\hj \end{equation*}

for some function \(a(y)\text{.}\) If \(A(y)\) is any antiderivative of \(a(y)\text{,}\) we have \(\vF=\vnabla A\text{,}\) so that \(\vF\) is conservative with potential \(A(y)\text{.}\)

Solution 1:

When \(a\) approaches 0 from the right, the numerator approaches negative infinity, and the denominator approaches \(-1\text{.}\) So, \(\ds\lim_{a \to 0^+}\dfrac{a^2-\frac{1}{a}}{a-1}=\infty\text{.}\)

More precisely, using Theorem 2.5.9:

\begin{align*} &\lim_{a \to 0^+} \frac{1}{a}=+\infty\\ \mbox{Also,}& \lim_{a \to 0^+} a^2=0 \end{align*}

So, using Theorem 2.5.9,

\begin{align*} & \lim_{a \to 0^+} a^2-\frac{1}{a}=-\infty\\ \text{Furthermore,}&\lim_{a \to 0^+}a-1=-1\\ \text{So, using our theorem,}&\lim_{a \to 0^+}\frac{a^2-\frac{1}{a}}{a-1}=\infty \end{align*}

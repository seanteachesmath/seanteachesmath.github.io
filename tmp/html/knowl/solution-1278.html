Solution 8.2.2.27.1.

Using logarithm rules, we see

\begin{equation*} \displaystyle\sum_{n=5}^\infty \log\left(\frac{n-3}{n}\right)= \displaystyle\sum_{n=5}^\infty \big[\log(n-3)-\log n\big] \end{equation*}

which looks like a telescoping series. Let's make a table to figure out the partial sums.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{c||cc||c} \mathbf{n}&\mathbf{\log(n\!-\!3)}\!\!&\mathbf{-\log n}&\mathbf{s_n}\\ \hline 5&\log 2&\color{red}{-\log 5}&\log 2 - \color{red}{\log 5}\\ 6&\log 3&\color{green}{-\log 6}&\log 2 +\log 3- \color{red}{\log 5}\color{green}{-\log 6}\\ 7&\log 4&\color{purple}{-\log 7}&\log 2 +\log 3+\log 4- \color{red}{\log 5}\color{green}{-\log 6} \color{purple}{-\log 7}\\ 8&\color{red}{\log 5} &\color{blue}{-\log 8} &\log 2 +\log 3+\log 4 \color{green}{-\log 6} \color{purple}{-\log 7}\color{blue}{-\log 8}\\ 9&\color{green}{\log 6}&\color{orange}{-\log 9}&\log 2 +\log 3+\log 4 \color{purple}{-\log 7}\color{blue}{-\log 8}\color{orange}{-\log 9}\\ 10&\color{purple}{\log 7}&\color{brown}{-\log 10}&\log 2 +\log 3+\log 4 \color{blue}{-\log 8}\color{orange}{-\log 9}\color{brown}{-\log 10}\\ 11&\color{blue}{\log 8}&\color{yellow}{-\log 11}&\log 2 +\log 3+\log 4 \color{orange}{-\log 9}\color{brown}{-\log 10}\color{yellow}{-\log 11}\\ \vdots&&& \end{array} \end{equation*}

There is a “lag” before the terms cancel, which is why they “build up” more than we saw in past examples. Still, we can clearly see the \(N\)th partial sum:

\begin{align*} \amp\sum_{n=5}^N \Big(\log(n-3)-\log(n)\Big)\\ &\hskip0.5in=\log 2+\log 3 + \log 4 - \log(N-2)-\log(N-1)-\log(N)\\ &\hskip0.5in=\log \left(\frac{24}{N(N-1)(N-2)}\right)\\ \end{align*}

when \(N \geq 7\text{.}\) So,

\begin{align*} \sum_{n=5}^\infty \Big(\log(n-3)-\log(n)\Big)&= \lim_{N \to \infty}s_N\\ &=\lim_{N \to \infty}\log \left(\frac{24}{N(N-1)(N-2)}\right)\\ &=-\infty \end{align*}

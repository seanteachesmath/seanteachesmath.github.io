Let \(C\) be the part of the curve of intersection of the parabolic cylinder \(x = y^2\) and the hyperbolic paraboloid \(3z = 2xy\) with \(y\ge 0\text{.}\)

  1. Write a vector parametric equation for \(C\) using \(x\) as the parameter.
  2. Find the length of the part of \(C\) between the origin and the point \((9, 3, 18)\text{.}\)
  3. A particle moves along \(C\) in the direction for which \(x\) is increasing. If the particle moves with constant speed 9, find its velocity vector when it is at the point \((1, 1, \frac{2}{3})\text{.}\)
  4. Find the acceleration vector of the particle of part (c) when it is at the point \((1, 1, \frac{2}{3})\text{.}\)

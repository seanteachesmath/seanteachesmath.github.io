We now return to the claim, made in §12.9 on central forces, that if \(\vr(t)\) obeys Newton's inverse square law

\begin{equation*} \difftwo{\vr}{t} = -\frac{GM}{r^3}\vr = -\frac{GM}{r^2}\hat\vr \end{equation*}

then the curve obeys Kepler's laws

  1. \(\vr(t)\) runs over an ellipse having one focus at the origin and
  2. \(\vr(t)\) sweeps out equal areas in equal times and
  3. the square of the period is proportional to the cube of the major axis of the ellipse.

We just showed, in §12.9, that the fact that \(-\frac{GM}{r^3}\vr\) is parallel to \(\vr\) implies that \(\vr(t)\) lies in a plane through the origin and sweeps out equal area in equal times. We now verify the remaining Kepler laws.

Item.
Consider the definition of the definite integral
\begin{align*} \int_a^b f(x) \dee{x} &= \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_{i,n}^*)\cdot\frac{b-a}{n} \end{align*}
If we now substitute \(b=a\) in this expression we have
\begin{align*} \int_a^a f(x) \dee{x} &= \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n f(x_{i,n}^*)\cdot\underbrace{\frac{a-a}{n}}_{=0}\\ &= \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{i=1}^n \underbrace{f(x_{i,n}^*)\cdot 0}_{=0}\\ &= \lim_{n \to \infty} 0\\ &= 0 \end{align*}
as required.

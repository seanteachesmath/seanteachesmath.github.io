Consider the vector field \(\vF\) defined as

\begin{equation*} \vF(x,y,z) = \Big( (1 + ax^2 )ye^{3x^2} - bxz \cos(x^2 z)\,,\, xe^{3x^2} \,,\, x^2 \cos(x^2 z) \Big) \end{equation*}

where \(a\) and \(b\) are real valued constants.

  1. Compute \(\vnabla\times\vF\text{.}\)
  2. Determine for which values \(a\) and \(b\) the vector field \(\vF\) is conservative.
  3. For the values of \(a\) and \(b\) obtained in part (b), find a potential function \(f\) such that \(\vnabla f = \vF\text{.}\)
  4. Evaluate the line integral
    \begin{equation*} \int_C \Big(ye^{3x^2} + 2xz\cos(x^2 z)\Big) \dee{x} + xe^{3x^2} \dee{y} + x^2 \cos(x^2 z) \dee{z} \end{equation*}
    where \(C\) is the arc of the curve \((t, t, t^3)\) starting at the point \((0, 0, 0)\) and ending at the point \((1, 1, 1)\text{.}\)

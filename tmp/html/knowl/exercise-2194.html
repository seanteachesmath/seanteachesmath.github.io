Exercise 12. M317 2010A.

Let \(\vF(x,y,z) = \vr/r^3\) where \(\vr= x\,\hi + y\,\hj + z\,\hk\) and \(r = |\vr|\text{.}\)

  1. Find \(\vnabla \cdot \vF\text{.}\)
  2. Find the flux of \(\vF\) outwards through the spherical surface \(x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = a^2\text{.}\)
  3. Do the results of (a) and (b) contradict the divergence theorem? Explain your answer.
  4. Let \(E\) be the solid region bounded by the surfaces \(z^2 - x^2 - y^2 + 1 = 0\text{,}\) \(z = 1\) and \(z = -1\text{.}\) Let \(\sigma\) be the bounding surface of \(E\text{.}\) Determine the flux of \(\vF\) outwards through \(\sigma\text{.}\)
  5. Let \(R\) be the solid region bounded by the surfaces \(z^2 - x^2 - y^2 + 4y - 3 = 0\text{,}\) \(z = 1\) and \(z = - 1\text{.}\) Let \(\Sigma\) be the bounding surface of \(R\text{.}\) Determine the flux of \(\vF\) outwards through \(\Sigma\text{.}\)

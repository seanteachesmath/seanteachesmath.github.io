(a) Let \(v(t)\) be the speed of the car at time \(t\text{.}\) Then, by the trapezoidal rule with \(a=0\text{,}\) \(b=2\text{,}\) \(\De t=1/3\text{,}\) the distance traveled is

\begin{align*} \int_0^2 v(t)\,dt &\approx\De t\Big[\half v(0)+v(1/3)+v(2/3)+v(3/3) +v(4/3)\\ \amp\hskip2.5in+v(5/3)+\half v(2)\Big]\\ &=\frac{1}{3}\Big[\half 50+70+80+55+60+80+\half 40\Big] =130\text{ km} \end{align*}

A particle of mass \(m = 1\) has position \(\vr(0) = \hj\) and velocity \(\vv_0 = \hi + \hk\) at time \(t = 0\text{.}\) The particle moves under a force

\begin{equation*} \vF(t) = \hj - \sin t\,\hk \end{equation*}

where \(t\) denotes time.

  1. Find the position \(\vr(t)\) of the particle as a function of \(t\text{.}\)
  2. Find the position \(\vr(t_1)\) of the particle when it crosses the plane \(x = \pi/2\) for the first time at \(t_1\text{.}\)
  3. Determine the work done by \(\vF\) in moving the particle from \(\vr(0)\) to \(\vr(t_1)\text{.}\)

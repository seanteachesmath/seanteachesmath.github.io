(f) True. Let \(S=\Set{\vr}{|\vr-\vc|=R}\) be a sphere. Denote by \(V=\Set{\vr}{|\vr-\vc|\le R}\) the ball whose boundary is \(S\text{.}\) Let \(H\) be one hemisphere of \(S\) with outward pointing normal and let \(H'\) be the other hemisphere of \(S\) with inward point normal. Then the boundary of \(V\text{,}\) with outward pointing normal, can be viewed as consisting of two parts, namely \(H\) and \(-H'\text{,}\) where by \(-H'\) we mean \(H'\) but with outward pointing normal. Then, by the divergence theorem

\begin{align*} \dblInt_H \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} - \dblInt_{H'} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\dblInt_{\partial V} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} \gt 0 \end{align*}

which implies that \(\dblInt_H \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} \gt \dblInt_{H'} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\text{.}\)

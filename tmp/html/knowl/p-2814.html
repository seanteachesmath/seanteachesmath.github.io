  1. We differentiate implicitly.
    \begin{align*} x^3y(x)+y(x)^3&=10 x\\ 3x^2y(x)+x^3y'(x)+3y(x)^2y'(x)&=10\\ \end{align*}

    Subbing in \(x=1\) and \(y(1)=2\) gives

    \begin{align*} (3)(1)( 2)+(1)y'(1)+(3)(4) y'(1)&=10\\ 13y'(1)&=4\\ y'(1)&=\frac{4}{13} \end{align*}

  2. From part 3.14.2.18.a, the slope of the curve at \(x=1,\ y=2\) is \(\dfrac{4}{13}\text{,}\) so the curve is increasing, but fairly slowly. The angle of the tangent line is \(\tan^{-1}\left(\frac{4}{13}\right)\approx 17^\circ\text{.}\) We are also told that \(y''(1) \lt 0\text{.}\) So the slope of the curve is decreasing as \(x\) passes through 1. That is, the line is more steeply increasing to the left of \(x=1\text{,}\) and its slope is decreasing (getting less steep, then possibly the slope even becomes negative) as we move past \(x=1\text{.}\)

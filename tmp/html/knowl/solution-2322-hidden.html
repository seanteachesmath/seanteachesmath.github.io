Note that, since \(z^2-2az = (z-a)^2 -a^2\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} S=\Set{(x,y,z}{ x^2+y^2 +(z-a)^2=4a^2,\ z\ge 0} \end{equation*}

Let \(V\) be the solid

\begin{equation*} V=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2+(z-a)^2\le 4a^2,\ z\ge 0} \end{equation*}

It is the interior of the sphere of radius \(2a\) centred on \((0,0,a)\text{.}\) The surface of \(V\) (with outward normal) is the union of \(S\) (with normal pointing away from the origin) and the disk

\begin{equation*} B=\Set{(x,y,0)}{x^2+y^2\le 3a^2} \end{equation*}

with normal \(-\hk\text{.}\) Hence, by the Divergence Theorem

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} &=\tripInt_V\vn\cdot\vF\,\dee{V}-\dblInt_B\vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &=\tripInt_V(2x+2y+1)\,\dee{V}-\dblInt_B(-3-x)\,\dee{S} \end{align*}

Both \(V\) and \(B\) are invariant under \(x\rightarrow -x\) and under \(y\rightarrow -y\text{,}\) so \(\tripInt_Vx\,\dee{V}=\tripInt_Vy\,\dee{V}=\dblInt_B x\,\dee{S}=0\) and

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} &=\tripInt_V \,\dee{V}+3\dblInt_B\,\dee{S} \end{align*}

To evaluate the integral over \(V\text{,}\) we note that \(z\) runs from \(0\) to \(3a\) and that the cross section of

\begin{equation*} V=\Set{(x,y,z)}{0\le z\le 3a,\ x^2+y^2\le 4a^2-(z-a)^2,\ z\ge 0} \end{equation*}

with fixed \(z\) is the circular disk \(x^2+y^2\le 4a^2-(z-a)^2 = 3a^2 +2a z - z^2\text{,}\) which has area \(\pi\big({\sqrt{3a^2+2az-z^2}\,}\big)^2\text{.}\) So

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\, \dee{S} & =\int_0^{3a}\pi\big({\sqrt{3a^2+2az-z^2}\,}\big)^2\,\dee{z}+3\,\text{Area}(B)\\ &=\pi\int_0^{3a}(3a^2+2az-z^2)\,\dee{z}+3\pi(3a^2)\\ &=\pi \left(3a^2\times 3a+2a\times\frac{9a^2}{2}-\frac{27a^3}{3}\right) +9\pi a^2\\ &= 9\pi a^3+9\pi a^2 \end{align*}

Solution 5.1.2.11.1.

Our function \(f(x)\) bears some resemblance to the derivative of arcsine, \(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} f(x)=\dfrac{7}{\sqrt{3-3x^2}}&=\dfrac{7}{\sqrt{3}}\left(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\right)\\ \diff{}{x}\left\{\frac{7}{\sqrt{3}}\arcsin(x)\right\}&=\frac{7}{\sqrt{3}}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-x^2}}\right)=f(x) \end{align*}

So, the most general antiderivative of \(f(x)\) is \(F(x)=\dfrac{7}{\sqrt{3}}\arcsin(x)+C\text{.}\)

Item 8.6.7.50.b.

We'll evaluate the given series at \(x=2\text{.}\) This yields the series

\begin{equation*} \sqrt[3]{2}=1+\frac{1}{6}+\sum_{n=2}^\infty(-1)^{n-1}\frac{(2)(5)(8)\cdots(3n-4)}{3^n\, n!}. \end{equation*}

This series is alternating, so if we use the partial sum \(S_N\text{,}\) our absolute error is at most

\begin{equation*} |a_{N+1}|=\frac{(2)(5)(8)\cdots(3N-1)}{3^{N+1}\, (N+1)!} \end{equation*}

(if \(N \ge 2\)). We want to know which value of \(N\) makes this at most 0.01. We test several values.

\(N\) \(|a_{N+1}|\)
3 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)}{3^4\cdot 4!}\approx 0.04\)
4 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)(11)}{3^5\, 5!}\approx 0.03\)
5 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)(11)(14)}{3^6\, 6!}\approx 0.023\)
6 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)(11)(14)(17)}{3^7\, 7!}\approx 0.019\)
7 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)(11)(14)(17)(20)}{3^8\, 8!}\approx 0.016\)
8 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)(11)(14)(17)(20)(23)}{3^9\, 9!}\approx 0.013\)
9 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)(11)(14)(17)(20)(23)(26)}{3^{10}\, 10!}\approx 0.012\)
10 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)(11)(14)(17)(20)(23)(26)(29)}{3^{11}\, 11!}\approx 0.0103\)
11 \(\dfrac{(2)(5)(8)(11)(14)(17)(20)(23)(26)(29)(32)}{3^{12}\, 12!}\approx 0.009\)

So, the approximation \(S_{11}\) has a sufficiently small error. That is, we would add up the first twelve terms.

(a) Think of

\begin{equation*} \vr(t) = (t,1) - (\sin t,\cos t) \end{equation*}

The \((t,1)\) part gives the position of the centre of the wheel at time \(t\text{.}\) The other part gives the position of the thumbtack with respect to the centre of the wheel. In particular,

  • at time \(t=0\text{,}\) \(\vr(0) = (0,0)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is on the ground (i.e. at \(y=0\)).
  • At time \(t=\pi\text{,}\) \(\vr(\pi) = (\pi,2)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is at its highest point (i.e. at \(y=2\)) and is above the centre of the wheel at \(x=\pi\text{.}\)
  • At time \(t=2\pi\text{,}\) \(\vr(2\pi) = (2\pi,0)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is back on the ground (i.e. at \(y=0\)) and is below the centre of the wheel at \(x=2\pi\text{.}\)
  • At time \(t=3\pi\text{,}\) \(\vr(3\pi) = (3\pi,2)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is again at its highest point (i.e. at \(y=2\)) and is above the centre of the wheel at \(x=3\pi\text{.}\)
  • At time \(t=4\pi\text{,}\) \(\vr(4\pi) = (4\pi,0)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is back on the ground (i.e. at \(y=0\)) and is below the centre of the wheel at \(x=4\pi\text{.}\)

(b) Since

\begin{align*} \vr(t) &= \big(t - \sin t\,,\, 1 - \cos t\big)\\ \vv(t) =\vr'(t)&= \big(1-\cos t\,,\, \sin t\big)\\ \diff{s}{t}(t)=|\vv(t)|&=\sqrt{2-2\cos t}\\ \va(t) = \vv'(t)&=\big(\sin t\,,\, \cos t\big)\\ \vv(t)\times \va(t)&=\det\left[\begin{matrix}\hi&\hj&\hk \\ 1-\cos t & \sin t & 0 \\ \sin t & \cos t & 0\end{matrix}\right] =\big(\cos t-1\big)\,\hk \end{align*}

the curvature

\begin{gather*} \ka(t) =\frac{|\vv(t)\times\va(t)|}{|\vv(t)|^3} =\frac{|\cos t-1|}{(2-2\cos t)^{3/2}} =\frac{1}{2^{3/2}\sqrt{1-\cos t}} \end{gather*}

(c) The radius of curvature at time \(t=\pi\) is

\begin{equation*} \rho(\pi) = \frac{1}{\ka(\pi)} = \frac{1}{1/2^{3/2}\sqrt{2}} =4 \end{equation*}

(d) At time \(\pi\text{,}\) the tack is at \(\vr(\pi)=(\pi,2)\text{,}\) which is at the top of its trajectory. Looking at the sketch in part (a), we see that, at that time \(\hN(\pi) = -\hj\text{.}\) So the osculating circle at time \(t=\pi\) has center

\begin{equation*} \vr(\pi) + \rho(\pi) \hN(\pi) =(\pi,2) + 4(0,-1) =(\pi,-2) \end{equation*}

and radius \(\rho(\pi)=4\text{.}\) So the equation of the osculating circle at time \(\pi\) is

\begin{equation*} (x-\pi)^2 +(y+2)^2 = 16 \end{equation*}

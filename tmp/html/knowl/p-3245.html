Differential equations play a central role in modelling a huge number of different phenomena, including the motion of particles, electromagnetic radiation, financial options, ecosystem populations and nerve action potentials. Most universities offer half a dozen different undergraduate courses on various aspects of differential equations. We are barely going to scratch the surface of the subject. At this point we are going to restrict ourselves to a few very simple differential equations for which we can just guess the solution.

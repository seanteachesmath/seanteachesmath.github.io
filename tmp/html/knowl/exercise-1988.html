Exercise 29. M317 2000D.

A skier descends the hill \(z =\sqrt{4-x^2-y^2}\) along a trail with parameterization

\begin{equation*} x=\sin(2\theta),\qquad y=1-\cos(2\theta),\qquad z=2\cos\theta,\qquad 0\le\theta\le\frac{\pi}{2} \end{equation*}

Let \(P\) denote the point on the trail where \(x = 1\text{.}\)

  1. Find the vectors \(\hat\vT, \hat\vN, \hat\vB\) and the curvature \(\ka\) of the ski trail at the point \(P\text{.}\)

  2. The skier's acceleration at \(P\) is \(\va = (-2, 3, -2\sqrt{2})\text{.}\) Find, at \(P\text{,}\)

    1. the rate of change of the skier's speed and
    2. the skier's velocity (a vector).

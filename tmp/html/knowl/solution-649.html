Solution 4.7.4.6.1.

We rearrange the expression to a more natural form:

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}(\log x)^2e^{-x} &=\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}\underbrace{\dfrac{(\log x)^2}{e^{x}}}_{\atp {\mathrm{num}\to\infty} {\mathrm{den}\to\infty}} \end{align*}

Both the numerator and denominator go to infinity as \(x\) goes to infinity. So, we can apply l'HÃ´pital's Rule. In fact, we end up applying it twice.

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}(\log x)^2e^{-x} &=\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}\underbrace{\dfrac{2\log x}{xe^{x}}}_{\atp {\mathrm{num}\to\infty} {\mathrm{den}\to\infty}}\\ &=\lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}\dfrac{2/x}{xe^{x}+e^x} \end{align*}

The numerator gets smaller and smaller while the denominator gets larger and larger, so:

\begin{align*} \lim_{x\rightarrow\infty}(\log x)^2e^{-x} &={0} \end{align*}

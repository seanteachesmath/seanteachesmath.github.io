Solution 1: Define \(x=\frac{1}{n}\text{,}\) and \(f(x)=\left(1+3x+5x^2\right)^{1/x}\text{.}\) Then \(b_n=f\left(\frac{1}{n}\right)=f(x)\text{,}\) and

\begin{equation*} \lim_{n \to \infty} f\left(\frac{1}{n}\right) = \lim_{x \to 0^+}f(x). \end{equation*}

If this limit exists, it is equal to \(\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}b_n\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \lim_{x \to 0^+}f(x)&= \lim_{x \to 0^+} \left(1+3x+5x^2\right)^{1/x}\\ \lim_{x \to 0^+}\log[f(x)]&= \lim_{x \to 0^+} \log\left[\left(1+3x+5x^2\right)^{1/x}\right]\\ \amp=\underbrace{ \lim_{x \to 0^+}\frac{ \log\left[1+3x+5x^2 \right]}{x}}_{\atp{\mathrm{num}\to 0}{\mathrm{den}\to0}}\\ &=\lim_{x \to 0^+}\frac{\frac{3+10x}{1+3x+5x^2}}{1}=3\\ \lim_{x \to 0^+}f(x)&=e^3 \end{align*}

Since the limit exists, \(\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}b_n=e^3\text{.}\)

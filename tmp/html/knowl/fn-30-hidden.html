One needs to be very careful making statements about infinity. At some point in our lives we get around to asking ourselves “what is the biggest number”, and we realise there isn't one. That is, we can go on counting integer after integer, for ever and not stop. Indeed the set of integers is the first infinite thing we really encounter. It is an example of a countably infinite set. The set of real-numbers is actually much bigger and is uncountably infinite. In fact there are an infinite number of different sorts of infinity! Much of the theory of infinite sets was developed by Georg Cantor; we mentioned him back in Section 1.2 and he is well worth googling.

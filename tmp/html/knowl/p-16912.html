(e) Yes. Note that

  • \(\displaystyle \big(-\sqrt{2-z^2}\sin \phi\big)^2 +\big(\sqrt{2-z^2}\cos \phi\big)^2 +\big(z\big)^2=2\)
  • For \(1\le z\le\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) we have obviously have \(z \gt 0\text{.}\)
  • As \(z\) runs from \(1\) to \(\sqrt{2}\text{,}\) \(r(z)=\sqrt{2-z^2}\) runs from \(1\) to \(0\text{,}\) so that \(\big(x=-r(z)\sin\phi\,,\,y=r(z)\cos\phi\big)\) covers all of \(x^2+y^2\le1\) as \(z\) runs from \(1\) to \(\sqrt{2}\) and \(\phi\) runs from \(0\) to \(2\pi\text{.}\)

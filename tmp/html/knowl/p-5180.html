For any constant \(n \neq -1\text{,}\) an antiderivative of \(x^n\) is \(\frac{1}{n+1}x^{n+1}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} F'(x)&=3x^2+5x^4+10x-9\\ F(x)&=3\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)x^3+5\left(\frac{1}{5}\right)x^5+10\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)x^2-9\left(\frac{1}{1}\right)x^1+C\\ &=x^3+x^5+5x^2-9x+C \end{align*}

(f) When \(\theta=0\text{,}\) \(r=\theta=0\text{.}\) As

\begin{equation*} \diff{}{\theta} \theta =1 \qquad\text{for all }\theta \end{equation*}

\(r=\theta\) increases as \(\theta\) increases for all \(\theta\ge 0\text{.}\) Furthermore the rate of increase is independent of \(\theta\text{.}\) So \(r\) starts at \(r=0\) when \(\theta=0\) and increases at a constant rate as \(\theta\) increases. That matches figure (D).

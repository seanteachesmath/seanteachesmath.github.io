Call the silo \(V\text{.}\) Call the sides and top of the silo \(S\text{.}\) Call the base of the silo (namely, \(x^2+y^2\le 1\text{,}\) \(z=0\)) \(B\text{.}\) By the divergence theorem,

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vV\cdot\hn\ \dee{S} + \dblInt_B \vV\cdot(-\hk)\ \dee{S} &=\tripInt_V\vnabla\cdot\vV\ \dee{V}\\ \dblInt_S \vV\cdot\hn\ \dee{S} - \dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1} (x^2+y)\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} &=\tripInt_V(2xyz+z)\ \dee{V} \end{align*}

By oddness under \(Y\rightarrow -y\text{,}\) \(\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1} y\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} = \tripInt_V xyz\ \dee{V}=0\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vV\cdot\hn\ \dee{S} &=\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1} x^2\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y} +\tripInt_V z\ \dee{V}\\ &=\int_0^1 \dee{r}\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ r\ (\overbrace{r\cos\theta}^{x})^2 +\tripInt_V z\ \dee{V} \end{align*}

We can evaluate the volume integral by decomposing \(V\) into thin horizontal pancakes. See Section 1.6 in the CLP-2 text. For \(0\le z\le 1\text{,}\) the horizontal cross-section of the silo at height \(z\) is a circle of radius \(1\) and hence of area \(\pi\text{.}\) For \(z\ge1\text{,}\) the horizontal cross-section of the silo at height \(z\) is again a circle. Its radius is determined by the equation \(x^2+ y^2+ z^2 = 2\) of the top of the silo. The radius is \(\sqrt{2-z^2}\text{,}\) so the cross-section has area \(\pi(2-z^2)\text{.}\) The biggest that \(z\) can get is \(\sqrt{2}\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vV\cdot\hn\ \dee{S} &=\int_0^1 \dee{r}\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta} \ r\ (r\cos\theta)^2+\int_0^1 dz\ \pi z +\int_1^{\sqrt{2}}dz\ \pi(2-z^2) z\\ &=\left[\int_0^1 \dee{r}\ r^3\right] \left[\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta} \ \frac{\cos(2\theta)+1}{2}\right]+\int_0^1 dz\ \pi z\\ &\hskip1in+\int_1^{\sqrt{2}}dz\ \pi(2-z^2) z\\ &=\left[\frac{r^4}{4}\right]_0^1\pi +\left[\pi\frac{z^2}{2}\right]_0^1 +\pi\left[z^2-\frac{z^4}{4}\right]_1^{\sqrt{2}}\\ &=\frac{\pi}{4}+\frac{\pi}{2}+\pi\left[1-\frac{3}{4}\right]\\ &=\pi \end{align*}

For an efficient, sneaky, way to evaluate \(\int_0^{2\pi} \cos^2 \theta\ \dee{\theta}\text{,}\) see Example 13.4.4.

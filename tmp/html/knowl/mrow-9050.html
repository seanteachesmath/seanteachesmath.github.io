\begin{align*} \diff{v}{t}&=-k\,v^2\\ -\frac{\dee{v}}{v^2}&=k\,\dee{t}\\ \int- \frac{\dee{v}}{v^2}&=\int k\,\dee{t}\\ \frac{1}{v}&=kt+C\\ \end{align*}

At \(t=0\text{,}\) \(v=40\) so

\begin{align*} \frac{1}{40}&=k\times 0+ C\\ C&=\frac{1}{40}\\ \end{align*}

Therefore,

\begin{align*} v(t)&=\frac{1}{kt+C}=\frac{1}{kt+1/40}=\frac{40}{40kt+1}\tag{$*$}\\ \end{align*}

The constant of proportionality \(k\) is determined by

\begin{align*} v(10)&=20\\ 20&=\frac{40}{40k\times 10+1}\\ \frac{1}{2}&=\frac{1}{400k+1}\\ 400k+1&=2\\ k&=\frac{1}{400} \end{align*}

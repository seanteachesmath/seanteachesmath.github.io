Solution 6.11.6.10.1.

According to TheoremÂ 6.11.13 in the text, the error associated with the Simpson's rule approximation is no more than \(\dfrac{L}{180}\dfrac{(b-a)^5}{n^4}\text{,}\) where \(L\) is a constant such that \(|f^{(4)}(x)| \leq L\) for all \(x\) in \([a,b]\text{.}\) If \(L=0\text{,}\) then the error is no more than 0 regardless of \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) or \(n\)--that is, the approximation is exact.

Any polynomial \(f(x)\) of degree at most 3 has \(f^{(4)}(x)=0\) for all \(x\text{.}\) So, any polynomial of degree at most 3 is an acceptable answer. For example, \(f(x)=5x^3-27\text{,}\) or \(f(x)=x^2\text{.}\)

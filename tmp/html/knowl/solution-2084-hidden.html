From the text, the skateboarder will make it all the way around when \(\frac{5}{2}(5) \le\frac{E}{mg}\text{.}\) Energy \(E\) is given by \(E=\frac12m|\vv|^2+mgy\text{,}\) the sum of the kinetic and potential energy of the system. At \(y=0\text{,}\) all the energy is kinetic, so \(E=\frac12m|\vv|^2\text{,}\) where \(|\vv|\) is the skater's velocity at the bottom of the culvert.

So, we solve:

\begin{align*} \frac{25}{2}&\le\frac{E}{mg}=\frac{\frac12m|\vv|^2}{m\cdot9.8}\\ |\vv|&\ge5\sqrt{9.8} \end{align*}

So, a speed of \(5\sqrt{9.8}\) m/s or higher is needed. (That's about 56 kph.)

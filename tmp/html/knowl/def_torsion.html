Definition 12.4.1.
  1. The binormal vector at \(\vr(s)\) is \(\hat\vB(s) = \hat\vT(s)\times \hat\vN(s)\text{.}\) The normal vector \(\hat\vN(s)\) is sometimes called the unit principal normal vector to distinguish it from the binormal vector.
  2. We define the torsion \(\tau(s)\) by
    \begin{equation*} \diff{\hat\vB}{s}(s) = -\tau(s)\hat\vN(s) \end{equation*}
    The negative sign is included so that \(\tau(s) \gt 0\) indicates “right handed twisting”. There will be an explanation of what this means in Example 12.4.4 below.
  3. The osculating plane at \(\vr(s)\) (the plane that fits the curve best at \(\vr(s)\)) is the plane through \(\vr(s)\) with normal vector \(\hat\vB(s)\text{.}\) The equation of the plane is
    \begin{equation*} \hat\vB(s)\cdot\big\{(x,y,z)-\vr(s)\big\}=0 \end{equation*}

We are, however, free to choose \(n\) to be any positive integer we want. If we take \(n\) to be very large — in particular much much larger than \(a\) — then \(n!\) will be much much larger than \(a^{2n}\) (we showed this in Example 8.6.8), and consequently

\begin{gather*} 0 \lt I_n \lt \left(\frac{a^2}{b} \right)^n\frac{1}{n!} \lt 1 \end{gather*}

Which means that the integral cannot be an integer. This gives the required contradiction, showing that \(\pi\) is irrational.

When we take a very small interval from \(t\) to \(t+h\text{,}\) the change in arclength \(s(t+h)-s(t)\) is approximately \(|\vr(t+h)-\vr(t)|\text{,}\) because our curve is approximated by a straight line. So, \(\frac{s(t+h)-s(t)}{h} \approx \frac{|\vr(t+h)-\vr(t)|}{h}\text{,}\) leading to \(\diff{s}{t}=|\diff{\vr}{t}|=|\vv(t)|\text{.}\)

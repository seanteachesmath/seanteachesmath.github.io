Solution 2: Using the idea of Question 10, we express the series we're interested in as the difference of two series that we can easily evaluate.
\begin{align*} \sum_{k=7}^{\infty} \frac{1}{8^k}&= \sum_{k=0}^{\infty} \frac{1}{8^k}- \sum_{k=0}^{6} \frac{1}{8^k}\\ \end{align*}

Using Lemma 8.2.5,

\begin{align*} &=\frac{1}{1-\frac18}-\frac{1-\frac{1}{8^7}}{1-\frac18}\\ &=\frac{1}{7\times 8^6} \end{align*}

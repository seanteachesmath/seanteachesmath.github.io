We must also be able to treat an integral like \(\int_0^1\frac{\dee{x}}{x}\) that has a finite domain of integration but whose integrand is unbounded near one limit of integration  2  Our approach is similar — we sneak up on the problem. We compute the integral on a smaller domain, such as \(\int_t^1\frac{\dee{x}}{x}\text{,}\) with \(t \gt 0\text{,}\) and then take the limit \(t\rightarrow 0+\text{.}\)

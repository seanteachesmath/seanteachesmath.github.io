(a) Our object is subject to a central force. So the acceleration \(\va(t)\) is parallel to \(\vr(t)\) and

\begin{equation*} \diff{\ }{t}\big(\vr(t)\times\vv(t)\big) =\vv(t)\times\vv(t) + \vr(t)\times\va(t) =\vZero +\vZero =\vZero \end{equation*}

By Lemma 12.8.2, \(\vv(t) = \diff{r}{t}(t)\ \hat\vr\big(\theta(t)\big) + r(t)\ \diff{\theta}{t}(t)\ \hat\vth\big(\theta(t)\big)\text{.}\) Because \(\vr(t)=r(t)\hat\vr\big(\theta(t)\big)\) is parallel to \(\hat\vr\big(\theta(t)\big)\) and is perpendicular to \(\hat\vth\big(\theta(t)\big)\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \vr(t)\times\vv(t) =r(t)^2\dot\theta(t)\,\hat\vr\big(\theta(t)\big)\times \hat\vth\big(\theta(t)\big) \end{equation*}

and, in particular,

\begin{equation*} |\vr(t)\times\vv(t)| = r(t)^2|\dot\theta(t)| \end{equation*}

is a constant. As \(\dot\theta(t)\) is continuous, \(r(t)^2\dot\theta(t)\) is also constant.

Stokes' theorem, which is Theorem 15.4.1, says that \(\oint_C\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\dblInt_S\nabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\) for any surface \(S\) whose boundary is \(C\text{.}\) For the given vector field

\begin{align*} \nabla\times\vF(x,y,z) &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi &\hj &\hk\\ \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial y} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial z}\\ -z & x & y \end{matrix} \right]\\ &=\hi-\hj+\hk \end{align*}

Choose

\begin{align*} S &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{z=y,\ \tfrac{x^2}{4}+\tfrac{y^2}{2}+\tfrac{z^2}{2}\le 1}\\ &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{z=y,\ \tfrac{x^2}{4}+y^2\le 1} \end{align*}

to be the part of the plane \(z=y\) bounded by the ellipsoid.

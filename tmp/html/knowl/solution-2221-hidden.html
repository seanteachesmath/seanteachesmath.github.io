The upper half cone obeys \(f(x,y,z)=x^2+y^2-z^2=0\text{.}\) So, by (14.3.3),

\begin{equation*} \dee{S}=\left|\frac{\vnabla f}{\vnabla f\cdot\hk}\right|\dee{x}\,\dee{y} =\left|\frac{2x\hi+2y\hj-2z\hk}{-2z}\right|\dee{x}\,\dee{y} =\frac{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}}{z}\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}

But on the cone \(x^2+y^2=z^2\text{,}\) and \(z \gt 0\text{,}\) so that

\begin{equation*} \dee{S}=\frac{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+z^2}}{z}\dee{x}\,\dee{y} =\frac{\sqrt{2z^2}}{z}\dee{x}\,\dee{y} =\sqrt{2}\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{equation*}

and

\begin{align*} x^4-y^4+y^2z^2-z^2x^2+1 &=x^4-y^4+y^2\big(x^2+y^2\big)-\big(x^2+y^2)x^2+1 =1 \end{align*}

We have to integrate \((x,y)\) over the interior of \(x^2+y^2=2x\text{,}\) or equivalently, the interior of \((x-1)^2+y^2=1\text{,}\) which is the disk

\begin{equation*} D=\Set{(x,y)}{(x-1)^2+y^2\le 1} \end{equation*}

So

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S\overbrace{(x^4-y^4+y^2z^2-z^2x^2+1)}^{=1}\,\dee{S} &=\sqrt{2}\dblInt_D \dee{x}\,\dee{y} =\sqrt{2}\ \text{Area}(D) =\sqrt{2}\ \pi \end{align*}

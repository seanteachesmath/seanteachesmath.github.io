In cylindrical coordinates

\begin{equation*} x=r\cos\theta\qquad y=r\sin\theta\qquad z=z \end{equation*}

In these coordinates the equation, \(x^2+y^2=2ay\text{,}\) of the cylinder becomes

\begin{equation*} r^2=2ar\sin\theta\qquad\text{or}\qquad r=2a\sin\theta \end{equation*}

That is, \(r=f(\theta)\) with \(f(\theta) =2a\sin\theta\text{.}\) Parametrize the cylinder by \(\vr(\theta,z) = x(\theta,z)\,\hi + y(\theta,z)\,\hj + z(\theta,z)\,\hk\) with

\begin{align*} x(\theta,z)&=f(\theta)\cos\theta=2a\sin\theta\cos\theta=a\sin2\theta\\ y(\theta,z)&=f(\theta)\sin\theta=2a\sin\theta\sin\theta=a(1-\cos2\theta)\\ z(\theta,z)&=z \end{align*}

Under this parametrization,

\begin{align*} &\pdiff{\vr}{\theta}=2a\cos2\theta\,\hi+2a\sin2\theta\,\hj\qquad \pdiff{\vr}{z}=\hk\\ &\implies \pdiff{\vr}{\theta}\times\pdiff{\vr}{z} =-2a\cos2\theta\,\hj+2a\sin2\theta\,\hi\\ &\implies \dee{S}=\left|\pdiff{\vr}{\theta}\times\pdiff{\vr}{z}\right|\, \dee{\theta}\,\dee{z} =2a\,\dee{\theta}\,\dee{z} \end{align*}

We still have to determine the limits of integration. The figure on the left below provides a top view of the cylinder.

From it we see that \(0\le\theta\le\pi\text{.}\) The cone \(z^2=x^2+y^2=r^2\) (i.e. \(z=\pm r\)) and the cylinder \(r=2a\sin\theta\) intersect at \(z^2=r^2=4a^2\sin^2\theta\text{.}\) So, for each fixed \(\theta\text{,}\) \(z\) runs from \(-2a\sin\theta\) to \(z=+2a\sin\theta\text{.}\) (See the figure on the right above. It shows a constant \(\theta\) cross-section.) Finally,

\begin{align*} {\rm Area} &= \int_{|z|\le 2a\sin\theta}2a\,\dee{\theta}\,\dee{z} =2a\int_0^\pi \!\!\dee{\theta}\int_{-2a\sin\theta}^{2a\sin\theta} \!\!\!\dee{z} =8a^2\int_0^\pi \!\!\dee{\theta}\ \sin\theta\\ &=8a^2\Big[-\cos\theta\Big]_0^\pi\\ &=16a^2 \end{align*}

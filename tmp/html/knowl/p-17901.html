(a) Let \(\cS\) denote the boundary of \(\cR\text{.}\) Then “the total flux of \(\vF = \nabla u\) out through the boundary of \(\cR\)” is given by the integral

\begin{equation*} I = \dblInt_\cS \vF\cdot \hn\,\dee{S} \end{equation*}

Thanks to the divergence theorem,

\begin{align*} I &= \tripInt_\cR \nabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} = \tripInt_\cR \nabla\cdot\nabla u\,\dee{V} = \tripInt_\cR \Big(\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} +\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} +\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial z^2}\Big)\,\dee{V}\\ &= 0 \end{align*}

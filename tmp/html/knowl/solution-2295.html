Solution 15.2.6.20.1.

The divergence of \(\vF\text{,}\) namely,

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &= \pdiff{}{x}\big(x^2 z +\cos \pi y\big) +\pdiff{}{y}\big(yz +\sin \pi z\big) +\pdiff{}{z}\big(x-y^2\big)\\ &=2xz +z \end{align*}

is a lot simpler than \(\vF\) itself. So let's use the divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.2).

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot \hn\,\dee{S} =\tripInt_B \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} =\tripInt_B (2xz+z)\,\dee{V} \end{gather*}

As \(B\) is invariant under \(x\rightarrow-x\) while \(2xz\) is odd under \(x\rightarrow-x\text{,}\) the integral \(\tripInt_B 2xz\,\dee{V}\) is zero. To help set up the limits of integration for \(\tripInt_B z\,\dee{V}\text{,}\) note that, in \(B\text{,}\)

  • \((x,y)\) runs over the rectangle \(-1\le x\le 1\text{,}\) \(0\le y\le 2\) and
  • for each fixed \((x,y)\text{,}\) \(z\) runs over \(0\le z\le 3-y\text{.}\)

So

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot \hn\,\dee{S} &= \int_{-1}^1\dee{x}\int_0^2\dee{y}\int_0^{3-y}\dee{z}\ z\\ &= \frac{1}{2}\int_{-1}^1\dee{x}\int_0^2\dee{y}\ (3-y)^2\\ &=-\frac{1}{2}\int_{-1}^1\dee{x}\int_3^1\dee{u}\ u^2 \qquad \text{with } u = 3-y,\ \dee{u}=-\dee{y}\\ &=-\frac{1}{2}\int_{-1}^1\dee{x}\ \Big[\frac{1^3}{3} - \frac{3^3}{3}\Big]_{-1}^1\\ &=\frac{26}{3} \end{align*}

(a) (i) Here is a sketch of the part of the plane in question.

We can use \(x\) and \(y\) as parameters. As we can rewrite the equation of the plane as \(z = \frac{1}{3}\big(16-2x-4y\big)\text{,}\) we have the parametrization

\begin{equation*} \vr(x,y) = x\,\hi +y\,\hj +\frac{1}{3}\big(16-2x-4y\big)\,\hk \end{equation*}

In terms of \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) the condition \(z= \frac{1}{3}\big(16-2x-4y\big)\ge 0\) is \(16-2x-4y\ge 0\) or \(x+2y\le 8\text{.}\) So the domain is

\begin{equation*} \Set{(x,y)}{x\ge 0,\ y\ge 0,\ x+2y\le 8} \end{equation*}

Renaming \(x\) to \(u\) and \(y\) to \(v\text{,}\) the parametrization is also

\begin{equation*} \vr(u,v) = \Big(u\,,\, v\,,\, \frac{1}{3}(16-2u-4v)\Big)\,\hk\qquad u\ge 0,\ v\ge 0,\ u+2v\le 8 \end{equation*}

(a) (ii) Here is a sketch of the part of the cap in the first octant.

The full sphere can be parametrized, using spherical coordinates with \(\rho=4\text{,}\) by

\begin{align*} &\vr(\theta,\varphi) = 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi\,\hi +4\sin\theta\sin\varphi\,\hj +4\cos\varphi\,\hk\\ &0\le\theta\le 2\pi,\ 0\le\varphi\le \pi \end{align*}

In these coordinates, the condition \(4/\sqrt{2} \le z \le 4\) is

\begin{align*} \frac{4}{\sqrt{2}} \le 4\cos\varphi \le 4 &\iff \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \le \cos\varphi \le 1\\ &\iff 0\le\varphi\le\frac{\pi}{4} \end{align*}

So our parametrization is

\begin{align*} &\vr(\theta,\varphi) = 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi\,\hi +4\sin\theta\sin\varphi\,\hj +4\cos\varphi\,\hk\\ &0\le\theta\le 2\pi,\ 0\le\varphi\le \frac{\pi}{4} \end{align*}

Renaming \(\theta\) to \(u\) and \(\varphi\) to \(v\text{,}\) the parametrization is also

\begin{equation*} \vr(u,v) = \big(4\cos u\sin v\,,\, 4\sin u\sin v\,,\, 4\cos v\big)\qquad 0\le u\le 2\pi,\ 0\le v\le \frac{\pi}{4} \end{equation*}

(a) (iii) Here is a sketch of the hyperboloid.

If we use \(x\) and \(y\) as parameters, then, since \(z = \sqrt{1 + x^2 + y^2}\text{,}\) we have the parametrization

\begin{equation*} \vr(x,y) = x\,\hi +y\,\hj +\sqrt{1 + x^2 + y^2}\,\hk \end{equation*}

In terms of \(x\) and \(y\text{,}\) the condition \(1\le z\le 10\) is

\begin{equation*} 1\le \sqrt{1 + x^2 + y^2}\le 10\qquad \text{or}\qquad 0\le x^2+y^2\le 99 \end{equation*}

So the domain is

\begin{equation*} \Set{(x,y)}{x^2+y^2\le 99} \end{equation*}

Renaming \(x\) to \(u\) and \(y\) to \(v\text{,}\) the parametrization is also

\begin{equation*} \vr(u,v) = \big(u\,,\, v\,,\, \sqrt{1 + u^2 + v^2}\big)\qquad u^2+v^2\le 99 \end{equation*}

Alternatively, if we replace \(x\) and \(y\) with the polar coordinates \(r\) and \(\theta\text{,}\) we get the parametrization

\begin{equation*} \vr(r,\theta) = r\cos\theta\,\hi +r\sin\theta\,\hj +\sqrt{1 + r^2}\,\hk \qquad 0\le\theta\le 2\pi,\ 0\le r\le\sqrt{99} \end{equation*}

Renaming \(r\) to \(u\) and \(\theta\) to \(v\text{,}\) the parametrization is also

\begin{equation*} \vr(u,v) = \big(u\cos v\,,\, u\sin v\,,\, \sqrt{1 + u^2}\big)\qquad 0\le v\le 2\pi,\ 0\le u\le\sqrt{99} \end{equation*}

(b) Let's use the parametrization

\begin{align*} &\vr(\theta,\varphi) = 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi\,\hi +4\sin\theta\sin\varphi\,\hj +4\cos\varphi\,\hk\\ &0\le\theta\le 2\pi,\ 0\le\varphi\le \frac{\pi}{4} \end{align*}

from part (a) (ii), so that

\begin{align*} \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\theta}\times\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\varphi} &= \det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi & \hj & \hk\\ -4\sin\theta\sin\varphi & 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi & 0\\ 4\cos\theta\cos\varphi & 4\sin\theta\cos\varphi & -4\sin\varphi \end{matrix}\right]\\ &= -16\big(\cos\theta\sin^2\varphi\,,\, \sin\theta\sin^2\varphi\,,\, \sin\varphi\cos\varphi\big) \end{align*}

and, by (14.3.1),

\begin{align*} \dee{S} &= \left|\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\theta}\times \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\varphi}\right|\,\dee{\theta}\dee{\varphi}\\ &= 16\sin\varphi\sqrt{\cos^2\theta\sin^2\varphi + \sin^2\theta\sin^2\varphi +\cos^2\varphi}\,\dee{\theta}\dee{\varphi}\\ &= 16\sin\varphi\,\dee{\theta}\dee{\varphi} \end{align*}

So the area is

\begin{align*} \text{Area} =\dblInt_S \dee{S} &=\int_0^{\pi/4}\dee{\varphi}\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ 16\sin\varphi =32\pi \int_0^{\pi/4}\dee{\varphi}\ \sin\varphi\\ &=32\pi \Big[-\cos\varphi\Big]_0^{\pi/4}\\ &=32\pi\Big[1-\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\Big] \end{align*}

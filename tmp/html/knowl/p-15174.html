We are now going to study vector-valued functions of one real variable. That is, we are going to study functions that assign to each real number \(t\) (typically in some interval) a vector 1  \(\vr(t)\text{.}\) For example

\begin{equation*} \vr(t) = \big( x(t), y(t), z(t)\big) \end{equation*}

might be the position of a particle at time \(t\text{.}\) As \(t\) varies, \(\vr(t)\) sweeps out a curve.

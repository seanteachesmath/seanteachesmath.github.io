Solution 15.1.6.5.1.

(a) We are to compute the divergence of \(\frac{\vr}{r} =\frac{x\,\hi+y\,\hj+z\,\hk}{[x^2+y^2+z^2]^{1/2}}\text{.}\) Since

\begin{align*} \pdiff{}{x}\frac{x}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{1/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{1/2}} -\frac{1}{2}\frac{x(2x)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}}\\ &=\frac{y^2+z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}}\\ \pdiff{}{y}\frac{y}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{1/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{1/2}} -\frac{1}{2}\frac{y(2y)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}}\\ &=\frac{x^2+z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}}\\ \pdiff{}{z}\frac{z}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{1/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{1/2}} -\frac{1}{2}\frac{z(2z)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}}\\ &=\frac{x^2+y^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} \end{align*}

the specified divergence is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\left(\frac{\vr}{r}\right) &= \frac{2x^2+2y^2+2z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} =\frac{2r^2}{r^3} =\frac{2}{r} \end{align*}

(b)

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\big(yz\,\hi + 2xz\,\hj + e^{xy}\,\hk\big) &=\det\left[\begin{matrix}\hi&\hj&\hk\\ \pdiff{}{x}& \pdiff{}{y}& \pdiff{}{z}\\ yz&2xz&e^{xy}\end{matrix}\right]\\ &=\big(xe^{xy}-2x\big)\,\hi-\big(ye^{xy}-y\big)\,\hj+z\,\hk \end{align*}

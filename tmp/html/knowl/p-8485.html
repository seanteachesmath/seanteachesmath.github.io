If we hang 10 kg from the spring, gravity exerts a force of \((10 \text{ kg})(9.8 \text{ m/sec}^2) = 98 \frac{\text{kg}\cdot\text{m}}{\text{sec}^2}\text{.}\) This will be matched by the spring with a force of \(kx\) newtons, where \(k\) is the spring constant and \(x\) is the amount stretched.

\begin{align*} kx&=98 \frac{\text{kg}\cdot\text{m}}{\text{sec}^2}\\ \left(980 \frac{\text{kg}}{\text{sec}^2}\right)\left(x\text{ m}\right)&=98 \frac{\text{kg}\cdot\text{m}}{\text{sec}^2}\\ x&=\frac{1}{10}\text{ m} = 10\text{ cm} \end{align*}

So, we should put the mark at 10 cm below the natural length of the spring.

Finally to verify the claim (E2), we check that the vector field \(\vG=\vF-\alpha_\vF\vTh\) is conservative on \(\cD\text{.}\) To do so, it suffices to check that \(\oint_{\cC}\vG\cdot \dee{\vr}=0\) for any closed curve \(\cC\) in \(\cD\text{.}\) In fact we can restrict our attention to curves \(\cC\) that are simple, closed, counterclockwise oriented curves on \(\cD\text{.}\) A curve is called simple if it does not cross itself. Closed curves which are not simple can be split up into simple closed subcurves. And changing the orientation of \(\cC\) just changes the sign of \(\oint_{\cC}\vG\cdot \dee{\vr}=0\text{,}\) which does not affect whether it is zero or not.

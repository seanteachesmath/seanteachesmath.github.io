For any \(d\times d\) matrix \(\cV\text{,}\) the solution of

\begin{equation*} \vb'(t)=\cV\vb(t)\quad \vb(t_0)=\ve \end{equation*}

is

\begin{equation*} \vb(t)=e^{\cV (t-t_0)}\ve \end{equation*}

where the exponential of a \(d\times d\) matrix \(B\) is defined by the power series

\begin{equation*} e^B=\bbbone+B+\half B^2+\frac{1}{3!}B^3+\cdots=\smsum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{1}{n!}B^n \end{equation*}

with \(\bbbone\) denoting the \(d\times d\) identity matrix. This sum converges 4

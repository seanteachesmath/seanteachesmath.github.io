The trick exploits the fact that most polynomials that appear in homework assignments and on tests have integer coefficients and some integer roots. Any integer root of a polynomial that has integer coefficients, like \(D(x)=x^3+3x^2+7x+5\text{,}\) must divide the constant term of the polynomial exactly.

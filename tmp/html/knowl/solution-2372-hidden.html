• \(\cS\) be the surface specified in the question, with upward pointing normal, and
  • \(\cD\) be the disk \(\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2\le 4,\ z=0}\text{,}\) with normal \(\hn=\hk\text{,}\) and
  • \(\cC\) be the circle \(\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2=4,\ z=0}\text{,}\) oriented as in the figure below.

Note that \(\cC\) is the boundary curve for both \(\cS\) and \(\cD\text{.}\) So, by Stokes' theorem, twice

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{\cS}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\int_{\cC}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\dblInt_{\cD}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_{\cD}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hk\,\dee{S} \end{align*}

Now the \(\hk\) component of \(\vnabla\times\vF\) is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hk &=\pdiff{}{x}\big[x(3+y)\big] - \pdiff{}{y}\big[x\ln(1+z)\big] =3+y \end{align*}

As \(y\) is odd under \(y\rightarrow-y\) and \(\cD\) is invariant under \(y\rightarrow-y\text{,}\) we have \(\dblInt_{\cD}y\,\dee{S}=0\) and

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_{\cS}\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} = \dblInt_{\cD}(3+y)\,\dee{S} =3\dblInt_{\cD}\dee{S} =3\,\text{Area}(\cS) =3\,\pi 2^2=12\pi \end{gather*}

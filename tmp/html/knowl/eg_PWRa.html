Example 8.5.4. Finite nonzero radius of convergence.

We already know that, if \(a\ne 0\text{,}\) the geometric series \(\sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty a x^n\) converges when \(|x| \lt 1\) and diverges when \(|x|\ge 1\text{.}\) So, in the terminology of Definition 8.5.3, the geometric series has radius of convergence \(R=1\text{.}\) As a consistency check, we can also compute \(R\) using 8.5.2. The series \(\sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty a x^n\) has \(A_n=a\text{.}\) So

\begin{equation*} R=\bigg[\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\Big|\frac{A_{n+1}}{A_n}\Big|\bigg]^{-1} =\Big[\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}1\Big]^{-1} =1 \end{equation*}

as expected.

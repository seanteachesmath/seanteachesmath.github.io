Solution 15.2.6.40.1.

(a) The divergence of \(\vF\) is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &= \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x}(x+\sin y) +\tfrac{\partial }{\partial y}(z+y) +\tfrac{\partial }{\partial z}(z^2)\\ &=2+2z \end{align*}

(b) Set

\begin{align*} V &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2\le 25,\ 0\le z\le \sqrt{25-x^2-y^2}}\\ S_T &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2+z^2 = 25,\ z\ge 0}\\ S_B &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2 \le 25,\ z= 0} \end{align*}

Note that the boundary, \(\partial V\text{,}\) of \(V\) consists ot two parts â€” \(S_T\) with upward normal, and \(S_B\) with normal \(-\hk\text{.}\) We are to find the flux through \(S_T\) with upward normal. By the divergence theorem, it is

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S_T} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} - \dblInt_{S_B} \vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S}\\ &= \tripInt_V (2+2z)\,\dee{V} \end{align*}

since \(\vF\cdot\hk=z^2=0\) on \(S_B\text{.}\) We'll compute the volume integral by expressing it as an iterated integral, with the \(z\) integration on the outside. In \(V\text{,}\) \(z\) ranges for \(0\) to \(5\text{.}\) The set of points at exactly height \(z\) in \(V\) is \(\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2\le 25-z^2}\text{.}\) So

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S_T} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &= \int_0^5\dee{z} \dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 25-z^2} \dee{x}\,\dee{y}\ (2+2z)\\ &= \int_0^5\dee{z}\ (2+2z)\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 25-z^2} \dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &= \int_0^5\dee{z}\ \pi(25-z^2) (2+2z) \end{align*}

since \(\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 25-z^2} \dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) is the area of a disk of radius \(\sqrt{25-z^2}\text{.}\) Continuing,

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S_T} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &= \pi\int_0^5\dee{z}\ (50 -2z^2 +50z -2z^3)\\ &=\pi\Big(50\times 5-2\frac{5^3}{3} +50\frac{5^2}{2}-2\frac{5^4}{4}\Big)\\ &=\pi 5^3 \Big(2-\frac{2}{3} +5-\frac{5}{2}\Big)\\ &= \pi\,5^3 \Big(\frac{4}{3}+\frac{5}{2}\Big)\\ &=\pi\frac{23}{6} 5^3 =479\frac{1}{6}\pi \end{align*}

(c) To start, consider any closed surface \(S\) that is the boundary of a solid \(V\text{.}\) Use

  • the outward pointing normal for \(S\text{,}\)
  • \(|V|\) to denote the volume of \(V\text{,}\) and
  • \(\bar z=\frac{1}{|V|}\tripInt_V z\ \dee{V}\) to denote the \(z\)-component of the centroid (i.e. centre of mass with constant density) of \(V\text{.}\)

Then, by the divergence theorem

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} =\tripInt_V (2+2z)\,\dee{V}\\ &=2\tripInt_V \,\dee{V} +2\tripInt_V z\,\dee{V}\\ &=2|V| +2|V|\bar z \end{align*}

This takes the value \(-9\) if and only if

\begin{gather*} 2|V|\bar z =-9-2|V| \iff \bar z = -\frac{9}{2|V|}-1 \end{gather*}

One surface which obeys this condition is the unit cube (with outward normal) centred on \(\big(0,0, -\frac{11}{2}\big)\text{.}\)

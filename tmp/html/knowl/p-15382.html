Let \(C\) be the curve of intersection of the surfaces \(y=x^2\) and \(z=\frac{2}{3}x^3\text{.}\) A particle moves along \(C\) with constant speed such that \(\diff{x}{t} \gt 0\text{.}\) The particle is at \((0,0,0)\) at time \(t=0\) and is at \((3,9,18)\) at time \(t=\frac{7}{2}\text{.}\)

  1. Find the length of the part of \(C\) between \((0,0,0)\) and \((3,9,18)\text{.}\)
  2. Find the constant speed of the particle.
  3. Find the velocity of the particle when it is at \(\big(1,1,\frac{2}{3}\big)\text{.}\)
  4. Find the acceleration of the particle when it is at \(\big(1,1,\frac{2}{3}\big)\text{.}\)

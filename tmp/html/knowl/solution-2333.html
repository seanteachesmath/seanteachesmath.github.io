Solution 15.3.1.5.1. Direct evaluation

Here is a sketch of \(C\text{.}\)

The square consists of four line segments.

  • The bottom line segment may be parametrized \(\vr(x)=(x,0),\ 0\le x\le 3\text{.}\) So the line integral along this segment is
    \begin{equation*} \int_0^3 \vF(\vr(x))\cdot\diff{\vr}{x}\ \dee{x} =\int_0^3 (0, 0)\cdot(1, 0)\ \dee{x} =0 \end{equation*}
  • The second line segment may be parametrized \(\vr(y)=(3,y),\ 0\le y\le 3\text{.}\) So the line integral along this segment is
    \begin{equation*} \int_0^3 \vF(\vr(y))\cdot\diff{\vr}{y}\ \dee{y} =\int_0^3 (9y^2, 6y)\cdot(0, 1)\ \dee{y} =\int_0^3 6y\ \dee{y} =27 \end{equation*}
  • The third line segment may be parametrized \(\vr(t)=(3-t,3),\ 0\le t\le 3\text{.}\) So the line integral along this segment is
    \begin{align*} \int_0^3 \vF(\vr(t))\cdot\diff{\vr}{t}\ \dee{t} &=\int_0^3 \big(9(3-t)^2\,,\, 6(3-t)\big)\cdot(-1\,,\,0)\ \dee{t}\\ &=-\int_0^3 9(3-t)^2\ \dee{t} =-81 \end{align*}
  • The final line segment may be parametrized \(\vr(t)=(0,3-t),\ 0\le t\le 3\text{.}\) So the line integral along this segment is
    \begin{equation*} \int_0^3 \vF(\vr(t))\cdot\diff{\vr}{t}\ \dee{t} =\int_0^3 (0, 0)\cdot(0, -1)\ \dee{t} =0 \end{equation*}

The full line integral is

\begin{equation*} \oint_C\vF\cdot d\vr=0+27-81+0=-54 \end{equation*}

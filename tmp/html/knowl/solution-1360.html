Solution 8.5.3.8.1.

We apply the ratio test for the series whose \(n^{\rm th}\) term is \(a_n=\dfrac{(-1)^n(x+2)^n}{\sqrt{n}}\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\bigg|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\bigg| &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \bigg|\frac{(x+2)^{n+1}}{\sqrt{n+1}} \ \frac{\sqrt{n}}{(x+2)^n}\bigg|\\ &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} |x+2|\ \frac{\sqrt{n}}{\sqrt{n+1}}\\ &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} |x+2|\ \frac{1}{\sqrt{1+1/n}}\\ &=|x+2| \end{align*}

So the series must converge when \(|x+2| \lt 1\) and must diverge when \(|x+2| \gt 1\text{.}\) When \(x+2=1\text{,}\) the series reduces to

\begin{gather*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{\sqrt{n}} \end{gather*}

which converges by the alternating series test. When \(x+2=-1\text{,}\) the series reduces to

\begin{gather*} \sum_{n=1}^\infty \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \end{gather*}

which diverges by the \(p\)--series test with \(p=\frac{1}{2}\text{.}\) So the interval of convergence is \(-1 \lt x+2\le 1\) or \((-3,-1]\text{.}\)

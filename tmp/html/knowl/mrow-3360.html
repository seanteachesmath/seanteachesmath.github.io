\begin{align*} |f(x)-T_n(x)| &= \left|\frac{f^{(n+1)}(c)}{(n+1)!}(x-a)^{n+1}\right|\\ \end{align*}

for some \(c\) strictly between \(x\) and \(a\text{.}\) In our case, \(n=5\text{,}\) \(a=0\text{,}\) and \(x=-\dfrac{1}{4}\text{,}\) so

\begin{align*} \left|f\left(-\frac{1}{4}\right)-T_5\left(-\frac{1}{4}\right)\right| &= \left|\frac{f^{(6)}(c)}{6!}\left(-\frac{1}{4}-0\right)^6\right|\\ &= \frac{\left|f^{(6)}(c)\right|}{6!\cdot 4^6}\\ \end{align*}

for some \(c\) in \(\left(-\frac{1}{4},0\right)\text{.}\) We'll need to know the sixth derivative of \(f(x)\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} f(x)&=\log (1-x)\\ f'(x)&=-(1-x)^{-1}\\ f''(x)&=-(1-x)^{-2}\\ f'''(x)&=-2(1-x)^{-3}\\ f^{(4)}(x)&=-3!(1-x)^{-4}\\ f^{(5)}(x)&=-4!(1-x)^{-5}\\ f^{(6)}(x)&=-5!(1-x)^{-6}\\ \end{align*}

Plugging in \(\left|f^{(6)}(c)\right|=\dfrac{5!}{(1-c)^{6}}\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \left|f\left(-\frac{1}{4}\right)-T_5\left(-\frac{1}{4}\right)\right| &= \frac{5!}{6!\cdot 4^6\cdot(1-c)^6}=\frac{1}{6\cdot 4^6 \cdot (1-c)^6} \end{align*}

Solution 12.3.1.8.1.

Note that \((\cos t+\sin t )^2+(\sin t - \cos t)^2=2\) for all \(t\text{.}\) So, the points \((x,y)\) of our curve lie on \(x^2+y^2=2\text{,}\) which is a circle of radius \(\sqrt{2}\text{.}\) Indeed

\begin{align*} x(t)&= \cos t+\sin t =\sqrt{2}\big[\cos t \cos\tfrac{\pi}{4} +\sin t \sin\tfrac{\pi}{4}\big]\\ &=\sqrt{2}\cos\big(t-\tfrac{\pi}{4}\big)\\ y(t)&= \sin t-\cos t =\sqrt{2}\big[\sin t \cos\tfrac{\pi}{4}-\cos t\sin \tfrac{\pi}{4}\big]\\ &=\sqrt{2}\sin\big(t-\tfrac{\pi}{4}\big) \end{align*}

So, \(\vr(t)\) circumnavigates a circle of radius \(\sqrt{2}\) and consequently has curvature \(\ka=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\text{.}\)

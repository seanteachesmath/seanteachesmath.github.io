Since \(x=g(y,z)\) with \(g(x,y)=y^2+z^2\text{,}\) (14.3.2) gives

\begin{equation*} \hn\,\dee{S}=\pm (1,-g_y,-g_z)\,\dee{y}\dee{z} =\pm (1,-2y,-2z)\,\dee{y}\dee{z} \end{equation*}

We choose the \(+\) sign so that \(\hn \cdot \hi \gt 0\text{.}\) Furthermore

\begin{align*} S&=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x=y^2+z^2,\ x\le 2y}\\ &=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x=y^2+z^2,\ y^2+z^2\le 2y}\\ &=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x=y^2+z^2,\ (y-1)^2+z^2\le 1}\\ &=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x=y^2+z^2,\ (y,z)\text{ in }D} \end{align*}

where \(D=\Set{(x,y)}{(y-1)^2+z^2\le 1}\) is a disk with radius \(1\text{.}\) Hence

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot \hn\ \dee{S} = \dblInt_D (2, z, y) \cdot (1,-2 y, -2z)\ \dee{y} \dee{z} = \dblInt_D (2 - 4yz)\ \dee{y} \dee{z} \end{equation*}

Since \(-4yz\) is odd under \(z\rightarrow -z\) the integral of \(-4yz\) is zero and

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S \vF \cdot \hn\, \dee{S}= 2\,\text{Area}(D)=2\pi \end{equation*}

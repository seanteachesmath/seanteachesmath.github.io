Exercise Group 4.7.4.1–3.

In Questions 4.7.4.1 to 4.7.4.3, you are asked to give pairs of functions that combine to make indeterminate forms. Remember that an indeterminate form is indeterminate precisely because its limit can take on a number of values.

1.

Give two functions \(f(x)\) and \(g(x)\) with the following properties:

  1. \(\displaystyle \ds\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)=\infty\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \ds\lim_{x \to \infty} g(x)=\infty\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \ds\lim_{x \to \infty} \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}=2.5\)

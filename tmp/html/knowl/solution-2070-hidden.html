We parametrize the unit circle as \((\cos t, \sin t)\text{,}\) \(0 \le t \le 2\pi\text{.}\)

A tiny slice of the hoop with length \(\dee{s}\) has mass \((x^2 \mathrm{kg}/\mathrm{m})(\dee{s} \mathrm{m})=x^2\dee{s} \mathrm{kg}\text{.}\) So, the entire hoop has mass:

\begin{align*} \int_C x^2\,\dee{s}&=\int_0^{2\pi} \cos^2 t \sqrt{(-\sin t)^2+(\cos t)^2}\,\dee{t}=\int_0^{2\pi} \cos^2 t \,\dee{t}\\ &=\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{1+\cos(2t)}{2}\ \dee{t} =\left[\frac{t}{2} +\frac{\sin(2t)}{4} \right]_0^{2\pi}=\pi \mathrm{kg} \end{align*}

For an efficient, sneaky, way to evaluate \(\int_0^{2\pi} \cos^2 t\ \dee{t}\text{,}\) see Example 13.4.4.

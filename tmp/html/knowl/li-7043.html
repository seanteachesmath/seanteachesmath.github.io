On the other hand, in the limit \(\theta\rightarrow\frac{\pi}{2}\text{,}\) which corresponds to \(f_x\) and/or \(f_y\) becoming infinite and our surface becoming perpendicular to the \(xy\)-plane, \(\dee{S}\) becomes “infinity times” \(\dee{x}\dee{y}\text{.}\) In this case, we should represent our surface either in the form \(x=g(y,z)\) or in the form \(y=h(x,z)\text{,}\) rather than in the form \(z=f(x,y)\text{.}\)

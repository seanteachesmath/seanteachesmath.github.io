Solution 15.3.1.21.1.

(a) Parametrize the circle \(\vr(\theta)=(\cos\theta,\sin\theta)\text{.}\) Then

\begin{align*} \vF\big(\vr(\theta)\big) &=\sin^3\theta\,\hi -\cos\theta \sin^2\theta\,\hj\\ \diff{\vr}{\theta}(\theta)&=-\sin\theta\,\hi+\cos\theta\,\hj\\ \vF\big(\vr(\theta)\big)\cdot \diff{\vr}{\theta}(\theta) &=-\sin^4\theta-\cos^2\theta\sin^2\theta=-\sin^2\theta\\ \oint_C\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\int_0^{2\pi}\vF\big(\vr(\theta)\big)\cdot \diff{\vr}{\theta}(\theta)\ \dee{\theta} =-\int_0^{2\pi}\sin^2\theta\ \dee{\theta}\\ &=-\int_0^{2\pi}\frac{1-\cos(2\theta)}{2}\ \dee{\theta}\\ &=-{\Big[\frac{\theta}{2}-\frac{\sin(2\theta)}{4}\Big]}_0^{2\pi} =-\pi \end{align*}

For an efficient, sneaky, way to evaluate \(\int_0^{2\pi} \sin^2\theta\ \dee{\theta}\) see Example 13.4.4.

(b) Denote by \(W\) the washer shaped region between the circle \(x^2+y^2=1\) and the ellipse \(\frac{x^2}{16}+\frac{y^2}{25}=1\text{.}\) It is sketched below. By Green's theorem

\begin{gather*} \oint_{C_0}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}-\oint_C\vF\cdot \dee{\vr} =\dblInt_W \Big[\pdiff{}{x}\vF_2 -\pdiff{}{y}\vF_1\Big]\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{gather*}

For the specified \(\vF\)

\begin{align*} &\pdiff{}{x}\vF_2 -\pdiff{}{y}\vF_1 =-\pdiff{}{x}\frac{xy^2}{ {(x^2+y^2)}^2} -\pdiff{}{y}\frac{y^3}{ {(x^2+y^2)}^2}\\ &\hskip0.5in=-\frac{y^2}{ {(x^2+y^2)}^2}+2\frac{xy^2(2x)}{ {(x^2+y^2)}^3} -\frac{3y^2}{ {(x^2+y^2)}^2}+2\frac{y^3(2y)}{ {(x^2+y^2)}^3}\\ &\hskip0.5in=\frac{-y^2(x^2+y^2)+4x^2y^2-3y^2(x^2+y^2)+4y^4}{ {(x^2+y^2)}^3}\\ &\hskip0.5in=0 \end{align*}

Consequently

\begin{equation*} \oint_{C_0}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}-\oint_C\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}=0 \implies \oint_{C_0}\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}=\oint_C\vF\cdot \dee{\vr}=-\pi \end{equation*}

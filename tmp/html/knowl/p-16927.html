First note that,

  • for A, B and C, \(\vr(\theta, \phi) =x(\theta, \phi)\,\hi+ y(\theta, \phi)\,\hj+z(\theta, \phi)\,\hk\) obeys
    \begin{equation*} x(\theta, \phi)^2+ y(\theta, \phi)^2+z(\theta, \phi)^2 = 4 \end{equation*}
    and so lies on \(S_1\)
  • for D, E and F, \(\vr(\theta, z) =x(\theta, z)\,\hi+ y(\theta, z)\,\hj+z(\theta, z)\,\hk\) obeys
    \begin{equation*} x(\theta, z)^2+ y(\theta, z)^2=4-z(\theta, z)^2 \end{equation*}
    and so lies on \(S_1\)
  • for G, H and I, \(\vr(\theta, z) =x(\theta, z)\,\hi+ y(\theta, z)\,\hj+z(\theta, z)\,\hk\) obeys
    \begin{equation*} x(\theta, z)^2+ y(\theta, z)^2=z(\theta, z)^2 \end{equation*}
    and so lies on \(S_3\)
  • for J, K and L, \(\vr(x, y) =x(x, y)\,\hi+ y(x, y)\,\hj+z(x, y)\,\hk\) obeys
    \begin{equation*} x(x, y)^2+ y(x, y)^2=z(x, y)^2 \end{equation*}
    and so lies on \(S_3\)

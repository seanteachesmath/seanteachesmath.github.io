(a) Think of

\begin{equation*} \vr(t) = (t,1) - (\sin t,\cos t) \end{equation*}

The \((t,1)\) part gives the position of the centre of the wheel at time \(t\text{.}\) The other part gives the position of the thumbtack with respect to the centre of the wheel. In particular,

  • at time \(t=0\text{,}\) \(\vr(0) = (0,0)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is on the ground (i.e. at \(y=0\)).
  • At time \(t=\pi\text{,}\) \(\vr(\pi) = (\pi,2)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is at its highest point (i.e. at \(y=2\)) and is above the centre of the wheel at \(x=\pi\text{.}\)
  • At time \(t=2\pi\text{,}\) \(\vr(2\pi) = (2\pi,0)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is back on the ground (i.e. at \(y=0\)) and is below the centre of the wheel at \(x=2\pi\text{.}\)
  • At time \(t=3\pi\text{,}\) \(\vr(3\pi) = (3\pi,2)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is again at its highest point (i.e. at \(y=2\)) and is above the centre of the wheel at \(x=3\pi\text{.}\)
  • At time \(t=4\pi\text{,}\) \(\vr(4\pi) = (4\pi,0)\text{.}\) The thumbtack is back on the ground (i.e. at \(y=0\)) and is below the centre of the wheel at \(x=4\pi\text{.}\)

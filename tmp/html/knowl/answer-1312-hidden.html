Let \(\displaystyle f(x) = \frac{5}{x(\log x)^{3/2}}\text{.}\) Then \(f(x)\) is positive and decreases as \(x\) increases. So the sum \(\displaystyle\sum_3^{\infty} f(n)\) and the integral \(\displaystyle\int_3^\infty f(x) \,\dee{x}\) either both converge or both diverge, by the integral test, which is Theorem 8.3.5. For the integral, we use the substitution \(u = \log x\text{,}\) \(\dee{u} = \frac{\dee{x}}{x}\) to get

\begin{gather*} \int_3^\infty \frac{5 \,\dee{x}}{x(\log x)^{3/2}} = \int_{\log 3}^\infty \frac{5 \,\dee{u}}{u^{3/2}} \end{gather*}

which converges by the \(p\)-test (which is Example 6.12.8) with \(p=\frac{3}{2} \gt 1\text{.}\)

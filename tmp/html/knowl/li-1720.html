Another way  7  to evaluate the same limit is to observe that it can be massaged into the form of the limit definition of the derivative. First notice that
\begin{align*} \lim_{X\rightarrow 0} \frac{X}{e^X-1} &= \left[\lim_{X\rightarrow 0} \frac{e^X-1}{X} \right]^{-1} \end{align*}
provided this second limit exists and is nonzero  8 . This second limit should look a little familiar:
\begin{align*} \lim_{X\rightarrow 0} \frac{e^X-1}{X} &= \lim_{X\rightarrow 0} \frac{e^X-e^0}{X-0} \end{align*}
which is just the definition of the derivative of \(e^x\) at \(x=0\text{.}\) Hence we have
\begin{align*} \lim_{X\rightarrow 0} \frac{X}{e^X-1} &=\left[\lim_{X\rightarrow 0}\, \frac{e^X-e^0}{X-0} \right]^{-1}\\ &=\left[\diff{}{X}e^X\Big|_{X=0} \right]^{-1}\\ &=\left[e^X\big|_{X=0}\right]^{-1}\\ &=1 \end{align*}

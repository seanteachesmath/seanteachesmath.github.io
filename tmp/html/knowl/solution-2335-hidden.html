Call the trapezoid \(T\text{.}\)

By Green's theorem,

\begin{align*} &\oint_C (x\sin y^2 -y^2)\,\dee{x}+(x^2y\cos y^2+3x)\,\dee{y}\\ &\hskip1in=\dblInt_T\left\{\pdiff{}{x}(x^2y\cos y^2+3x) -\pdiff{}{y} (x\sin y^2 -y^2)\right\}\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &\hskip1in=\dblInt_T\big(2xy\cos y^2+3-2xy\cos y^2+2y\big)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\\ &\hskip1in=\dblInt_T\big(3+2y\big)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y} \end{align*}

The integral \(\dblInt_T(2y)\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) vanishes because \(2y\) changes sign under \(y\rightarrow-y\) while the domain of integration is invariant under \(y\rightarrow -y\text{.}\) The integral \(\dblInt_T 3\,\dee{x}\,\dee{y}\) is \(3\) times the area of the trapezoid, which is its width (1) times the average of its heights \((\half[2+4])=3\text{.}\) So

\begin{equation*} \oint_C (x\sin y^2 -y^2)\,\dee{x}+(x^2y\cos y^2+3x)\,\dee{y} =3\times 1\times 3 = 9 \end{equation*}

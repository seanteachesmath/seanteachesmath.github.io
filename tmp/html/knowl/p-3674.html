We approximate the function \(f(x)=\sqrt{x}\) about \(x=4\text{,}\) since 4 is a perfect square and it is close to 5.

\begin{align*} f(4)&=\sqrt{4}=2\\ f'(x)&=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{x}} \qquad \Rightarrow \qquad f'(4)=\frac{1}{2\sqrt{4}}=\frac{1}{4}\\ f(x) &\approx f(4)+f'(4)(x-4)=2+\frac{1}{4}(x-4)\\ f(5)&\approx 2+\frac{1}{4}(5-4)=\frac{9}{4} \end{align*}

We estimate \(\sqrt{5}\approx\dfrac{9}{4}\text{.}\)

5. 1997A.

Let

\begin{equation*} f(x)=\frac{x^5}{20}+\frac{5x^3}{6}-10x^2+500x+1000 \end{equation*}

Show that \(f(x)\) has exactly one inflection point.

6. 1996D.

Let \(f(x)\) be a function whose first two derivatives exist everywhere, and \(f''(x) \gt 0\) for all \(x\text{.}\)

  1. Show that \(f(x)\) has at most one critical point and that any critical point is an absolute minimum for \(f(x)\text{.}\)
  2. Show that the maximum value of \(f(x)\) on any finite interval occurs at one of the endpoints of the interval.

Solution 15.4.3.26.1.

Note that

\begin{equation*} \vnabla\times\vF=\det\left|\begin{matrix}\hi&\hj&\hk\\ \frac{\partial }{\partial x}& \frac{\partial }{\partial y}& \frac{\partial }{\partial z}\\ z^2&x^2&y^2\end{matrix}\right| =2y\,\hi+2z\,\hj+2x\,\hk \end{equation*}

Let \(D\) be the disk in the plane \(x+y+z=3\) whose boundary is \(C\) and let \(\hn=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}(\hi+\hj+\hk)\) be the upward unit normal to \(D\text{.}\) If the circle is oriented counterclockwise, when viewed from above, then, by Stokes' theorem (Theorem 15.4.1),

\begin{align*} \oint_C \vF\cdot \dee{\vr} &=\dblInt_D \vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\ \dee{S}\\ &=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \dblInt_D \big(2y\,\hi+2z\,\hj+2x\,\hk\big)\cdot(\hi+\hj+\hk)\ \dee{S}\\ &=\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\dblInt_D2\overbrace{\big(x+y+z\big)}^{=3\text{ on } D}\ \dee{S}\\ &=2\sqrt{3}\dblInt_D \dee{S} =2\sqrt{3}\pi R^2 \end{align*}

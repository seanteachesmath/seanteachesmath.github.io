we are told that \(\vr(t)\perp\vr'(t)\text{,}\) so that \(\vr(t)\cdot\vr'(t)=0\text{,}\) for all \(t\text{.}\) Consequently

\begin{equation*} \diff{}{t}|\vr(t)|^2 =\diff{}{t}\big[\vr(t)\cdot\vr(t)\big] =2\vr(t)\cdot\vr'(t)=0 \end{equation*}

So \(|\vr(t)|^2\) is a constant, say \(A\text{,}\) independent of time and \(\vr(t)\) always lies on the sphere of radius \(\sqrt{A}\) centred on the origin.

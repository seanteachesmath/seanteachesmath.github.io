Solution 4.4.11.9.16.1.
  • By EquationÂ 4.4.33, there is \(c\in(0,1)\) such that the error is
    \begin{align*} \left|\frac{f'''(c)}{3!}\cdot (1-0)^3\right| &= \left|\frac{e^{-c}}{6(8+c^2)}\right|. \end{align*}
  • When \(0 \lt c \lt 1\text{,}\) we know that \(1 \gt e^{-c} \gt e^{-1}\) and \(8 \leq 8+c^2 \lt 9\text{,}\) so
    \begin{align*} \left|\frac{e^{-c}}{6(8+c^2)}\right| &=\frac{e^{-c}}{6(8+c^2)}\\ & \lt \frac{1}{6 |8+c^2|}\\ & \lt \frac{1}{6\times 8} = \frac{1}{48} \lt \frac{1}{40} \end{align*}
    as required.
  • Alternatively, notice that \(e^{-c}\) is a decreasing function of \(c\text{,}\) while for \(0 \lt c\) \(8+c^2\) is an increasing function of \(c\text{.}\) Hence the fraction is a decreasing function of \(c\) and takes its largest value at \(c=0\text{.}\) Hence
    \begin{align*} \left|\frac{e^c}{6(8+c^2)}\right| & \leq \frac{1}{6\times 8} = \frac{1}{48} \lt \frac{1}{40}. \end{align*}

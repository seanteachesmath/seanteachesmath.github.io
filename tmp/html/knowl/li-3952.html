Solution 1: Set \(a_n= \frac{n+\sin n}{1+n^2}\text{.}\) We first try to develop some intuition about the behaviour of \(a_n\) for large \(n\) and then we confirm that our intuition was correct.

  • Step 1: Develop intuition. When \(n\gg 1\text{,}\) the numerator \(n+\sin n\approx n\text{,}\) and the denominator \(1+n^2\approx n^2\) so that \(a_n\approx \frac{n}{n^2}=\frac{1}{n}\) and it looks like our series should diverge by the \(p\)-test (Example 8.3.6) with \(p=1\text{.}\)
  • Step 2: Verify intuition. To confirm our intuition we set \(b_n=\frac{1}{n}\) and compute the limit
    \begin{align*} \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{a_n}{b_n} \amp=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{ \frac{n+\sin n}{1+n^2} } {\frac{1}{n}} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{n[n+\sin n]} {1+n^2}\\ \amp=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{1+\frac{\sin n}{n}} {\frac{1}{n^2}+1} =1 \end{align*}
    We already know that the series \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty b_n =\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty\frac{1}{n}\) diverges by the \(p\)-test with \(p=1\text{.}\) So our series diverges by the limit comparison test, Theorem 8.3.11.

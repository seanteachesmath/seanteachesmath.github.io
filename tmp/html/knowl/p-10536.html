To verify that our intuition is correct, we apply the limit comparison test with

\begin{gather*} a_n= \frac{n^2-\sin n}{n^6+n^2} \quad\text{and}\quad b_n= \frac{1}{n^4} \end{gather*}

which is valid since

\begin{align*} \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n} \amp=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\left|\frac{(n^2-\sin n)}{n^6+n^2}\right|\cdot\frac{n^4}{1} =\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{|n^6-n^4\sin n|}{n^6+n^2}\\ \amp=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\frac{1-n^{-2}\sin n}{1+n^{-4}} =1 \end{align*}

exists and is nonzero. Since the series \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty b_n\) converges, the series \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty\dfrac{|n^2-\sin n|}{n^6+n^2}\) converges too. Therefore, the series \(\sum\limits_{n=1}^\infty\dfrac{n^2-\sin n}{n^6+n^2}\) converges absolutely.

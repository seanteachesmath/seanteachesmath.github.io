Solution 8.3.11.43.1.

The sequence \(\{\frac{1}{n^2}\}\) decreases to zero as \(n\) increases to infinity. So, by the alternating series error bound, which is given in Theorem 8.3.14, \(\frac{\pi^2}{12}-S_N\) lies between zero and the first omitted term, \(\frac{(-1)^{N}}{(N+1)^2}\text{.}\) We therefore need \(\frac{1}{(N+1)^2} \leq 10^{-6}\text{,}\) which is equivalent to \(N+1 \geq 10^3\) and \(N\geq 999\text{.}\)

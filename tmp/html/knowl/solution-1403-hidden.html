Our series looks something like the Taylor series for sine, \(\sin x = \displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^{n}}{(2n+1)!}x^{2n+1}\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \amp\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1)!}\left(\frac{\pi}{4} \right)^{2n+1}\left(1+2^{2n+1} \right)\\ \amp\hskip0.5in= \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1)!}\left[\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right)^{2n+1}+\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)^{2n+1} \right]\\ &\hskip0.5in=\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1)!}\left(\frac{\pi}{4}\right)^{2n+1} +\sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{(2n+1)!}\left(\frac{\pi}{2}\right)^{2n+1}\\ &\hskip0.5in=\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{4} \right)+\sin\left(\frac{\pi}{2} \right)\\ &\hskip0.5in=\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}+1=\frac{1+\sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{2}} \end{align*}

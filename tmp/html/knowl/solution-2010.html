Solution 12.1.1.29.1.

Using the Theorem of Pappus, we can calculate the surface area and volume of a pipe with the same length and radius as this pipe. So, we need to find the length of the pipe, \(L\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \diff{\vr}{t}&=(\sqrt{2t},t,1)\\ \left| \diff{\vr}{t}\right|&=\sqrt{2t+t^2+1}=|t+1|\\ L&=\int_0^{10}(t+1)\dee{t}=60 \end{align*}

A pipe with radius 3 and length 60 has surface area \(60(2\pi\cdot 3)=360\pi\) and volume \(60(\pi \cdot3^2)=540\pi\text{.}\)

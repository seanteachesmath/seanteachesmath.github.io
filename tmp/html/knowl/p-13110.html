Let \(z = f (x, y) = \ln(4x^2 + y^2)\text{.}\)

  1. Use a linear approximation of the function \(z = f (x, y)\) at \((0, 1)\) to estimate \(f(0.1, 1.2)\text{.}\)
  2. Find a point \(P (a, b, c)\) on the graph of \(z = f(x, y)\) such that the tangent plane to the graph of \(z = f (x, y)\) at the point \(P\) is parallel to the plane \(2x + 2y - z = 3\text{.}\)

By Theorem 13.4.7, the condition that “\(\int_C\vF\cdot\dee{\vr}=0\) for all closed paths \(C\)” is equivalent to the condition that “\(\vF\) is conservative”, which, since \(\vF\) is defined on all of \(\bbbr^3\text{,}\) is equivalent to the condition that \(\vF\) pass the screening test

\begin{align*} \vZero =\vnabla\times \vF &=\det\left[\begin{matrix}\hi&\hj&\hk \\ \frac{\partial }{\partial x}& \frac{\partial }{\partial y}& \frac{\partial }{\partial z}\\ e^x \sin y & ae^x \cos y + bz & cx\end{matrix}\right]\\ &= -b\,\hi-c\,\hj+\big(ae^x\cos y-e^x\cos y\big)\,\hk \end{align*}

which is the case if and only if \(b=c=0\) and \(a=1\text{.}\)

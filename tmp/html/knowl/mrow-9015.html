\begin{align*} \diff{P}{t}&=-k\sqrt{P}\\ \frac{\dee{P}}{\sqrt{P}}&=-k\,\dee{t}\\ \int\frac{\dee{P}}{\sqrt{P}}&=\int-k\,\dee{t}\\ 2\sqrt{P}&=-kt+C\\ \end{align*}

At \(t=0\text{,}\) \(P=90,000\) so

\begin{align*} 2\sqrt{90,000}&=-k\times 0+C\\ C&=2\times 300=600\\ \end{align*}

Therefore,

\begin{align*} 2\sqrt{P}&=-kt+600\tag{$*$}\\ \end{align*}

Now, we find \(k\text{.}\) Let \(t\) be measured in weeks. Then when \(t=6\text{,}\) \(P=40,000\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 2\sqrt{40,000}&=-6k+600\\ 2\cdot 200&=-6k+600\\ k&=\frac{200}{6} = \frac{100}{3}\\ \end{align*}

Substituting our value of \(k\) into (\(*\)):

\begin{align*} 2\sqrt{P}&=-\frac{100}{3}t+600\\ \end{align*}

To find when the population will be 10,000, we set \(P=10,000\) and solve for \(t\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 2\sqrt{10,000}&=-\frac{100}{3}t+600\\ 2\cdot 100&=-\frac{100}{3}t+600\\ \frac{100}{3}t&=400\\ t&=12 \end{align*}

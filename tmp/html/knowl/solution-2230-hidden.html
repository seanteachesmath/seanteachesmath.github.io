The surface is a sphere of radius \(2\) centered on \((0,0,2)\text{,}\) The plane \(z=1\) intersects the sphere on the circle \(x^2+y^2=3\text{.}\) Let \(F(x,y,z)=x^2+y^2+(z-2)^2\text{.}\) Then, by (14.3.3),

\begin{align*} \dee{S}&=\Big|\frac{\vnabla F}{\vnabla F\cdot\hk}\Big|\,\dee{x}\dee{y} =\Big|\frac{2x\hi+2y\hj+2(z-2)\hk}{2(z-2)}\Big|\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &=\Big|\frac{x\hi+y\hj+(z-2)\hk}{(z-2)}\Big|\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &=\frac{\sqrt{x^2+y^2+(z-2)^2}}{|z-2|}\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &=\frac{2}{|z-2|}\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

since \(x^2+y^2+(z-2)^2=4\) on \(\cS\text{.}\) On \(\cS\text{,}\) \(z\le 2\text{,}\) so \(|z-2|=2-z\) and

\begin{align*} \dblInt_\cS f(x,y,z)\,\dee{S} &=\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 3} (2-z)(x^2+y^2)\,\frac{2}{|z-2|}\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &=2\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 3} (x^2+y^2)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

Switching to polar coordinates

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_\cS f(x,y,z)\,\dee{S} =2\int_0^{\sqrt{3}}\dee{r}\ r\int_0^{2\pi}\dee{\theta}\ r^2 =2(2\pi)\frac{r^4}{4}\Big|_0^{\sqrt{3}} =9\pi \end{equation*}

Solution 15.4.3.15.1.

Denote by

\begin{equation*} S=\Set{(x,y,z)}{z=x+4,\ x^2+y^2\le 4} \end{equation*}

the part of the plane \(z=x+4\) that is contained in the cylinder \(x^2+y^2=4\text{.}\) Orient \(S\) by the downward pointing normal \(\hn =\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(1,0,-1)\text{.}\) Then \(C\) is the boundary of \(S\text{.}\) The part of \(C\) and \(S\) that are in the first octant are sketched below.

We may parametrize \(S\) by

\begin{equation*} \vr(x,y) = (x,y, x+4)\qquad\text{with}\quad x^2+y^2\le 4 \end{equation*}

So,

\begin{equation*} \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial x}\times\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial y} = \det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi & \hj & \hk \\ 1 & 0 & 1 \\ 0 & 1 & 0 \end{matrix}\right] = \big(-1, 0, 1\big) \end{equation*}

and, by (14.3.1),

\begin{gather*} \hn\,\dee{S} = -\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial x}\times \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial y}\,\dee{x}\dee{y} = (1,0,-1)\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{gather*}

We have chosen to “\(-\)” sign in \(\hn\,\dee{S} = \pm\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial x}\times \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial y}\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\) to give the downward pointing normal. As the curl of \(\vF\) is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi &\hj &\hk\\ \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial y} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial z}\\ x^3 + 2y & \sin(y) + z & x + \sin(z^2) \end{matrix} \right]\\ &= -\hi -\hj -2\hk \end{align*}

Stokes' theorem (Theorem 15.4.1) gives

\begin{align*} \oint_C\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} &=\dblInt_S\vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_S (-1,-1,-2)\cdot(1,0,-1)\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &=\dblInt_S \dee{x}\dee{y} =4\pi \end{align*}

Finally taking the limit as \(n \to \infty\) (i.e. taking the limit as the slice width goes to zero), we get
\begin{align*} \text{Mass} &=\lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n \De x \int_{B(x_i^*)}^{T(x_i^*)} f\big(x_i^*,y\big)\,\dee{y} = \lim_{n\rightarrow\infty}\sum_{i=1}^n F(x_i^*)\,\De x \end{align*}
Now we are back in familiar 1-variable territory. The sum \(\sum\limits_{i=1}^n F(x_i^*)\,\De x\) is a Riemann sum approximation to the integral \(\int_a^b F(x)\,\dee{x}\text{.}\) So
\begin{align*} \text{Mass} &= \int_a^b F(x)\,\dee{x} =\int_a^b \left[\int_{B(x)}^{T(x)} f\big(x,y\big)\,\dee{y}\right]\dee{x} \end{align*}

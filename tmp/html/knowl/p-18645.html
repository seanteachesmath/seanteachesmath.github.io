(1): Let \(\cR_1\) be the region between \(S\) and \(T\text{.}\) It is the shaded region in the figure on the left below. Note that \(\cR_1\) is contained in the domain of \(\vF\text{,}\) so that \(\vnabla\times\vF=\vZero\) on all of \(\cR_1\text{.}\) The boundary of \(\cR_1\) is \(S-T\text{,}\) meaning that the boundary consists of two parts, with one part being \(S\) and the other part being \(-T\text{.}\) So, by Stokes' theorem

\begin{equation*} \int_S \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} - \int_T \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} =\int_{\partial \cR_1} \vF\cdot\dee{\vr} =\dblInt_{\cR_1} \vnabla\times\vF\cdot\hk\, \dee{S} =0 \end{equation*}

and (1) is true.

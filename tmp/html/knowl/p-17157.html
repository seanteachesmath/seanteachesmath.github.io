From it we see that \(0\le\theta\le\pi\text{.}\) The cone \(z^2=x^2+y^2=r^2\) (i.e. \(z=\pm r\)) and the cylinder \(r=2a\sin\theta\) intersect at \(z^2=r^2=4a^2\sin^2\theta\text{.}\) So, for each fixed \(\theta\text{,}\) \(z\) runs from \(-2a\sin\theta\) to \(z=+2a\sin\theta\text{.}\) (See the figure on the right above. It shows a constant \(\theta\) cross-section.) Finally,

\begin{align*} {\rm Area} &= \int_{|z|\le 2a\sin\theta}2a\,\dee{\theta}\,\dee{z} =2a\int_0^\pi \!\!\dee{\theta}\int_{-2a\sin\theta}^{2a\sin\theta} \!\!\!\dee{z} =8a^2\int_0^\pi \!\!\dee{\theta}\ \sin\theta\\ &=8a^2\Big[-\cos\theta\Big]_0^\pi\\ &=16a^2 \end{align*}

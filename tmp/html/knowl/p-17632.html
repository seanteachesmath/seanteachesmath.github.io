(b) Let \(V\) be the interior of \(S\text{.}\) Then, by the divergence theorem,

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\tripInt_V\vnabla\cdot\vF\ \dee{V} =\tripInt_V(1+1+2z)\ \dee{V} \end{equation*}

By oddness under \(z\rightarrow -z\text{,}\) the \(z\) integral vanishes, so that

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =2\tripInt_V\ \dee{V} =2\,\text{Volume}(V) =2\frac{4\pi}{3} =\frac{8\pi}{3} \end{equation*}

We apply the ratio test with \(a_n= (-1)^n\frac{(x-1)^n}{2^n(n+2)}\text{.}\) Since

\begin{align*} \lim_{n\to\infty} \bigg| \frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n} \bigg| &= \lim_{n\to\infty} \bigg| \frac{(x-1)^{n+1}}{2^{n+1}(n+3)}\ \frac{2^n(n+2)}{(x-1)^n} \bigg|\\ &= \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{|x-1|}{2} \frac{n+2}{n+3}\\ &= \frac{|x-1|}{2} \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{1+2/n}{1+3/n} = \frac{|x-1|}{2} \end{align*}

the series converges if

\begin{align*} \frac{|x-1|}{2} \lt 1 &\iff |x-1| \lt 2 \iff -2 \lt (x-1) \lt 2\\ \amp\iff -1 \lt x \lt 3 \end{align*}

and diverges if \(|x-1| \gt 2\text{.}\) So the series has radius of convergence \(2\text{.}\) Checking the left endpoint \(x=-1\text{,}\) so that \(\frac{x-1}{2}=-1\text{,}\) we see that

\begin{gather*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\frac{(-1-1)^n}{2^n(n+2)} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{1}{n+2} \end{gather*}

is the divergent harmonic series. At the right endpoint \(x=3\text{,}\) so that \(\frac{x-1}{2}=+1\) and

\begin{gather*} \sum_{n=0}^\infty (-1)^n\frac{(3-1)^n}{2^n(n+2)} = \sum_{n=0}^\infty \frac{(-1)^n}{n+2} \end{gather*}

converges by the alternating series test. Therefore the interval of convergence of the original series is \(-1 \lt x\le 3\text{,}\) or \(\big(-1,3\big]\text{.}\)

(5): False. For any conservative vector field \(\vF\text{,}\) with potential \(f\text{,}\) \(\int_V \vF\cdot\dee{\vr}\) is just the difference of the values of \(f\) at the two end points of \(V\text{.}\) It is easy to choose an \(f\) for which those two values are different. For example \(f(x,y)=x\) does the job.

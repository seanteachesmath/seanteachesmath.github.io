The full sphere can be parametrized, using spherical coordinates with \(\rho=4\text{,}\) by

\begin{align*} &\vr(\theta,\varphi) = 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi\,\hi +4\sin\theta\sin\varphi\,\hj +4\cos\varphi\,\hk\\ &0\le\theta\le 2\pi,\ 0\le\varphi\le \pi \end{align*}

In these coordinates, the condition \(4/\sqrt{2} \le z \le 4\) is

\begin{align*} \frac{4}{\sqrt{2}} \le 4\cos\varphi \le 4 &\iff \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \le \cos\varphi \le 1\\ &\iff 0\le\varphi\le\frac{\pi}{4} \end{align*}

So our parametrization is

\begin{align*} &\vr(\theta,\varphi) = 4\cos\theta\sin\varphi\,\hi +4\sin\theta\sin\varphi\,\hj +4\cos\varphi\,\hk\\ &0\le\theta\le 2\pi,\ 0\le\varphi\le \frac{\pi}{4} \end{align*}

Renaming \(\theta\) to \(u\) and \(\varphi\) to \(v\text{,}\) the parametrization is also

\begin{equation*} \vr(u,v) = \big(4\cos u\sin v\,,\, 4\sin u\sin v\,,\, 4\cos v\big)\qquad 0\le u\le 2\pi,\ 0\le v\le \frac{\pi}{4} \end{equation*}

(a) This right circular cone symmetric about the \(z\)-axis projects down onto a disk \(\cD\) in the plane \(z=0\text{.}\) Setting \(z=b\) gives

\begin{equation*} \cD=\Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2 + y^2 \le a^2,\ z=0} \end{equation*}

Since \(G(x,y,z) = b^2(x^2+y^2)-a^2z^2\) is constant on \(\cS\text{,}\) the area elements \(\dee{S}\) on \(\cS\) are related to area elements \(\dee{x}\dee{y}\) on \(\cD\) as follows:

\begin{align*} \dee{S} &= \frac{|\nabla G(x,y,z)|}{ |\nabla G(x,y,z)\cdot\hk|}\,\dee{x}\dee{y} = \frac{2|(b^2 x,b^2 y,-a^2 z)|}{2|a^2z|}\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &= \frac{\sqrt{b^4(x^2+y^2)+a^4 z^2}}{ a^2 z}\,\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

by (14.3.3). The defining equation for \(\cS\) gives \(z=\frac{b}{ a}\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\text{,}\) so

\begin{equation*} \dee{S} = \frac{\sqrt{b^4(x^2+y^2)+a^2 b^2(x^2+y^2)}}{ a\sqrt{b^2(x^2+y^2)}}\,\dee{x}\dee{y} = \frac{1}{ a}\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\,\dee{x}\dee{y}. \end{equation*}

Hence \(I = \frac{\sqrt{a^2+b^2}}{ a}\dblInt_\cD(x^2+y^2)\,\dee{x}\dee{y}\text{.}\)

(b) Or, parametrize the surface \(\cS\) using \(\theta\) and \(t\) as follows:

\begin{align*} &x = t\cos\theta,\ y=t\sin\theta,\ z = \frac{b}{ a}\sqrt{x^2+y^2} = \frac{b}{ a}t, \tag{$*$}\\ &0\le\theta\le 2\pi,\ 0\le t\le a. \end{align*}

Then we have, by (14.3.1),

\begin{align*} \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial t}\times\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\theta} &= \det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi & \hj & \hk \\ \cos\theta & \sin\theta & b/a \\ -t\sin\theta & t\cos\theta & 0 \end{matrix}\right]\\ &= \big(-\frac{b}{ a}t\cos\theta, -\frac{b}{ a}t\sin\theta, t\big), \end{align*}

so

\begin{equation*} \dee{S} = \left|\frac{\partial\vr}{\partial t}\times \frac{\partial\vr}{\partial\theta}\right|\,\dee{t}\,\dee{\theta} = t\sqrt{1+b^2/a^2}\,\dee{t}\,\dee{\theta}. \end{equation*}

It follows that for the rectangular region \(\cR\) of the \(t\theta\)-plane described in \((*)\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} I = \dblInt_\cR \big(t^2\big)t\sqrt{1+b^2/a^2}\,\dee{t}\,\dee{\theta}. \end{equation*}

(c) Using polar coordinates in (a) would give

\begin{equation*} I = \frac{\sqrt{a^2+b^2}}{ a} \int_{\theta=0}^{2\pi} \int_{r=0}^a r^2\,r\,\dee{r}\,\dee{\theta} =\frac{\pi}{ 2}a^3\sqrt{a^2+b^2}. \end{equation*}

Direct integration in (b) gives the same thing, because

\begin{equation*} I = \dblInt_\cD \big(t^2\big)t\sqrt{1+b^2/a^2}\,\dee{t}\,\dee{\theta} = \frac{\sqrt{a^2+b^2}}{ a}\int_{\theta=0}^{2\pi} \int_{t=0}^a t^3\,\dee{t}\,\dee{\theta}. \end{equation*}

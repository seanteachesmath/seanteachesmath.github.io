Solution 15.2.6.23.1.

(a) Since

\begin{align*} \pdiff{}{x}\frac{x}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} -\frac{3}{2}\frac{x(2x)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ &=\frac{-2x^2+y^2+z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ \pdiff{}{y}\frac{y}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} -\frac{3}{2}\frac{y(2y)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ &=\frac{x^2-2y^2+z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ \pdiff{}{z}\frac{z}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} &=\frac{1}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{3/2}} -\frac{3}{2}\frac{z(2z)}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}}\\ &=\frac{x^2+y^2-2z^2}{{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}} \end{align*}

the specified divergence is

\begin{align*} \vnabla\cdot\vF &= \frac{(-2x^2+y^2+z^2) + (x^2-2y^2+z^2) + (x^2+y^2-2z^2)} {{[x^2+y^2+z^2]}^{5/2}} =0 \end{align*}

if \((x,y,z)\ne\vZero\) and is not defined if \((x,y,z)=\vZero\text{.}\)

(b), (c) Set

\begin{align*} V_1 &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2 + (y - 2)^2 + z^2 \le 9}\\ V_2 &= \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2 + (y - 2)^2 + z^2 \le 1} \end{align*}

Here are side views of both \(V_1\) and \(V_2\text{.}\)

Both \(V_1\) and \(V_2\) are spherical balls centred on \((0,2,0)\text{.}\) The difference between them is that \(V_1\) has radius \(3\) while \(V_2\) has radius \(1\text{.}\) In particular \((0,0,0)\) is not in \(V_2\text{.}\) So \(\vnabla\cdot\vF\) is well-defined and zero throughout \(V_2\) and, by the divergence theorem (Theorem 15.2.2),

\begin{gather*} \dblInt_{S_2} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\tripInt_{V_2} \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} =0 \end{gather*}

On the other hand, \((0,0,0)\) is in \(V_1\text{.}\) We cannot blindly apply the divergence theorem to \(V_1\) â€” \(\vnabla\cdot\vF(x,y,z)\) is not defined at the point \((x,y,z)=(0,0,0)\) in \(V_1\text{.}\) We can work around this obstruction by

  • choosing a number \(\rho \gt 0\) that is small enough that the sphere
    \begin{equation*} S_\rho = \Set{(x,y,z)}{x^2+y^2+z^2=\rho^2} \end{equation*}
    is completely contained inside \(V_1\) (for example, \(\rho=\frac{1}{2}\) is fine)
  • and then removing the interior of \(S_\rho\) from \(V_1\text{.}\)

This produces

\begin{equation*} V_3 = \Set{(x,y)}{x^2 + (y - 2)^2 + z^2 \le 9,\ x^2+y^2+z^2\ge\rho^2} \end{equation*}

whose side view is sketched below.

The boundary of \(V_3\) consists of two parts

  • the sphere \(S_1\text{,}\) with outward normal and
  • the sphere \(S_\rho\) with inward normal \(\hn=-\frac{\vr}{|\vr|}\)

The divergence \(\vnabla\cdot\vF\) is well-defined and zero throughout \(V_3\) so that, by the divergence theorem,

\begin{gather*} 0=\tripInt_{V_3} \vnabla\cdot\vF\,\dee{V} =\dblInt_{S_1} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} +\dblInt_{S_\rho} \vF\cdot\Big(-\frac{\vr}{|\vr|}\Big)\,\dee{S} \end{gather*}

So

\begin{align*} \dblInt_{S_1} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} &=\dblInt_{S_\rho} \vF\cdot\Big(\frac{\vr}{|\vr|}\Big)\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_{S_\rho} \Big(\frac{\vr}{|\vr|^3}\Big)\cdot\Big(\frac{\vr}{|\vr|}\Big)\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_{S_\rho} \frac{1}{|\vr|^2}\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_{S_\rho} \frac{1}{\rho^2}\,\dee{S}\\ &=\frac{1}{\rho^2}\ 4\pi\rho^2=4\pi \end{align*}

since \(S_\rho\) is a sphere of radius \(\rho\) and hence of surface area \(4\pi\rho^2\text{.}\)

(d) The flux integrals \(\dblInt_{S_1} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\) and \(\dblInt_{S_2} \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\) are different, because the one point, \((0,0,0)\text{,}\) where \(\vnabla\cdot\vF\) fails to be well-defined and zero, is contained inside \(S_1\) but is not contained inside \(S_2\text{.}\)

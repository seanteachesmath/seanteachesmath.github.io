One alternative calculation of the curvature, using \(x(t) = a\cos t \text{,}\) \(y(t)=a\sin t\text{,}\) is

\begin{align*} \ka(t) &=\frac{\big| \diff{x}{t}(t)\difftwo{y}{t}(t)-\diff{y}{t}(t)\difftwo{x}{t}(t) \big|}{\Big[\big(\diff{x}{t}(t)\big)^2 +\big(\diff{y}{t}(t)\big)^2\Big]^{3/2}}\\ &=\frac{\big| -a\sin t\big(-a\sin t\big)-a\cos t\big(-a\cos t\big) \big|}{\big[\big(-a\sin t\big)^2 +\big(a\cos t\big)^2\big]^{3/2}}\\ &=\frac{1}{a} \end{align*}

Another alternative calculation of the curvature, using \(y(x) =\sqrt{a^2-x^2}\) (for the part of the circle with \(y \gt 0\)),

\begin{align*} y'(x) &= -\frac{x}{\sqrt{a^2-x^2}}=-\frac{x}{y(x)} \\ y''(x) &= -\frac{y(x) - xy'(x)}{y(x)^2} = -\frac{y(x)^2 + x^2}{y(x)^3} = -\frac{a^2}{y(x)^3} \end{align*}

is

\begin{align*} \ka(x) &=\frac{\big|\difftwo{y}{x}(x)\big|} {\Big[1+\big(\diff{y}{x}(x)\big)^2\Big]^{3/2}} =\frac{\frac{a^2}{y(x)^3}} {\Big[1+\frac{x^2}{y(x)^2}\Big]^{3/2}} =\frac{a^2}{\big[y(x)^2+x^2\big]^{3/2}}\\ &=\frac{1}{a} \end{align*}

Let \(n\ge 1\) be an integer.

  1. Let \(\va\in\bbbr^n\) and \(\veps \gt 0\text{.}\) The open ball of radius \(\veps\) centred on \(\va\) is
    \begin{equation*} B_\veps(\va)=\big\{\ \vx\in\bbbr^n\ \big|\ |\vx-\va| \lt \veps\ \big\} \end{equation*}
    Note the strict inequality in \(|\vx-\va| \lt \veps\text{.}\)
  2. A subset \(\cO\subset\bbbr^n\) is said to be an “open subset of \(\bbbr^n\)” if, for each point \(\va\in\cO\text{,}\) there is an \(\veps \gt 0\) such that \(B_\veps(\va)\subset\cO\text{.}\) Equivalently, \(\cO\) is open if and only if it is a union of open balls.
  3. A subset \(\cD\subset\bbbr^n\) is said to be (pathwise) connected if every pair of points in \(\cD\) can be joined by a piecewise smooth curve in \(\cD\text{.}\)

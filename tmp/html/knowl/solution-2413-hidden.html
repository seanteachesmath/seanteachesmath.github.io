(a) \(P\) is the \(x\)-component of \(\vF\text{.}\) As we travel vertically upward through \(A\text{,}\) that \(x\)-component decreases. Hence \(P_y \lt 0\) at \(A\text{.}\)

(b) \(Q\) is the \(y\)-component of \(\vF\text{.}\) As we travel horizontally to the right through \(A\text{,}\) that \(y\)-component increases. Hence \(Q_x \gt 0\) at \(A\text{.}\)

(c) \(\vnabla\times\vF = (Q_x-P_y)\hk\) and \(Q_x-P_y \gt 0\) at \(A\text{,}\) so that the curl of \(\vF\) at \(A\) is in the direction of \(+\hk\text{.}\)

(d) Along the curve \(\cC_1\) the magnitude of the angle between \(\vF\) and \(\dee{\vr}\) is less than \(90^\circ\text{,}\) so that \(\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} \gt 0\) and \(\int_{\cC_1}\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} \gt 0\text{.}\)

(e) Along the curve \(\cC_2\) the magnitude of the angle between \(\vF\) and \(\dee{\vr}\) is greater than \(90^\circ\text{,}\) so that \(\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} \lt 0\) and \(\int_{\cC_2}\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} \lt 0\text{.}\)

(f) If \(\vF\) were conservative, we would have \(\int_{\cC_1}\vF\cdot\dee{\vr} =\int_{\cC_2}\vF\cdot\dee{\vr}\text{.}\) As these two integrals have opposite signs \(\vF\) is not conservative. (Since \(\vF\) is not conservative, it is not the gradient of some function. At \(A\text{,}\) \(P_x \gt 0\) and \(Q_y \gt 0\text{.}\) So \(\vF\) is not divergence free and is not the curl of a vector potential.)

The slope of the curve is \(\ds\diff{y}{x}\text{.}\) To find \(\ds\diff{y}{x}\text{,}\) we use the chain rule:

\begin{align*} \diff{y}{t}&=\diff{y}{x}\cdot\diff{x}{t}\\ \diff{}{t}\left\{\cos^2 t\right\}&=\diff{y}{x}\cdot\diff{}{t}\{\sin t\}\\ -2\cos t \sin t &= \diff{y}{x} \cdot \cos t\\ \diff{y}{x}&=-2\sin t\\ \end{align*}

So, when \(t=\dfrac{10\pi}{3}\text{,}\)

\begin{align*} \diff{y}{x}&=-2\sin\left(\frac{10\pi}{3}\right)=-2\left(-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)=\sqrt{3}. \end{align*}

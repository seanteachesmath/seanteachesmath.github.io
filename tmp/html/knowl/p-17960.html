Let \(R\) be a finite region in the \(xy\)-plane, whose boundary, \(C\text{,}\) consists of a single, piecewise smooth, simple closed curve that is oriented couterclockwise. “Simple” means that the curve does not intersect itself. Use Green's theorem to show that

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_R\vnabla\cdot\vF\ \dee{x}\,\dee{y}=\oint_C\vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{s} \end{equation*}

where \(\vF=F_1\,\hi+F_2\,\hj\text{,}\) \(\hn\) is the outward unit normal to \(C\) and \(s\) is the arclength along \(C\text{.}\)

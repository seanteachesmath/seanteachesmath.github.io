Let

\begin{equation*} \vB = (2x)\,\hi+(2z-2x)\,\hj +(2x-2z)\,\hk \end{equation*}

This vector field obeys

\begin{equation*} \vnabla\cdot\vB = \frac{\partial }{\partial x}(2x) +\frac{\partial }{\partial y}(2z-2x) +\frac{\partial }{\partial z}(2x-2z) =0 \end{equation*}

and so passes the screening test of Theorem 15.1.12. We'll now find a vector potential \(\vA= A_1\,\hi + A_2\,\hj + A_3\,\hk\) for \(\vB\text{.}\) As in the last example, we'll simplify the computations by further requiring 10  that \(A_3=0\text{.}\)

\(\hT\) is tangent to the curve, while \(\hN\) is perpendicular to it.

Using the right-hand rule and \(\hB=\hT \times \hN\text{,}\) \(\hB\) points out of the page (towards the reader).

To see this, point the fingers of your right hand in the direction of \(\hT\text{,}\) and curl them inwards until they are in the direction of \(\hN\text{.}\) To do this, your thumb must be pointing towards you, not away from you. Your thumb shows the direction of \(\hT \times \hN\text{.}\)

It is not too hard to make this rigorous using the principle of mathematical induction. The interested reader should do a little search-engine-ing. Induction is a very standard technique for proving statements of the form “For every natural number \(n\text{,}\)…”. For example
\begin{align*} \text{For every natural number $n$, }& \sum_{k=1}^n k = \frac{n(n+1)}{2} & \text{or}\\ \text{For every natural number $n$, }& \ddiff{n}{}{x} \left\{ \log(1+x)\right\} = (-1)^{n-1} \frac{(n-1)!}{(1+x)^n} \end{align*}
It was also used by Polya (1887–1985) to give a very convincing (but subtly (and deliberately) flawed) proof that all horses have the same colour.

Direct Solution. The surface is given by the implicit equation \(f(x,y,z)=0\) with \(f(x,y,z)=x^2+y^2+2z^2-1\text{.}\) Hence, by (14.3.3),

\begin{equation*} \hn\,\dee{S}=\frac{\vnabla f}{\vnabla f\cdot\hk}\dee{x}\dee{y} =\frac{2x\hi+2y\hj+4z\hk}{4z}\dee{x}\dee{y} \end{equation*}

This \(\hn\) has positive \(\hk\) component. Assume that it is the desired \(\hn\text{,}\) though this was not specified in the question. Since

\begin{align*} \vnabla\times\vF &=\det\left[\begin{matrix} \hi &\hj &\hk\\ \tfrac{\partial }{\partial x} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial y} & \tfrac{\partial }{\partial z}\\ x^2-y-1 & e^{\cos y}+z^3 & 2xz+z^5 \end{matrix} \right]\\ &=-3z^2\,\hi-2z\,\hj+\hk \end{align*}

we have

\begin{align*} &\dblInt_S\vnabla\times \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S}\\ &=\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1}\hskip-10pt\big(-3z(x,y)^2\,\hi-2z(x,y)\,\hj+\hk\big) \cdot\frac{2x\,\hi+2y\,\hj+4z(x,y)\,\hk}{4z(x,y)}\ \dee{x}\dee{y}\\ &=\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1}\Big(-\frac{3}{2}x\,z(x,y)-y+1\Big)\ \dee{x}\dee{y} \end{align*}

Since \(y\) is an odd function of \(y\) and \(x\,z(x,y)=x\sqrt{\half(1-x^2-y^2)}\) is an odd function of \(x\text{,}\) they both integrate to zero. Hence

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vnabla\times \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_{x^2+y^2\le 1}1\ \dee{x}\dee{y} =\pi \end{equation*}

Tricky Solution. Let \(V\) be the solid \(x^2+y^2+2z^2\le 1\text{,}\) \(z\ge 0\text{.}\) The surface of \(V\) consists of \(S\) with upward pointing normal and the disk \(D=\Set{(x,y,z)}{z=0,\ x^2+y^2\le 1}\) with normal \(-\hk\text{.}\) By the divergence theorem, Theorem 15.2.2,

\begin{align*} \dblInt_S\vnabla\times \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} +\dblInt_D\vnabla\times \vF\cdot(-\hk)\,\dee{S} &=\tripInt_V\vnabla\cdot\vnabla\times\vF\ \dee{V}\\ &=\tripInt_V 0\ \dee{V}=0 \end{align*}

Hence

\begin{equation*} \dblInt_S\vnabla\times \vF\cdot\hn\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_D\vnabla\times \vF\cdot\hk\,\dee{S} =\dblInt_D \dee{S} =\pi \end{equation*}

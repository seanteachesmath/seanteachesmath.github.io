(d) Another formula for the normal component of acceleration is \(\ka(t)\big(\diff{s}{t}(t)\big)^2\hat\vN(t)\text{.}\) So the magnitude of the normal component of acceleration is \(\ka(t)\big(\diff{s}{t}(t)\big)^2\) and, by part (c),

\begin{gather*} \ka(t)\left(\diff{s}{t}(t)\right)^2 =\big|t\cos t \,\hi - t\sin t \,\hj \big| =t \end{gather*}

Consequently, by part (a),

\begin{equation*} \ka(t) = \frac{t}{\left(\diff{s}{t}(t)\right)^2} = \frac{1}{5t} \end{equation*}

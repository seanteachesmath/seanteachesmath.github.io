Consider a spherical shell of radius \(a\) with mass density \(\mu\) per unit area. Think of it as a hollow planet 9 . We are going to determine the gravitational force that it exerts on a particle of mass \(m\) a distance \(b\) away from its centre. This particle can be either outside the shell (\(b \gt a\)) or inside the shell (\(b \lt a\)). We can choose the coordinate system so that the centre of the shell is at the origin and the particle is at \((0,0,b)\text{.}\) By Newton's law of gravitation, the force exerted on the particle by a tiny piece of the shell of surface area \(\dee{S}\) located at \(\vr\) is

\begin{equation*} \frac{G\,(\mu\dee{S})\,m}{|\vr-(0,0,b)|^3}(\vr-(0,0,b)) \end{equation*}

Here \(G\) is the gravitational constant, \(\mu\dee{S}\) is the mass of the tiny piece of shell, \(m\) is the mass of the particle

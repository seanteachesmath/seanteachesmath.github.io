A method of audio compression relies on reducing a complicated wave (see Figure 3.2.4) into a colection of its simpler components (see Figure 3.2.1, Figure 3.2.2, and Figure 3.2.3). The labels 'low', 'medium', and 'high' are all relative, but useful for comparison of the images and corresponding sounds. These waves all repeat fairly quickly, which is required if we actually want to hear them. Waves repeating much more slowly could be heard by elephants, while those repeating much more quickly might confuse bats.

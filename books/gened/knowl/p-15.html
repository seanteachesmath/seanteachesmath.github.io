With natural or counting numbers we can generate an infinite number of patterns. Some of these patterns come from lists, or sequences, of numbers. For example, how can we generate

\begin{equation*} 1, 2, 3, 4, \dots \end{equation*}

and what might come next? I would guess \(5\text{.}\) What about

\begin{equation*} 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, \dots \end{equation*}

and what might come next? I would guess \(8\text{.}\) The first of these patterns arises by repeated addition of ones, the second is a bit more complicated - the Fibonacci series. We start with two 1's and the next number is the sum of the previous two. Mathematically we might introduce `subscript notation' to track the numbers in an ordered list,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] F_0 \amp = 1\\ F_1 \amp = 1\\ F_n \amp = F_{n-1} + F_{n-2}, \text{ for } n \ge 2 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

This pattern is used (and perhaps more often abused) throughout the natural sciences as a source or explanation of biological patterns.

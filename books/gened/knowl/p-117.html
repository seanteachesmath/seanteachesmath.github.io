Mathematical modeling of infectious disease Nearly every year we are faced with a newly emerged or reemerged infectious pathogen somewhere around the planet. This is a math problem, though one shared with experts from many other disciplines. During the current coronavirus outbreak, caused by the pathogen known as COVID19, mathematicians, some acting as mathematicians and others more as statisticians, have featured prominently. Mathematicians (and specialty-statisticians often referred to as epidemiologists) and others are using the best available knowledge about the virus, its origin, its transmission, and its effect on the infected, to try to understand the pace of the current outbreak and to try to understand its future.

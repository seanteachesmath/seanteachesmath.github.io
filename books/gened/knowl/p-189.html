When modeling spread of something infectious (a pathogen, a rumor, a behavior) we break a population into relevant groups. One of the simplest breakdowns is into groups of

  1. susceptible (these are the `healthy' people)

  2. infectious

  3. recovered

There are some subtleties here that I will mention, but not spend any more time on. The name of the group `infectious' suggests that these individuals are not only \textit{infected}, but are also capable of transmitting their infection to others. The `recovered' group is sometimes called `removed' to indicate the group of individuals that are \textit{removed} from the process of infection either by natural recovery or death.

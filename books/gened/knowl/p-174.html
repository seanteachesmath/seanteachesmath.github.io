An easily overlooked complication lies in Table \ref{tab::paper}: by the sixth fold, the paper is actually thicker than it is wide. There is no longer enough paper to fold! Assuming that we could fold the paper 100 times, it would be \(1.268\times10^{28} \text{mm}\) (or \(1.268\times10^{22} \text{kilometers}\)) thick! These results, are illustrated in Fig. \ref{fig::paper_exp}. The diameter of the earth is \(1.2742\times10^4 \text{kilometers}\text{,}\) so this result is shocking if not almost incomprehensible.

Word problems get a bad rap.

  • What goes where?

  • What even matters?

  • Formatting numbers (e.g., is it \(50\%\) or \(0.50\text{?}\))

  • ''I have to think!?!''

  • Show your work.

  • Problems are time-consuming.

  • Interpretation of answer often required.

But, work problems are an idealized version of most real-world problem solving; in this case we know the course or book we are studying, the unit or chapter, and the topic or section. So, it should be the case that we know roughly \textit{what} to do, even if we do not exactly know \textit{how} to do it mechanically.

The answer to this requires us to think of a new type of number, the imaginary number. Here we say that the imaginary number \(i\) is a number such that,

\begin{equation*} i\cdot i = i^2 = -1 \end{equation*}

or, from the reverse that

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{-1} = i. \end{equation*}

With this new concept we can build numbers called complex numbers that consist of our typical `real part' combined with a term representing this new `imaginary part'. In general complex numbers are of the form \(a+bi\text{,}\) where \(a\) and \(b\) come from our usual sets of numbers; they can be whole numbers, fractions or decimals, positives or negatives.

Mathematical modeling is the art, and science, of writing and studying your own word problems about interesting situations. We 1) take real-world knowledge, 2) identify, often with the help of experts, seemingly important facts and details, 3) develop a verbal or visual `story' about what is happening, 4) translate the words and diagrams to mathematical notation and formulas, and 5) study the math problem and see what we have learned.

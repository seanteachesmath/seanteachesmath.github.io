We can visualize this multiplication by constructing the complex plane: two perpendicular axes, one that describes real values and one that describes imaginary values, which collectively describe all of the complex numbers. Plotting these values in the complex plane and tracing out the steps of multiplication, we can convince ourselves that each step multiplication by \(i\) serves to rotate our number counterclockwise around the origin of the graph. Here our numbers lie on the axes (which are perpendicular to one another), so tracking the rotations might be pictured a bit more easily than it can be in general.

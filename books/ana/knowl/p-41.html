As we will soon see, the ‘best’ reduced order polynomial approximation \(P_{n-1}\) to a polynomial \(P_n(x)\) is given by

\begin{equation*} P_{n-1}(x) = P_{n}(x) - a_n\tilde T_n(x) \end{equation*}

where \(\tilde T_n(x)\) is the \(n^\text{th}\) order monic Chebyshev polynomial and \(a_n\) is the coefficient of the highest order term in \(P_n(x)\text{.}\) To economize the polynomial approximation to \(f(x) = e^x\) given by a \(4^\text{th}\) order Maclaurin polynomial, we can subtract \(\dfrac{1}{24}\tilde T_4(x)\) from the original \(P_4(x)\) (note that \(a_4=\dfrac{1}{24}\) in the original polynomial representation). This alters coefficients of all powers of \(x\) of lower orders that are present in \(T_4(x)\) and eliminates the \(4^\text{th}\) order term entirely. Notice in Figure Figure 3.16 that the maximum error for the third order function is only slightly worse for \(x=1.0\) than for the fourth order function.

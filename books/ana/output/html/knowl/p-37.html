By a theorem, we have that the optimal choice of nodes for polynomial approximation by a degree \(n\) polynomial is given by the zeros of of the \((n+1)^\text{st}\) Chebyshev polynomial \(T_{n+1}(x)\text{.}\) For example, as given in Figure Figure 3.13, we take the approximation of \(f(x) = e^x\) on \([0, 1]\) with three points. We expect to to take, \(\tilde x_0 = 0\text{,}\) \(\tilde x_1 = 0.5\text{,}\) and \(\tilde x_2 = 1.0\text{,}\) but achieve better performance with Chebyshev nodes \(\bar x_i\text{.}\) The nodes for interpolation are given by the zeros of the Chebyshev polynomial \(T_3(x)\text{,}\) which in turn are given by \(\bar x_k = \cos\left(\dfrac{2k-1}{2n}\pi\right)\) for \(k=1, 2, 3\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \bar x_1 &= \cos\left(\dfrac{2(1)-1}{2(3)}\cdot\pi\right) = \cos\left(\dfrac{1\cdot\pi}{6}\right)= \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} ~\approx~ 0.866\\ \bar x_2 &= \cos\left(\dfrac{2(2)-1}{2(3)}\cdot\pi\right) = \cos\left(\dfrac{3\cdot\pi}{6}\right)= 0\\ \bar x_3 &= \cos\left(\dfrac{2(3)-1}{2(3)}\cdot\pi\right) = \cos\left(\dfrac{5\cdot\pi}{6}\right)= -\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} ~\approx~ -0.866 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

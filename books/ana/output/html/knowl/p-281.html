The coin is unbalanced and \(P(\text{success})\) is \(3\) times larger than \(P(\text{failure})\text{.}\) We first have to figure out the value for \(P(\text{success}) = \theta\text{.}\) We have said that \(P(\text{success}) = 3\cdot P(\text{failure})\) or \(\theta = 3\cdot (1-\theta)\text{.}\) This gives \(\theta = 0.75\) and, as above,

\begin{equation*} b(5; 9, 0.75) = {9 \choose 5}0.75^5(1-0.75)^{9-5} \approx 0.1168 \end{equation*}

Notice that the definition
\begin{equation*} m=\dfrac{y_1 − y_0}{x_1 − x_0} \end{equation*}
gives us all we need to derive both point-slope form and slope-intercept form. Let our point be \((x_0,y_0)\) and instead of \((x_1,y_1)\text{,}\) choose \((x,y)\) to be any other point on the line.
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] m \amp =\dfrac{y − y_0}{x − x_0}\\ m(x-x_0) \amp =\dfrac{y − y_0}{\cancel{x − x_0}}\cancel{(x-x_0)}\\ m(x-x_0) \amp = y − y_0\\ y_0 + m(x-x_0) \amp = y\\ y \amp = y_0 + m(x-x_0) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

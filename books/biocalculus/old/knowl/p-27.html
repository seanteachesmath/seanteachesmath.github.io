One (of many) important skills to succeed in this class and beyond is abstraction. This is

  1. the realization that the letter \(x\) in an equation might represent the physical size of a cell, the height of a tree, the size of a population, the age of an animal

  2. the understanding that although we are interested in a specific biological problem, we have to carefully apply the rules of mathematics to achieve a solution -- we solve an equation the same way whether or not it is purely a mathematical problem or represents a real-world problem

  3. the recognition that many visibly different biological dynamics might be linked by the same mathematical description, and that what we learn from one system could teach us about another -- even though we might not recognize any direct biological similarities between the two systems

Theorem 12.2.NaN.

Suppose \(w(x)=\frac{T(x)}{B(x)}\) where the 'top' function \(T = T(x)\) and the 'bottom' function \(B = B(x)\) are both differentiable and \(B(x)\neq 0\text{.}\) Then

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] w'(x) \amp= \frac{B(x)T'(x) - T(x)B'(x)}{(B(x))^2}\\ \frac{dw}{dx} \amp= \frac{B(x)\cdot\frac{dT}{dx} - T(x)\cdot\frac{dB}{dx}}{(B(x))^2} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Example 12.2.NaN. Basic quotient rule.

Suppose \(w(x)=\dfrac{x^2+3x+7}{2x^2}\text{.}\) Find \(w'(x)\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] w'(x) \amp= \frac{d}{dx}\left(\frac{x^2+3x+7}{2x^2}\right)\\ \amp= \frac{(2x^2)\cdot\dfrac{d}{dx}\left(x^2+3x+7\right) - (x^2+3x+7)\cdot\dfrac{d}{dx}\left(2x^2\right) }{(2x^2)^2}\\ w'(x) \amp= \frac{(2x^2)(2x+3) - (x^2+3x+7)(4x) }{(2x^2)^2}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

This example was written just to practice the technique, so we are not interested in any additional simplification of the final answer. Many of the errors in such a problem are the result of trying to simplify by dividing out what look like, but are not common factors on the right-hand side.

For problems arising in applications, the factoring or simplification is often nicer, but it still takes quite a bit of intuition to ways to group terms in such a complicated expression for the purposes of simplification.

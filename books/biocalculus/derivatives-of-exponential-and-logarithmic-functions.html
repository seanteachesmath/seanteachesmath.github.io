Subsection 2.3.1 Quick practice

  1. What is the derivative of \(f(x) = 3e^x\text{?}\)

  2. What is the derivative of \(f(x) = 3e^{7x}\text{?}\)

  3. What is the second derivative of \(f(x) = \ln(x)\text{?}\)

Answers:

  1. The derivative of \(f(x) = 3e^x\) is \(f'(x) = \dfrac{d}{dx}\left( 3e^x\right) = 3\dfrac{d}{dx}\left(e^x\right) = 3e^x\text{.}\)

  2. The derivative of \(f(x) = 3e^{7x}\) is \(f'(x) = \dfrac{d}{dx}\left( 3e^{7x}\right) = 3\dfrac{d}{dx}\left(e^{7x}\right) = 3(7e^{7x}) = 21e^{7x}\text{.}\)

  3. To find the second derivative of \(f(x) = \ln(x)\text{,}\) we need the first derivative \(f'(x) = \dfrac{1}{x}\text{.}\) The first derivative is \(f'(x) = \dfrac{1}{x} = x^{-1}\) (recall the rules for exponents/powers). The derivative of \(f'(x)\) or second derivative of \(f(x)\) is,

    \begin{equation*} f''(x) = \dfrac{d}{dx}\left(x^{-1}\right) = -1(x^{-2}) = -x^{-2} \end{equation*}

    (bring the old power down and reduce by one for the new power).

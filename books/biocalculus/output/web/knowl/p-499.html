To find the second derivative of \(f(x) = \ln(x)\text{,}\) we need the first derivative \(f'(x) = \dfrac{1}{x}\text{.}\) The first derivative is \(f'(x) = \dfrac{1}{x} = x^{-1}\) (recall the rules for exponents/powers). The derivative of \(f'(x)\) or second derivative of \(f(x)\) is,

\begin{equation*} f''(x) = \dfrac{d}{dx}\left(x^{-1}\right) = -1(x^{-2}) = -x^{-2} \end{equation*}

(bring the old power down and reduce by one for the new power).

The value of \(\displaystyle \int_{0}^1 t^{2}\,dt\text{,}\) which gives the area under the curve \(f(t) = t^{2}\) between \(t=0\) and \(t=1\) can be calculated using the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus. Since the integrand \(t^{2}\) is continuous on the interval from \(t=0\) to \(t=1\) we find

\begin{equation*} \int_{0}^1 t^{2}\,dt =\left.\left(\frac{t^{3}}{3}\right)\right|_{0}^1 = \frac{1^{3}}{3} - \frac{0^{3}}{3} = \frac{1}{3} \end{equation*}

which compares favorably with our earlier estimates.

Theorem 2.2.4. Power rule for derivatives.

Suppose \(f(x)=x^n\) for integers \(n\neq0\) (this allows \(n\) to be negative or positive)

\begin{equation*} \boxed{f'(x) = \frac{d}{dx}\Big(x^n\Big) = nx^{n-1}} \end{equation*}

This works for all powers \(n\) including fractions, provided that \(x>0\text{.}\)

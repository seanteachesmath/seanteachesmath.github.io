Example 1.4.6.

Here we look at the onset of AIDS symptoms in the years after initial HIV infection. Four years after infection 17% had symptoms, and seven years after infection 33% had symptoms.

  1. Write the formula for a linear function that relates the percent of patients with AIDS symptoms to time (years measured since infection).

  2. Define and interpret the slope and intercept (units included).

  3. How many patients (what percent) have symptoms after five and a half years?

  4. Using this model, at what time would you predict that half of all exposed patients have AIDS symptoms? What must you do to answer this question?

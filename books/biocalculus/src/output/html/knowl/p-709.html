Draw \(n=5\) ‘left-hand’ rectangles to approximate the area under the curve. Heights can be estimated from the graph or calculated using the corresponding function value.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \left(\substack{\text{area}\\\text{under}\\\text{curve}}\right) &\approx \text{sum of the area of the rectangles}\\ &= R_{1}+ R_2 + R_3 + R_4 + R_5\\ &= w_{1}h_{1} + w_2h_2+ w_3h_3+ w_4h_4+ w_5h_5\\ & = (0.2)(0^{2}) + (0.2)(0.2^{2}) + (0.2)(0.4^{2}) +(0.2)(0.6^{2}) +(0.2)(0.8^{2})\\ & = \dots\\ & = 0.24\end{aligned} \end{equation*}

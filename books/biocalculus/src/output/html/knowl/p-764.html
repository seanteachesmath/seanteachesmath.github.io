This turns out to be one of the simplest, most useful, but also most cleverly disguised applications of the technique of integration by \(u\)-substitution. Our goal is to put the \(A\)-like variables on the left and leave everything else on the right-hand side and immediately integrate with respect to time \(t\text{.}\) This results in

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \dfrac{dA}{dt} \amp = -k A^{0}\\ \dfrac{dA}{dt} \amp = -k\\ \int\dfrac{dA}{dt}\,dt \amp = \int -k\, dt \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

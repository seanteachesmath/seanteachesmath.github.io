\begin{equation*} m=\frac{y_1-y_0}{x_1-x_0} \end{equation*}

gives us all we need to derive both point-slope form and slope-intercept form. Let our point be \((x_0, y_0)\) and instead of \((x_1, y_1)\text{,}\) choose \((x, y)\) to be any other point on the line.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} m&=\frac{y-y_0}{x-x_0}&&(\text{definition})\\ %m(x-x_0)&= y-y_0&&(\text{multiply by denom.})\\ %m(x-x_0)+y_0&= y&&(\text{add $y$-coordinate})\\ %y&=m(x-x_0)+y_0&&(\text{flip equation})\\\end{aligned} \end{equation*}

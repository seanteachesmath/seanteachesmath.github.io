Preface Users guide

Welcome to what I hope is a fun, challenging, and rewarding class. This packet of notes and a scientific calculator are really the only things you should need for this class this semester. Each day we’ll work through a few pages together in class.

Occasionally I will pass out supplemental notes when something relevant hits the news or post notes online if we skip an example in the interest of time. With a three-hole punch, you should easily be able to add to these notes.

With the exception of a few ‘Quick Problems’ at the end of each section, the one thing you should notice that is missing from this packet is anything that resembles homework. The ‘Quick Problems’ and included answers are merely for your practice and will not be collected. Most homework for this class will be posted and submitted using a free online homework system. Occasionally I will have you hand in written problems with graphs or explanations.

While it might seem a bit wasteful, I have probably printed the associated notes one-sided to give you additional space for writing, drawing, or scratch work.

Our first test covers Topics 1 to 7, our second test covers Topics 8 to 14, our third test covers Topics 15 to 18. The final will be comprehensive and may include questions from Topics 19 to 21 if time permits.

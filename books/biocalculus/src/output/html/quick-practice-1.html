Subsection 3.1 Quick practice

  1. Oklahoma’s State Champion Sparkleberry tree   has a diameter at breast height of \(5.09\) inches, convert this to centimeters.[quick::dbh]

  2. Given the diameter found in Question [cross-reference to target(s) "quick::dbh" missing] (in centimeters), what is the area of the corresponding cross-sectional slice of tree? [quick::area]

read more here http://www.forestry.ok. gov/champion-trees

Answers:

  1. Oklahoma’s State Champion Sparkleberry tree has a diameter at breast height of \(5.1\) inches, as any good scientist should, we convert this to centimeters using

    \begin{equation*} \dfrac{5.1\text{ inches}}{1}\cdot\dfrac{2.54 \text{ cm}}{1 \text{ inches}} = \dfrac{5.09 \cancel{\text{ inches}}}{1}\cdot\dfrac{2.54 \text{ cm}}{1 \cancel{\text{ inches}}} = 12.93 \text{ centimeters}\text{.} \end{equation*}

  2. Given the diameter found in Question [cross-reference to target(s) "quick::dbh" missing] (in centimeters) we need to find the radius before computing the area. The radius is half of the diameter, \(r = 6.46\text{,}\) so the area of the corresponding cross-sectional slice of tree is \(A = \pi r^2 = \pi(6.46 \text{ cm})^2 =131.28 \text{ cm}^2\)

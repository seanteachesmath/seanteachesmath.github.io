Example 18.6. an exact value.

The value of \(\int_0^1 t\,dt\text{,}\) which gives the area under the curve \(f(t) = t^2\) between \(t = 0\) and \(t = 1\) can be calculated using the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus. Since the integrand \(t^2\) is continuous on the interval from \(t=0\) to \(t=1\) we find

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \int_0^1 t^2\,dt \amp= \Big(\dfrac{t^3}{3}\Big)\Big|_0^1\\ \amp= \Big(\dfrac{1^3}{3}\Big) -\Big(\dfrac{0^3}{3}\Big)\\ \int_0^1 t\,dt \amp= \dfrac{1}{3} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

which compares favorably with our earlier estimates.

Zeroth order.

Within this family, the first formula comes from the zeroth order reaction given by

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{dA}{dt} = -kA^0. \end{equation*}

Since this is \(A^0\text{,}\) the right-hand side simplifies to the constant \(-k\text{.}\) At this point, we are looking for an unknown function \(A(t)\) whose derivative is a constant \(-k\text{.}\) Thinking about this from the perspective of differentiation, we are looking for a function \(A(t)\) that is linear with a slope of \(-k\text{.}\) So,

\begin{equation*} A(t) = -kt+A_{0}, \end{equation*}

where \(A_{0}\) is the constant of integration.

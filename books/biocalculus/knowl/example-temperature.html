Example 4.3. Temperature conversion.

Here we will derive a common (and commonly mis-remembered) temperature formula.

  1. Recall: What is the formula for computing the temperature in F if you know temperature in C?

  2. Data: Consider the following data: \((0, 32), (20, 68), (37, 98.6), (100, 212)\text{.}\)

  3. Plot the points to determine whether a linear function holds.

  4. Slope: Identify a pair of points and compute the slope.

  5. Linear functions: Now write \(F(C)\text{,}\) first in point-slope form, then simplify to slope-intercept form.

Theorem 10.17. Power rule for derivatives.

Suppose \(f(x) = x^n\) for integers \(n \ne 0\) (this allows \(n\) to be negative or positive)

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{d}{dx}\Big(x^n\Big) = \dfrac{1}{n+1}x^{n+1} \end{equation*}

This works for all powers n including fractions, provided that x > 0.

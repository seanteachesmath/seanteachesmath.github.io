  1. In \(\dfrac{dq}{dt} = 2.1q(1-q)\text{,}\) the unknown quantity that is being differentiated, \(q = q(t)\text{,}\) appears on the right-hand side, and does so in the absence of the variable \(t\) on the right-hand side, this is indicative of an autonomous equation.

  2. The indefinite integral \(\displaystyle\int 3x+2\,dx\) evaluates to \(\displaystyle\int 3x+2\,dx = \dfrac{3}{2}x^{2}+2x+c\text{.}\)

  3. Integrating the rate of change of length gives length itself as a function of time.

  4. The indefinite integral \(\displaystyle\int 0\,dx\) evaluates to \(\displaystyle\int 0\,dx = c\text{.}\)

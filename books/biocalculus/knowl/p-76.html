Last time in our limit definition, we used the limit as \(\Delta t \to 0\text{.}\) We will briefly show why that is equivalent to the approach above and computationally easier. Since \(\Delta t = b a\text{,}\) we have \(b = a+\Delta t\text{.}\) Taking \(b \to a\) is the same as taking \(\Delta t \to 0\text{.}\) Substitute \(b = a+\Delta t\) in the definition for \(m_{\text{tan}}\) and simplify,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] m_{\text{tan}} \amp = \lim_{b\to a}\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\\ amp = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0}\dfrac{f(a+\Delta t)-f(a)}{(a+\Delta t)-a}\\ m_{\text{tan}} amp = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0}\dfrac{f(a+\Delta t)-f(a)}{\Delta t} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

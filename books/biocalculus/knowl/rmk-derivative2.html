Remark 11.4. Notation (using derivatives).

For a function \(f(x)\text{,}\) the derivative is written using the notation

\begin{equation*} \text{prime}\rightarrow f'(x) = \dfrac{df}{dx} \leftarrow \text{differential}, \end{equation*}

and is to be interpreted in any of the three following ways,

  1. intuitively as the instantaneous rate of change

  2. geometrically as the slope of the tangent line

  3. mathematically as \(\displaystyle f'(x) = \dfrac{df}{dx} = \lim_{h\to0}\dfrac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h}\)

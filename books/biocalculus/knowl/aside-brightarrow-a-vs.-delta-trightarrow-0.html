Aside (\(b\rightarrow a\) vs. \(\Delta t\rightarrow 0\)).

Last time in our limit definition, we used the limit as \(\Delta t\rightarrow0\text{.}\) We will briefly show why that is equivalent to the approach above and computationally easier. Since \(\Delta t = b-a\text{,}\) we have \(b=a+\Delta t\text{.}\) Taking \(b\rightarrow a\) is the same as taking \(\Delta t \rightarrow 0\text{.}\) Substitute \(b=a+\Delta t\) in the definition for \(m_{tan}\) and simplify,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} m_{tan} & = \lim_{b\rightarrow a}\dfrac{f(b)-f(a)}{b-a}\\ & = \lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0}\dfrac{f(a+\Delta t)-f(a)}{(a+\Delta t)-a}\\ m_{tan} & = \lim_{\Delta t \rightarrow 0}\dfrac{f(a+\Delta t)-f(a)}{\Delta t}\end{aligned} \end{equation*}

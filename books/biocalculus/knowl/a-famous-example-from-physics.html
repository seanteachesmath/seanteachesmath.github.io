A famous example from physics.

We often use rates of change to describe movement of an object.

Consider a seed in a flower in a very tall tree. The seed pops straight into the air with an initial velocity of 9.8 meters per second from an initial height of 14.7 meters. The position (height in meters from ground level) is described by

\begin{equation*} s(t) = -4.9t^{2}+9.8t+14.7, \end{equation*}

find the velocity of the seed and its acceleration.

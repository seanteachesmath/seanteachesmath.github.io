A relationship \(f(x)\) defined by
  1. \(y=f(x)=mx+b\text{,}\) with constants \(m\) and \(b\text{,}\) or
  2. \(y=f(x)= y_{0} + m(x-x_{0})\text{,}\) with constant \(m\) and a point \((x_{0}, y_{0})\)
is called a linear function between the variables \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\) If the formula involves only adding and subtracting constants with variables multiplied by constants, the equation describes a linear function.

Consider a seed in a flower in a very tall tree. From an initial height of 14.7 meters, the seed pops straight into the air with an initial velocity of 9.8 meters per second (\(\displaystyle v(0)=9.8~\frac{\text{m}}{\text{s}}\)) and an acceleration due to gravity of \(\displaystyle a(t)=-9.8~\frac{\text{m}}{\text{s${}^{2}$}}\) (in the downward direction). Find the velocity function \(v(t)\) and use that to find its position function \(s(t)\) of the seed.

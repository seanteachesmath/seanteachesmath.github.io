Consider a population of mice that obeys the equation \(N(t)=1+2t+t^{2}\text{.}\) The number of mice with hantavirus is described by \(I(t)=3t\text{,}\) (\(t\) in months). The prevalence of hantavirus in the population is defined as
\begin{equation*} p(t)=\displaystyle\frac{I(t)}{N(t)} \end{equation*}
How does the prevalence of hantavirus in the population change with time?

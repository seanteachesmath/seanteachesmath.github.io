Polynomials.
Since a polynomial (e.g., a quadratic or a cubic function) is the sum of terms that involve variables (like \(x\)) to powers (like \(x^{2}\)) multiplied by constants (like \(3x^{2}\)), we could decompose limits of polynomials using Theorem 1.7.8 and evaluate them with Theorem 1.7.5.

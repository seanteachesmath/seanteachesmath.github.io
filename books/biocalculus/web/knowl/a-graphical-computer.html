A graphical computer.
We have a powerful technique for studying the behavior of such models using only a pencil and paper. This technique is called cobwebbing, and its name will be come clear shortly.
Note: you often will not have the data points on this graph, so they have been removed.This is how we ‘cobweb’:
  1. Graph the updating function and the diagonal (the identity or line \(y=x\))
  2. Start at initial condition and go up or down to the updating function and over to the diagonal
  3. Repeat for as many steps as needed to find the pattern
  4. Sketch the solution at times \(t=0, 1, 2, \dots\)

The critical point of \(f(x) = 2x^{2}-7x+3\) is \(x = \dfrac{7}{4}\) and the second derivative of \(f(x)\) is \(f''(x) = 4\text{.}\) Since \(f\left(\dfrac{7}{4}\right)=4>0\) (as we just saw in the previous Question), \(f(x)\) is concave up at the critical point which means the point \(\left(\dfrac{7}{4}, -\dfrac{33}{8}\right)\) is a local minimum.

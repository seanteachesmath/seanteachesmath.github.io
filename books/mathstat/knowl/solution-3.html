Solution 3.2.2.1.

Since \(\mu = E[X]\text{,}\) we have

\begin{align*} \mu = E[X] \amp = \sum\limits_{x=1}^5 x\cdot f(x)\\ \amp = \sum\limits_{x=1}^5 x\cdot \left(\dfrac{2x-1}{25}\right)\\ \amp = \sum\limits_{x=1}^5 \left(\dfrac{2}{25}x^2 - \dfrac{1}{25}x\right)\\ \amp = \left(\dfrac{2}{25}\sum\limits_{x=1}^5 x^2\right) - \left(\dfrac{1}{25}\sum\limits_{x=1}^5 x\right)\\ \amp = \left(\dfrac{2}{25}\left(55\right)\right) - \left(\dfrac{1}{25}\left(15\right)\right)\\ \mu = E[X] \amp = \dfrac{19}{5} \end{align*}

So, the mean of the distribution of the random variable \(X\) is

\begin{equation*} \mu = E[X] = \dfrac{19}{6}\text{.} \end{equation*}

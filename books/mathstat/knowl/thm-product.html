Theorem 9.1.1.

Suppose \(p(x)=F(x)S(x)\) where the 'first' function \(F = F(x)\) and the 'second' function \(S = S(x)\) are both differentiable. Then

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] p'(x) \amp= F(x)S'(x)+S(x)F'(x)\\ \frac{dp}{dx}\amp= F(x)\cdot \frac{dS}{dx} + S(x) \cdot \frac{dF}{dx} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

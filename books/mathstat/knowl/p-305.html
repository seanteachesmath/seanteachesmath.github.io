The function used in Example 4.1.6 and Example 4.1.6 was an exponential distribution with \(\lambda = \sfrac{1}{\theta} = 3\text{.}\) Occasionally the exponential is parameterized by \(\lambda \gt 0\) with

\begin{equation*} f(x; \lambda) = \begin{cases}\lambda e^{-\lambda x}, \amp \quad \text{ for } 0 \lt x\\0, \amp \quad \text{elsewhere}\end{cases} \end{equation*}

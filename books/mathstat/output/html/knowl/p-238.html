Once the moment-generating function has been calculated, the main challenge probably lies in the expansion of the Maclaurin series. Recall that that the Maclaurin series for \(f(t)\) is in general given by

\begin{equation*} f(t) = \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}\dfrac{f^{(k)}(0)(t)^k}{k!}\text{.} \end{equation*}

One alternative to this requires calculating the integrals that define the necessary expectations, for example

\begin{equation*} \mu_r' = E[X^r] = \int_{0}^\infty x^r e^{-x}\,dx \end{equation*}

by integration by parts or tabular integration.

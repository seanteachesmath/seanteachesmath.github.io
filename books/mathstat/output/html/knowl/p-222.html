We could explicitly calculate

\begin{equation*} \int_{\mu-2\sigma}^{\mu+2\sigma} f(x)\,dx\text{,} \end{equation*}

specifically

\begin{equation*} \int_{-1}^{3} f(x)\,dx \end{equation*}

for this particular random variable. Alternatively (keeping in mind that integration might be messy) we could invoke Chebyshev's Theorem (below) and give a lower bound on the probability - that the desired probability is at least \(\frac{3}{4}\text{.}\)

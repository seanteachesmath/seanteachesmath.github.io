A random variable \(X\) has a Weibull distribution if and only if its probability density is given by

\begin{equation*} f(x; \alpha, \beta) = \begin{cases}kx^{\beta-1}e^{-\alpha x^\beta}, \amp \quad \text{ for }0 \lt x\\0, \amp \quad \text{elsewhere}\end{cases} \end{equation*}

for \(\alpha, \beta \gt 0\text{.}\) Express \(k\) in terms of \(\alpha, \beta\text{.}\)

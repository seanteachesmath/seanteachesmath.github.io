We start with the definition of variance and manipulate the integral rather creatively. First note that,

\begin{equation*} \sigma^2 = \int_{-\infty}^\infty (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx\text{.} \end{equation*}

Now, we are interested in the interval \(\mu-k\sigma \lt x \lt \mu + k\sigma\text{,}\) and rewrite the integral accordingly,

\begin{equation*} \sigma^2 = \int_{-\infty}^{\mu-k\sigma} (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^{\mu+k\sigma} (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^\infty (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx \end{equation*}

The integrand of the middle integral is nonnegative, so

\begin{equation*} \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^{\mu+k\sigma} (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx \ge 0, \end{equation*}

this means

\begin{equation*} \sigma^2 \ge \int_{-\infty}^{\mu-k\sigma} (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^\infty (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx \end{equation*}

since at most we have dropped a nonzero term from the right-hand side.

Now things get a bit more tricky - we are considering the intervals \((-\infty, \mu-k\sigma]\) and \([\mu+k\sigma, \infty)\text{.}\) This means either \(x \le \mu - k\sigma\) or \(x\ge \mu + k\sigma\text{.}\) Alternatively \(x - \mu \le - k\sigma\) or \(x - \mu \ge k\sigma\text{.}\) Together these give \((x - \mu)^2 \ge (k\sigma)^2\text{.}\) With this

\begin{align*} \sigma^2 \amp\ge \int_{-\infty}^{\mu-k\sigma} (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^\infty (x-\mu)^2f(x)\,dx\\ \amp\ge \int_{-\infty}^{\mu-k\sigma} (k\sigma)^2f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^\infty (k\sigma)^2f(x)\,dx\\ 1 \amp\ge k^2\left(\int_{-\infty}^{\mu-k\sigma} f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^\infty f(x)\,dx\right) \end{align*}

Provided that \(\sigma\neq0\text{,}\) we can divide a common factor of \(\sigma^2\) from both sides to reach the last line in the calculation above. Now, at this point

\begin{equation*} 1 \ge k^2\left(\int_{-\infty}^{\mu-k\sigma} f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^\infty f(x)\,dx\right) \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{1}{k^2} \ge \left(\int_{-\infty}^{\mu-k\sigma} f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^\infty f(x)\,dx\right)\text{.} \end{equation*}

The integrals give \(\operatorname{P}(|x-\mu| \ge k\sigma)\) or \(1 - \operatorname{P}(|x-\mu| \lt k\sigma)\text{.}\)

So,

\begin{gather*} \dfrac{1}{k^2} \ge \left(\int_{-\infty}^{\mu-k\sigma} f(x)\,dx + \int_{\mu+k\sigma}^\infty f(x)\,dx\right)\\ \dfrac{1}{k^2} \ge 1 - \operatorname{P}(|x-\mu| \lt k\sigma)\\ \operatorname{P}(|x-\mu| \lt k\sigma) \ge 1 - \dfrac{1}{k^2} \end{gather*}

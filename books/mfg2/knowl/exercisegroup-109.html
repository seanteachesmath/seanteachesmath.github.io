Exercise Group 2.5.6.53–60.

In Problems 53–60, find the domain and range of each transformation of the given function.

53.

\(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x^2} \)

  1. \(y=f(x-2) \)

  2. \(y=f(x)-2 \)

  3. \(y=f(x-3)-5 \)

Answer
54.

\(f(x)=\sqrt{x} \)

  1. \(y=-f(x) \)

  2. \(y=4+f(x) \)

  3. \(y=4-f(x) \)

55.

\(f(x)=x^2 \)

  1. \(y=-2f(x) \)

  2. \(y=6-2f(x) \)

  3. \(y=6-2f(x+3) \)

Answer
56.

\(f(x)=\dfrac{1}{x} \)

  1. \(y=3f(x) \)

  2. \(y=3+f(x-1) \)

  3. \(y=3-f(x-1) \)

57.

The domain of \(f\) is \([0, 10]\) and the range is \([-2, 2]\text{.}\)

  1. \(y=f(x-3) \)

  2. \(y=3f(x) \)

  3. \(y=2f(x-5) \)

Answer
58.

The domain of \(f\) is \([-4, 4]\) and the range is \([3, 10]\text{.}\)

  1. \(y=f(x)+10 \)

  2. \(y=f(x+10) \)

  3. \(y=f(x-1)+4 \)

59.

The domain of \(f\) is \((0,+\infty)\) and the range is \((0, 1)\text{.}\)

  1. \(y=5f(x) \)

  2. \(y=3f(x+2) \)

  3. \(y=2f(x-3)+2 \)

Answer
60.

The domain of \(f\) is \((-1, 1)\) and the range is \((-\infty, 0)\text{.}\)

  1. \(y=f(x+1) \)

  2. \(y=3-f(x+1) \)

  3. \(y=4+2f(x-1) \)

Because the variable we want is squared, we use extraction of roots. First, we multiply both sides by \(3\) to clear the fraction.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \alert{3}V \amp = \alert{3}(\frac{1}{3} \pi r^2h)\\ 3V \amp = \pi r^2h \amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by } \pi h.}\\ \frac{3V}{\pi h}\amp= r^2 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Take square roots.}}\\ \pm\sqrt{\dfrac{3V}{\pi h}}\amp= r \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Because the radius of a cone must be a positive number, we use only the positive square root: \(r = \sqrt{\dfrac{3V}{\pi h}}\text{.}\)

Exercise 42.

When a ship moves through the water, it creates waves that impede its own progress. Because of this resistance, there is an upper limit to the speed at which a ship can travel, given, in knots, by

\begin{equation*} v_{\text{max}}=1.3\sqrt{L} \end{equation*}

where \(L\) is the length of the vessel, in feet. (Source: Gilner, 1972)

  1. Graph maximum speed as a function of vessel length.

  2. The world's largest ship, the oil tanker Jahre Viking, is \(1054\) feet long. What is its top speed?

  3. As a ship approaches its maximum speed, the power required increases sharply. Therefore, most merchant ships are designed to cruise at speeds no higher than \(v_c = 0.8\sqrt{L}\text{.}\) Graph \(v_c\) on the same axes with \(v_\text{max}\text{.}\)

  4. What is the cruising speed of the Jahre Viking? What percent of its maximum speed is that?

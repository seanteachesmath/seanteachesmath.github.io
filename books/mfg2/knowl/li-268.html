Item b.

The table below gives the cost of sending printed material by first-class mail in 2016.

Weight in ounces \((w)\) Postage \((P)\)
\(0 \lt w \le 1 \) $0.47
\(1 \lt w \le 2 \) $0.68
\(2 \lt w \le 3 \) $0.89
\(3 \lt w \le 4 \) $1.10
\(4 \lt w \le 5 \) $1.31
\(5 \lt w \le 6 \) $1.52
\(6 \lt w \le 7 \) $1.73

If we know the weight of the article being shipped, we can determine the required postage from the table. For instance, a catalog weighing 4.5 ounces would require $1.31 in postage. In this example, \(w\) is the input variable and \(p\) is the output variable. We say that \(p\) is a function of \(w\text{.}\)

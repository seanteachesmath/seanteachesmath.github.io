Exercise 46.

Consider the graph of \(y = \dfrac{ax}{x + k}\text{,}\) where \(a\) and \(k\) are positive constants.

  1. What is the horizontal asymptote of the graph?

  2. Show that for \(x = k\text{,}\) \(y = \dfrac{a}{2}\text{.}\)

  3. Sketch the graph of \(y = \dfrac{ax}{x + k}\)for \(a=4\) and \(k=10\) in the window

    \begin{align*} {\text{Xmin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp {\text{Xmax}} = 60\\ {\text{Ymin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp {\text{Ymax}} = 5 \end{align*}

    Illustrate your answers to parts (a) and (b) on the graph.

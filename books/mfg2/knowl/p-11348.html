  1. The formula begins with \(x^3\) and ends with \(y^3\text{.}\) As you proceed from term to term, the exponents on \(x\) decrease while the exponents on \(y\) increase, and on each term the sum of the exponents is \(3\text{.}\) The coefficients of the two middle terms are both \(3\text{.}\)

  2. The formula is the same as for \((x - y)^3\text{,}\) except that the terms alternate in sign.

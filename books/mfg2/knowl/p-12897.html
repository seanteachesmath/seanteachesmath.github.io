Mikala sells \(\dfrac{320}{x} \) bottles of bath oil per week if she charges \(x\) dollars per bottle. Her supplier can manufacture \(\dfrac{1}{2}x+6\) bottles per week if she sells it at \(x\) dollars per bottle.

  1. Graph the demand function, \(D(x) = \dfrac{320}{x}\text{,}\) and the supply function, \(S(x) = \dfrac{1}{2}x+6\text{,}\) in the same window.

  2. Write and solve an equation to find the equilibrium price, that is, the price at which the supply equals the demand for bath oil. Label this point on your graph.

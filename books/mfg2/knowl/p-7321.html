Do not confuse the expression \(\dfrac{\log_{}(7)}{\log_{}(5)}\) with \(\log_{}{\left(\dfrac{7}{5}\right)}\text{;}\) they are not the same! Property (2) allows us to simplify \(\log{\left(\dfrac{x}{y}\right)}\text{,}\) but not \(\dfrac{\log_{} (x)}{\log_{} (y)}\text{.}\) We cannot rewrite \(\dfrac{\log_{}(7)}{\log_{}(5)}\text{,}\) so we must evaluate it as \((\log_{} (7))/(\log_{} (5))\text{.}\) You can check on your calculator that

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{\log_{}(7)}{\log_{}(5)}\ne \log_{}{\left(\dfrac{7}{5}\right)}= \log_{}(1.4)\text{.} \end{equation*}

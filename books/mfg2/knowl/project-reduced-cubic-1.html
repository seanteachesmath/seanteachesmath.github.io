Checkpoint 7.7.1. Solving cubics: Part I.

In this project, we solve cubic equations of the form

\begin{equation*} x^3 + mx = n \end{equation*}

Note that there is no quadratic term. This special form was first solved by the Italian mathematicians Scipione del Ferro and Niccol√≤ Fontana Tartaglia early in the sixteenth century. Tartaglia revealed the secret to solving the special cubic equation in a poem. He first found values \(u\) and \(v\) to satisfy the system

\begin{gather*} u-v=n\\ uv=\left(\frac{m}{3}\right)^3 \end{gather*}

  1. We will use Tartaglia's method to solve

    \begin{equation*} x^3 + 6x = 7 \end{equation*}

    What are the values of \(m\) and \(n\text{?}\)

  2. Substitute the values of \(m\) and \(n\) into Tartaglia's system, then use substitution to solve for \(u\) and \(v\text{.}\) You should find two possible solutions.

  3. For each solution of the system, compute \(x=\sqrt[3]{u}- \sqrt[3]{v} \text{.}\) You should get the same value of \(x\) for each \((u, v)\text{.}\)

  4. Check that your value for \(x\) is a solution of \(x^3 + 6x = 7\text{.}\)

Item a.
To find the half-life, we calculate the time elapsed when only half the original amount, or \(100\) mg, is left.
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] 200(0.73)^t \amp = 100\amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 200.}} \\ 0.73^t \amp = 0.5\amp\amp \blert{\text{Take the log of both sides.}} \\ t\log_{} (0.73) \amp = \log_{} (0.5)\amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by log 0.73.}} \\ t \amp= \frac{\log_{} (0.5)}{\log_{} (0.73)} \\ \amp= 2.2 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
The half-life is \(2.2\) hours.

  1. We use logarithms to help us solve exponential equations.

  2. The base \(b\) logarithm of \(x\), written \(\log_{b} (x)\text{,}\) is the exponent to which \(b\) must be raised in order to yield \(x\text{.}\)

  3. If \(b\gt 0\) and \(x \gt 0\text{,}\)

    \begin{equation*} y=\log_{b} (x) ~~~ \text{ if and only if } ~~~x=b^y \end{equation*}

  4. The graphs of exponential functions can be transformed by shifts, stretches, and reflections.

  5. The operation of taking a base \(b\) logarithm is the inverse operation for raising the base \(b\) to a power.

  6. Base \(10\) logarithms are called common logarithms, and \(\log_{} (x)\) means \(\log_{10} (x)\text{.}\)

  7. Steps for Solving Base 10 Exponential Equations.

    1. Isolate the power on one side of the equation.

    2. Rewrite the equation in logarithmic form.

    3. Use a calculator, if necessary, to evaluate the logarithm.

    4. Solve for the variable.

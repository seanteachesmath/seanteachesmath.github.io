Your answer to part (a) is actually Tartaglia's special cubic in disguise. Substitute \(x = a - b\text{,}\) \(m = 3ab\text{,}\) and \(n = a^3 - b^3\) to see this. Therefore, if we can find numbers \(a\) and \(b\) that satisfy

\begin{align*} 3ab \amp = m\\ a^3 - b^3 \amp = n \end{align*}

then the solution to Tartaglia's cubic is \(x = a - b\text{.}\)

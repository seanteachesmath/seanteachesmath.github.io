In the preceding examples, exponential growth was modeled by increasing functions of the form

\begin{equation*} P(t) = P_0 b^t \end{equation*}

where \(b \gt 1\text{.}\) The function \(P(t) = P_0b^t\) is a decreasing function if \(0 \lt b \lt 1\text{.}\) In this case, we say that the function describes exponential decay, and the constant \(b\) is called the decay factor.

Exercise 41.

If you walk in the normal way, your maximum speed, \(v\text{,}\) in meters per second, is limited by the length of your legs, \(r\text{,}\) according to the formula

\begin{equation*} v=\sqrt{gr} \end{equation*}

where the constant \(g\) is approximately \(10\) meters per second squared. (Source: Alexander, 1992)

  1. A typical adult man has legs about \(0.9\) meter long. How fast can he walk?

  2. A typical four-year-old has legs \(0.5\) meter long. How fast can she walk?

  3. Graph maximum walking speed as a function of leg length.

  4. Race-walkers can walk as fast as \(4.4\) meters per second by rotating their hips so that the effective length of their legs is increased. What is that effective length?

  5. On the Moon the value of \(g\) is \(1.6\) meters per second squared. How fast can a typical adult man walk on the Moon?

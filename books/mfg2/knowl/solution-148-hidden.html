1. We apply the zero-factor principle to the product \((x - 6)(x + 2)\text{.}\)

    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] (x - 6)(x+ 2) \amp = 0 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Set each factor equal to zero.}}\\ x - 6 = 0~~ \amp\text{or} ~~ x+2=0 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Solve each equation.}}\\ ~~~x=6~~ \amp\text{or} ~~ x=-2 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

    There are two solutions, \(6\) and \(-2\text{.}\) (You should check that both of these values satisfy the original equation.)

  2. To find the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph, we set \(y = 0\) and solve the equation

    \begin{equation*} 0 = x^2 - 4x - 12 \end{equation*}

    But this is the equation we solved in part (a), because \((x - 6)(x + 2) = x^2 - 4x - 12\text{.}\) The solutions of that equation were \(6\) and \(-2\text{,}\) so the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph are \(6\) and \(-2\text{.}\) You can see this by graphing the equation on your calculator, as shown below.

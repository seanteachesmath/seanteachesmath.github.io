The Discriminant.

The discriminant of a quadratic equation is \(D = b^2 - 4ac\text{.}\)

  1. If \(D \gt 0\text{,}\) there are two unequal real solutions.
  2. If \(D = 0\text{,}\) there is one real solution of multiplicity two.
  3. If \(D \lt 0\text{,}\) there are two complex solutions.

Exercise Group 7.6.2.63–66.

For Problems 63–66,

  1. Use polynomial division to write the fraction in the form \(y=\dfrac{k}{p(x)}+c \text{,}\) where \(k\) and \(c\) are constants.

  2. Use transformations to sketch the graph.

63.

\(y=\dfrac{3x+4}{x+3} \)

Answer
64.

\(y=\dfrac{5x+1}{x-2} \)

65.

\(y=\dfrac{x^2+2x+3}{(x+1)^2} \)

Answer
66.

\(y=\dfrac{x^2-4x+3}{(x-2)^2} \)

We set \(y\) equal to zero to find the \(x\)-intercepts.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] 0 \amp= x^2 + 3x + 1\amp\amp \blert{\text{Use the quadratic formula.}}\\ x \amp=\frac{-3\pm\sqrt{3^2-4(1)(1)}}{2(1)}\\ \amp=\frac{-3\pm\sqrt{5}}{2} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Rounding to the nearest tenth, we find that the \(x\)-intercepts are approximately \((-2.6, 0)\) and \((-0.4, 0)\text{.}\)

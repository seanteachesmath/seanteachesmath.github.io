Example 2.1.3. Calculating Numbers of Triangles.

With a bit of careful guessing, it's possible to explore formulas that support these patterns and that allow us to predict what happens as the number of steps increases (beyond our drawing ability).

How can we relate the numbers of each type of triangle and the total to the step number? Looking at the white triangle numbers, you might notice that the number increases by a factor of three each step. In fact, if we let \(W_{k}\) be the number of white triangles on step \(k\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} W_{k} =3^{k} \end{equation*}

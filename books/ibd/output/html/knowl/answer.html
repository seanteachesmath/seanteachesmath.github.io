Imposing structure reduces the growth rate (relative to \(r\)) in growing populations. Imposing structure slows the decay rate (relative to \(r\)) in shrinking populations. In this parameterization,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} \lambda_\pm &=\frac{\frac{2}{3}\pm\sqrt{\frac{4}{9}+4\left(\frac{16}{9}\right)\left(\frac{1}{2}\right) }}{2}\\ &=\frac{1}{3}\pm\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{\frac{4 }{9}+\frac{32}{9}}\\ &=\frac{1}{3}\pm 1\\ \lambda_\pm&=\frac{4}{3}, -\frac{2}{3}. \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \left( \begin{array}{cc} 0 &\frac{16}{9}\\[2pt] \frac{1}{2} &\frac{2}{3} \end{array}\right) \left( \begin{array}{c} x_1\\ x_2 \end{array} \right)= \frac{4}{3}\left( \begin{array}{c} x_1\\ x_2 \end{array} \right). \end{equation*}

Notice that \(ELR>\lambda_+>1\text{,}\) so that the population grows.

The eigenvector associated with \(\lambda=\frac{4}{3}\) is of the form

\begin{equation*} v_1=\left( \begin{array}{c}x_1\\ \frac{3}{4}x_1\end{array}\right). \end{equation*}

The eigenvector associated with \(\lambda=-\frac{2}{3}\) is of the form

\begin{equation*} v_1=\left( \begin{array}{c}x_1\\ -\frac{3}{8}x_1\end{array}\right). \end{equation*}

Since the law of mass action relies on concentrations of reactants, we must reconsider our receptors and nutrients in terms of ‘concentrations’. For a given cellular component \(Z\text{,}\) the ‘volumetric concentration of \(Z\)’ is,

\begin{equation*} \left[\text{concentration}\atop{\text{of }Z}\right]=\left[\text{average number}\atop{\text{of}~Z~\text{per cell}}\right]\times\left[\text{average number of}\atop\text{cells per volume}\right]. \end{equation*}

Treating \(N\text{,}\) \(X_0\text{,}\) \(X_1\text{,}\) and \(C\) as volumetric concentrations we can use the reaction diagram (Figure 4.1.1) to build differential equations to describe the changes in concentrations.

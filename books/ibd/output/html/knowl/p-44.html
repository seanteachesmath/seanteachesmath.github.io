To make use of the diagram in Fig. [cross-reference to target(s) "fig::bdp" missing] we should let

\begin{equation*} P_n(t)=\text{Prob}(\text{counting \(n\) animals at time \(t\)}), \end{equation*}

and consider how these probabilities evolve in time for all \(n\text{.}\) One important fact is that

\begin{equation*} P_i(0)=\begin{cases}1, \,\text{if \(i=n\)}\\0, \,\text{otherwise}\end{cases}, \end{equation*}

since we know that we start from \(N_0=n\) animals with absolute certainty. We can derive the differential equation by conservation,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} {2} P_n(t+\Delta t) & =\text{Prob(a birth)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n-1\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\\ & +\text{Prob(a death)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n+1\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\\ & +\text{Prob(no change)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\end{aligned} \end{equation*}

First we will apply the definition of \(P_n(t)\) and the others (i.e., \(P_{n-1}(t)\)). Notice that the equation is presented in two columns, on the left is the format ‘in words’ and on the right introduces the corresponding notation,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} {2} P_n(t+\Delta t) & =\text{Prob(a birth)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n-1\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\qquad=\text{Prob(a birth)}\times P_{n-1}(t) \\ & +\text{Prob(a death)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n+1\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\qquad+\text{Prob(a death)}\times P_{n+1}(t)\\ & +\text{Prob(no change)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\qquad+\text{Prob(no change)}\times P_{n}(t) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Below we could also consider \(\text{Prob(no change)}\) as \((1-\text{Prob(change)})\text{,}\) where \(\text{Prob(change)}\) is given by the probability of events resulting in exit from size \(n\text{.}\) The event probabilities are,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} {2} P_n(t+\Delta t) & =\text{Prob(a birth)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n-1\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\qquad=(n-1)r\Delta t P_{n-1}(t) \\ & +\text{Prob(a death)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n+1\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\qquad+(n+1)\mu\Delta t P_{n+1}(t)\\ & +\text{Prob(no change)}\times\text{Prob}(\text{\(n\) animals at time \(t\)}) &&\qquad+(1-nr\Delta t-n\mu\Delta t) P_{n}(t) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Finally, we can begin rearranging to derive the differential equation for \(P_n(t)\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} P_n(t+\Delta t) &=(n-1)r\Delta t P_{n-1}(t) +(n+1)\mu\Delta t P_{n+1}(t)+(1-nr\Delta t-n\mu\Delta t) P_{n}(t) \\ P_n(t+\Delta t) -P_n(t)&=(n-1)r\Delta t P_{n-1}(t) +(n+1)\mu\Delta t P_{n+1}(t)-(nr\Delta t-n\mu\Delta t) P_{n}(t) \\ \frac{P_n(t+\Delta t) -P_n(t)}{\Delta t}&=(n-1)r P_{n-1}(t) +(n+1)\mu P_{n+1}(t)-n(r -\mu) P_{n}(t) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Now, taking the limit \(\Delta t\rightarrow0\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} \frac{d^{} {P_n(t)}}{d {t}^{}}=(n-1)r P_{n-1}(t) +(n+1)\mu P_{n+1}(t)-(nr-n\mu) P_{n}(t) . \end{equation*}

We have to assume that \(P_i(t)=0\) for any \(i<0\text{,}\) and accept a system of coupled equations for \(n=0, 1, 2, \dots\text{.}\) We will study this particular model with a suite of techniques, including naive calculation, inspection, and somewhat elegant probability theory.

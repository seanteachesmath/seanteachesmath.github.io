Before developing our ‘method’ for solving all future problems, we will actually solve this one analytically. This is one of the few times we can solve our model analytically. As we do this, we will not pay much attention to whether or not \(r\) is positive or negative. In general, we expect parameters to be non-negative, but here \(r=b-m\) is a sort of ‘compound’ parameter and can be negative when \(m\) (a positive parameter) is larger than \(b\) (a positive parameter). To allow for growth or decay, without having to worry about negative parameters, we will specify \(r=b-m\text{.}\)

Subsection 1.4.3 Student Perspective

Day four began by noting three things.

  1. Most students were able to successfully create and upload an example LaTeX document to GitHub from day three.

  2. The first LaTeX writing assignment will be uploaded for students to begin working on by the end of the day. Its instructions will be self contained and require about one page of work by students.

  3. Each student will be required to type and submit a summary of one of the lectures for this course. Students will volunteer for a lecture that has not already been summarized by another student, beginning with the day three lecture. More details can be found in the math2753_summaries.220.pdf file on the new, class Google Drive link.

The day continued by briefly reviewing topics from day three and introducing new LaTeX features and concepts. The three most important were the align, itemize, and enumerate environments.

  1. Create a table of contents (TOC) with the \tableofcontents command.

  2. Create sections and subsections with the \section{} and \subsubsection{} commands.

  3. Two dollar signs $ ... $ may be used in place of the \( ... \) command and two double-dollar signs $$ ... $$ may be used in place of the \[ ... \] command. However, the instructor does not prefer it. More importantly, the developers recommend against it!

  4. During compiling errors, individual lines or blocks of code may be commented out to help pinpoint and troubleshoot the errors.

  5. To render a left-apostrophe or quotation mark, the apostrophe character that is usually found one the same key as the tilde symbol must be used.

  6. To render characters that already have assigned usage in the LaTeX typesetting language, the characters must be prefixed with a backward slash. For example, to display the ampersand character we would type \&.

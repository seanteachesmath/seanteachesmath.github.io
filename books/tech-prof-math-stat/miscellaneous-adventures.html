Subsection 5.1 Programming in R: difference equations (11/17)

Subsubsection 5.1.1 Preview

In the past we have studied two famous difference equations, or recurrence relations:

  1. Newton’s method of rootfinding by successive tangent line approximation,

    \begin{equation*} x_{n+1} = x_n - \dfrac{f(x_n)}{f'(x_{n})} \end{equation*}

  2. Generating terms of the Fibonacci sequence for \(n\geq2\) given \(x_0=x_1=1\text{,}\)

    \begin{equation*} x_n = x_{n-1} + x_{n-2} \end{equation*}

Today we will look at computing and graphing solutions to more general difference equations of the form

\begin{equation*} x_{n+1} = f(x_n) \end{equation*}

for certain important functions \(f(x)\text{.}\)

Subsubsection 5.1.2 Review

We had a very productive day programming the recurrence relation computationally and graphically. We experimented with the program to generate some exciting (mathematically ‘chaotic’) results.

Dr. Laverty referred to it as Hackathon DAGGER This is where a group of students get together, come up with a cool mathematical idea, and write a program that executes that idea.

Also known as discrete-time dynamical systems or recurrence relations, difference equations generically refer to the application of some mathematical function to generate terms in a sequence of values. One last, and famous, example of this family of problems is the discrete-logistic map or the discrete-logistic equation

\begin{equation*} x_{n+1} = rx_{n}(1-x_{n}) \end{equation*}

for \(0\lt x_{0}\lt 1\) and \(0 \lt r \lt 4\text{.}\)

A famous method in all of mathematics is Newton's Method. The idea is simple: when trying to solve complicated or otherwise analytically intractable equations of the form \(f(x) = 0\) for \(x\text{,}\) we often approximate the function by its tangent line and solve \(f_\text{tan}(x) = 0\) for \(x\) instead. Repeated application of this procedure generates a sequence of values that most often converges to the true solution of the original problem.

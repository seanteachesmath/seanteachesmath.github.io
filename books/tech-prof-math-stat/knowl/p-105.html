Today in class we revisited graphing. We approximated \(f(x)\) with the tangent of \(f(x)\text{.}\) We used Newton’s Method to show this, making guesses for \(x\text{,}\) first starting with 1. Using smaller “xlim” (limits for the x-axis) gives us a closer look at how close these tangent lines are to the actual slope. After we run our original plot with our guess at \(x=1\text{,}\) we can run the “ftan” (tangent line) and include “add=T” (add true) to add the tangent line to the previous graph.

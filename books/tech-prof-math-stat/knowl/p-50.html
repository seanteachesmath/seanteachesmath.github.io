\newcommand is a way to prevent long and similar code from repeating in your LaTeX file. \newcommand is composed of three main parts \newcommand{command call name}{# of arguments}{function you are passing the argument(s) through} Note: You can have more than one function to pass your argument(s) through. When you call a new command it will be: the \call_name{argument 1}...{arguments n} where n is your total amounnt of arguments. The major takeaway from introducing \newcommand is that it allows your LaTeX code to be concise, easier to read, and faster to write.

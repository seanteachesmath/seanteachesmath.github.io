We introduced the comment symbols INDEX comment symbols , Mathematica , (* ...) for Mathematica which is a ‘block comment’ style as opposed to the ‘line comment’ style in LaTeX (with %) or in R (with #). And the notation for defining a function, paying special attention to the underscore on the left-hand side. There are a few ways of entering functions distinguished by the format of the equals sign (delayed evaluation with a ‘colon equals’ (... := ...) or immediate evaluation with just an ‘equals’ (... = ...)). In most cases there will not be a noticeable difference between the two, but there are subtle differences. There is a hint in the documentation to lean towards the delayed evaluation, but I have not found this to be a major problem in my own use. This might be slightly easier on the computer if you are defining custom functions.

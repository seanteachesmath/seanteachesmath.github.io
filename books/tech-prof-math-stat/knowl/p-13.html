Today in class we learned more about formatting in LaTeX. LaTeX is used in all sorts of things I’ve never even realized - it makes sense now why nearly all my Math tests and Textbooks look the same, even if they are for different classes and different subjects. Class began with us looking at a typical Math test, and us learning how to format things similarly to the test. We learned the little things I often forget - how to add space, how to make information jump down to a new line (using ), and how to make bulleted and numbered lists. Errors often pop in LaTeX, so it’s important to always check and double check that you spell , spell check all your commands correctly and that you don’t forget a at the beginning of the line. We also learned that if you add different coding up in the preamble, it can affect your whole document. For example, we added code to the top of our document entitled usepackageamsmath, which allowed us to later in our document use code for aligning text.

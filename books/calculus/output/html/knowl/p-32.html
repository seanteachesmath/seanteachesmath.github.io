We are interested in determining \(\displaystyle \dfrac{d}{dx}\Big(\,\,?\,\,\Big) = f(x)\text{.}\)

  • antiderivatives.

    We call the function \(F(x)\) an antiderivative, and write \(\displaystyle F(x) = \int f(x)\,dx\text{.}\)

  • indefinite integral.

    The collection of all possible antiderivatives is called the indefinite integral \(\displaystyle \int f(x)\,dx = F(x) + c\text{.}\)

  • power rule.

    For \(n\neq1\text{,}\) \(\displaystyle \int x^n\,dx = \dfrac{x^{n+1}}{n+1}+c\) (add one to the power, then divide by the new power).
